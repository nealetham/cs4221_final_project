Do You Need A Waterproof Case For Your iPhone 12?

Another year, another new collection of iPhones. And another year of wondering whether Apple’s water resistance claims for the new iPhones mean you won’t need a waterproof case. As it did last year with the iPhone 11, Apple is prominently featuring the water resistance of the new iPhone 12 series, with photos of the smartphones being splashed. However, as always, water resistant and waterproof are not the same thing. And even water resistance claims are coming under scrutiny after Apple was fined by an Italian court — more on that shortly.

Here’s What Apple Says About iPhone 12 Series Water Resistance

Apple has posted the details about what its’ claim of “Even better spill and splash resistance” means.

According to Apple:

“iPhone 12 and iPhone 12 mini are splash, water, and dust resistant and were tested under controlled laboratory conditions with a rating of IP68 under IEC standard 60529 (maximum depth of 6 meters up to 30 minutes). Splash, water, and dust resistance are not permanent conditions and resistance might decrease as a result of normal wear. Do not attempt to charge a wet iPhone; refer to the user guide for cleaning and drying instructions. Liquid damage not covered under warranty.”

The exact same claim is made for the iPhone 12 Pro models.

While this would appear to indicate you could safely grab your iPhone 12, dive into a pool and use its camera to snap some great underwater photos, you might want to rethink that strategy. Especially telling in that legalese are the terms “tested under controlled laboratory conditions“ and “liquid damage not covered under warranty.”

Apple Fined by Italian Court Over iPhone Water Resistance Claims

This topic was in the news recently after an Italian court fined Apple €10m (about $12 million) for what it called misleading claims about the iPhone’s water resistance. The court ruled that Apple misled customers by conducting tests only in laboratory conditions with pure water instead of real-life scenarios. The court also found the company was “aggressive” in charging for water damage in iPhones that were under warranty.

In other words, if Apple decides your iPhone 12 was damaged by water exposure, be prepared to pay anywhere from $399 (iPhone 12 Mini) to $599 (iPhone 12 Pro Max) for repairs...

Do You Need a Waterproof Case for Your iPhone 12?

For daily use, when you might be caught in a rain shower, the iPhone 12 series’ IP68 water resistance rating should mean the smartphone is fine. I would still used a protective case to safeguard against bumps and falls — most will help a bit with water resistance as well by covering buttons.

However, if you plan to use your iPhone 12 for underwater photography, or you’re participating in an activity where it might get submerged (like boating), I would definitely be looking for a waterproof case.
