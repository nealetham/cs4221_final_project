    How's this for a pub crawl - here are the 10 best inns and alehouses that Derbyshire has to offer.

    And if you're still in the mood for more, we've also got the best pubs in Nottinghamshire and Leicestershire listed too.

    But how do we know they're the best places for a perfect pint? Because they've been hand picked by beer experts.

    Each year, local branches of Camra - the Campaign for Real Ale - up and down the country vote for their pub of the year.

    And we can reveal all the winners across the East Midlands for 2018 - ranging from community run pubs to micropubs to street-corner locals.

    Of course, it's not just the beer which lands a pub the votes - atmosphere, decor, welcome, service, value for money and customer mix are also considered alongside the quality of their real ale.

    Aside from serving beer, many pubs brew their own, host beer festivals, music and community events and boast fantastic interior designs.

    Here's the full selections from 2018 - and what Camra has to say about each one.

    Derbyshire

    Derby Camra

    • Smithfield Alehouse, Derby

    A handsome riverside pub with a central bar serving the main bar and separate quiet back bar. There is a separate snug room with a pool table and darts. It is a popular match day pub when Derby County are at home. On a fine day, the large outside terrace overlooking the river is very pleasant. This pub serves seven changing beers.

    Amber Valley Camra

    • White Hart, Bargate

    For the second year running, Sean and his team have taken the Amber Valley Camra Pub of the Year crown - congratulations to a fantastic pub. The White Hart was commended for its choice of well kept beers, friendly and knowledgeable staff, complemented by a clean and stylish decor.

    Ashbourne sub branch of Derby Camra

    • Smith's Tavern, Ashbourne

    Small, highly traditional town-centre pub, with as many as seven real ales on. Marston's portfolio of beers, from which the landlord selects the widest possible choice and is also allowed one free choice guest ale, served at weekends, always from a local or rare brewery. The pub also has a large selection of malt whiskies and holds three to four tutored tastings per year.

    Burton and South Derbyshire

    • The Colvile Arms, Lullington

    A popular free-house at the heart of an attractive hamlet at the southern tip of Derbyshire. It is leased from the Lullington Estate, the seat of the Colvile family until the early 1900s. Inside, there are high-backed settles with wood panelling. The bar and a comfortable lounge are situated on opposite sides of a central serving area. A second lounge/function room overlooks the beer garden and lawn (a former bowling green).

    Chesterfield Camra

    • The Rose and Crown, Brampton

    The Rose and Crown opened back in 2009 and, managed by Mat Randall, it is one of two pubs owned by Brampton Brewery. The pub serves a selection of Brampton beers, as well as a good range of guest ales, and it holds two beer festivals a year. The Rose and Crown has won many awards in Chesterfield Branch competitions, including being voted Summer Pub of the Season 2012, and Spring Pub of the Season 2013, 2015 and 2016.

    Dronfield and District Camra

    • Miners Arms, Hundall

    Situated in Dronfield Woodhouse, this pub serves the local community. There is a quiz every Tuesday and Thursday evening with all teams welcome and prizes for the winners. Good home-cooked food is served lunchtimes and evenings.

    Erewash Valley Camra

    • York Chambers, Long Eaton

    This pub opened December 2015 in a grade-two-listed building with the interior little changed, apart from the installation of a temperature-controlled coolroom cellar. It serves six ever-changing real ales straight from the cask. It also serves a range of ciders and quality wines. Located next to Long Eaton Green, which has regular bus routes to Derby, Nottingham and Loughborough. A sister pub to the Chequered Flag in Castle Donington.

    Mansfield and Ashfield Camra

    • The Devonshire Arms, South Normanton

    Genuine free house which offers up to five real ales and three real ciders and perries. A selection of home-cooked dishes is available every day except Sunday, when a very popular Sunday carvery is offered. Vegetarians, vegans and coeliacs are all catered for. Daily specials are available each day. Sky Sports is shown on two screens.

    Matlock and Dales Camra

    • The Thorn Tree, Matlock

    Perched high above Matlock Town, this superb traditional two-room pub enjoys beautiful views of the Derwent valley and Riber Castle from the heated patio area. Popular with workers from County Hall at lunchtimes and locals at night, children and dogs are welcome although cat-swinging is not recommended due to the compact nature of the establishment.

    Reputedly, a haunted wall clock has a transcendental approach to time in the lounge where regulars, ramblers and real ale enthusiasts convene to enjoy the atmosphere. Three permanent real ales are complemented by four ever-changing guests. Home-made food is served. Pie night on Wednesday.

    Sheffield and District Camra

    • Red Lion, Bakewell

    Historic coaching inn. This town centre pub with oak beams and open fires serves two regular beers.

    Nottinghamshire

    Nottingham Camra

    • The Station Hotel, Hucknall

    The pub has been operated by the local Lincoln Green brewery since February 2017 following a refurbishment. There is a traditional bar and a large comfortable piano lounge along with a room at the rear featuring traditional pub games. The railway station and tram stop are nearby.

    There are 20 hand pumps, all dispensing a different beer or cider. There is also an impressive selection of gins and whiskies - 50-plus of each. Missed your tram home? No worries you can stay the night in one of the hotel rooms. As one of the judges commented: “A great pub, well worth a return visit.”

    Mansfield and Ashfield Camra

    • Dandy Cock, Kirkby-in-Ashfield

    This micro pub offers up to four real ales on hand pull and up to six real ciders on tap. It has two small rooms with a cellar behind the bar which you can look into. A range of wines and gin is also available. Very dog friendly. On street parking only but there is a bus stop directly outside the front door.

    Newark Camra

    • Just Beer Micropub, Newark

    Also one of the 16 regional finalists in the running for national winner. It's a micro pub concentrating on cask ales, cider and perries. In February 2016, the milestone of 3,000 different beers from 1,000 different breweries was reached.

    World and unusual UK ales are available from the well-stocked fridge. Food includes locally sourced pork pies, cheese boards and pork scratchings. Traditional pub games are played, including an annual cribbage tournament. Three beer festivals are held each year. It has been the local Camra Pub of the Year for five years.

    North Nottinghamshire Camra

    • The Brew Shed, Retford

    A small pub with four hand-pulled beers on the bar plus a variety of other drinks, some of which are not often found in a pub of this size. There is a well-stocked fridge and a pleasing site of British bottle beers along with the continental choice. Real cider is available. Harrisons Brewery is opening behind the pub mid 2018.

    Vale of Belvoir Camra

    • Horse and Plough, Bingham

    Situated in the heart of a busy market town, this warm, friendly, one-room pub, owned by Castle Rock Brewery, is a former Methodist chapel and has a cottage-style interior and flagstone floor. Up to nine cask ales and three ciders are served. Freshly prepared food is served lunchtimes and evenings in the bar, and the first floor restaurant offers a varied seasonal menu.

    The Horse and Plough holds regular beer festivals plus live music and occasional comedy nights. Although small, the turnover of interesting and well-kept beers at this pub can't be understated.

    With easy public transport links to Nottingham, the Horse and Plough is the jewel in the crown of five pubs in Bingham serving cask ale, making a trip out to this market town on the edge of the vale of Belvoir well worth a visit.

    Leicestershire

    Leicester Camra

    • Blue Boar, Leicester

    This micro pub opened in 2016 on the ground floor of a building in a conservation area most recently used as offices. There is a single light airy room with a bar opposite as you enter. Behind the bar, through a glass partition, the cellar may be viewed. Board games and reading material may be found on shelves.

    The house beer is specially brewed by Leatherbitches Brewery and guest beers are sourced from microbreweries around the country and include many rare beers. This is probably the only pub in the city using oversize glasses to ensure customers get a full pint. The pub is named in honour of the Blue Boar Inn where Richard III stayed before the Battle of Bosworth Field.

    Hinckley and Bosworth Camra

    • Elbow Room, Hinckley

    This bar is a novel addition to Hinckley’s many pubs and it does set itself apart from others. The concept and ideas behind it fit in perfectly with the new development at The Crescent.

    The Elbow Room is something different and, what it does, it does really well. The beers, ciders, gins, cocktails etc are designed to attract a wide audience and, indeed, local people of all age groups frequent The Elbow Room. A visit to Hinckley has to include The Elbow Room.

    Loughborough and North Leicestershire Camra

    • Needle and Pin, Loughborough

    A micro pub in what was the old H and R electronics shop. The Needle and Pin is a charming venue with four changing real ales. There are also over four different ciders at any given point at a reasonable price. More than 50 continental and craft beers stocked.

    Melton Mowbray and District Camra

    • The Blue Bell, Hoby

    A picturesque busy village pub frequented by both diners and drinkers. The pub has a thatched roof and beer garden which overlooks the Wreake Valley. It is an ideal stopping-off point for those walking the Leicestershire Round.

    The pub has exposed wooden beams in the ceiling throughout and a tiled floor with rugs giving it a cosy traditional feel. There is a central bar with seating in front and to the right. To the left is a restaurant area. A skittle alley is available for hire for functions whilst the pub also hosts two dart teams.

    Rutland Camra

    • The Green Dragon, Ryhall

    With four hand pumps, the Dragon is described in the Good Beer Guide as a former Melbourns stone-built pub in the heart of the village. The main building is grade-two-listed and was built in the 17th century and gives the Green Dragon Pub a nice cosy feel, with its low ceilings and nooks and crannies.

    Superb home-cooked meals are served and, as the pub has its own pizza oven, these are a speciality of the house and served daily. The Dragon is what it a pub should be: welcoming, friendly and at the heart of the community. Well worth a visit.

    Vale of Belvoir Camra

    • Geese and Fountain, Croxton Kerrial

    The Geese and Fountain is a traditional village inn easily found on the A607, offering a wide selection of local food and drink, a quiet and welcoming atmosphere, and accommodation. There is no juke box or fruit machine, but regular live music and a monthly folk club supplement the hum of conversation. Dogs, children, cyclists and walkers are all very welcome.

    Having reopened after a long closure in 2016, the pub is listed in the 2017 Good Beer Guide, and also offers seven en suite bed-and-breakfast rooms. Breakfasts, lunches and dinners are served every day. Traditional pub games including darts and cribbage always available, and the pub's "village library" opened in early 2018. A large beer garden provides outstanding views over some fantastic countryside.
