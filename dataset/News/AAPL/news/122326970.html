What You Missed On Wall Street On Wednesday
Mid/Close Wrap
Sam Martinelli - email
Five top five lists with the news and calls moving stocks, powered by The Fly.

Get caught up quickly on the top news and calls moving stocks with these five Top Five lists.

1. STOCK NEWS:

  • Netflix (NFLX) reported Q4 global streaming paid net additions of 13.12M, well above consensus expectations [read more]
  • Texas Instruments (TXN) reported better-than-expected Q4 earnings but provided worse-than-expected Q1 guidance[read more]
  • AT&T (T) reported mixed Q4 results and provided lower-than-expected FY24 earnings guidance [read more]
  • Abbott (ABT) reported upbeat Q4 revenue and provided its outlook for FY24 earnings and organic sales[read more]
  • eBay (EBAY) will cut roughly 1,000 roles, or 9% of its full-time staff [read more]

2. WALL STREET CALLS:

  • Netflix (NFLX) was downgraded to Hold at Deutsche Bank and upgraded to Outperform at Macquarie following last night's earnings report from the streaming giant [read more]
  • New Street upgraded AMD (AMD) on AI chip spending growth[read more]
  • Biogen (BIIB) downgraded at UBS given low probability of Leqembi inflection[read more]
  • Ford (F) and Tesla (TSLA) were initiated with Sell ratings at Redburn Atlantic [read more]
  • Wells Fargo and BMO Capital downgraded DuPont (DD) [read more]

3. AROUND THE WEB:

  • Tesla (TSLA) cars in China are facing entry restrictions, Nikkei Asia reports [read more]
  • Wayfair's (W) recent employee layoffs focused on employees working remotely, WSJ reports[read more]
  • Apple (AAPL) is planning to add new fees and restrictions after allowing app downloads outside the App Store in the EU, WSJ says [read more]
  • Walgreens Boots Alliance (WBA) is considering options for its Shields Health Solutions, including a potential sale, Bloomberg says [read more]
  • DraftKings (DKNG) is near a marketing partnership deal with Barstool, Sportico reports [read more]

4. MOVERS:

  • Kura Oncology (KURA) increases after announcing it agreed to sell 1.38M shares [read more]
  • Heron Therapeutics (HRTX) gains after announcing the FDA approved its supplemental New Drug Application for ZYNRELEF [read more]
  • New Oriental Education (EDU) higher in New York after reporting quarterly results [read more]
  • BlackBerry (BB) falls in New York after announcing a $160M private share offering[read more]
  • B2Gold (BTG) declines in New York after reporting Q4 gold production results [read more]

5. EARNINGS/GUIDANCE:

  • DuPont (DD) reported preliminary Q4 results and pointed to lower-than-expected Q1 results, citing customer destocking and weakness in China [read more]
  • General Dynamics (GD) reported Q4 results, with CEO Phebe Novakovic commenting, "We had a solid fourth quarter, capping off a year that saw growth in all four segments and continued strong cash flow" [read more]
  • Kimberly-Clark (KMB) reported Q4 results, with EPS missing consensus [read more]
  • Baker Hughes (BKR) announced Q4 results[read more]
  • Teledyne (TDY) reported Q4 results and provided guidance for FY24 and Q1[read more]

INDEXES:

The Dow fell 99.06, or 0.26%, to 37,806.39, the Nasdaq gained 55.98, or 0.36%, to 15,481.92, and the S&P 500 advanced 3.95, or 0.081%, to 4,868.55.
