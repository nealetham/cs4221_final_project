Apple's controversial Web Eraser has yet to appear in beta versions of iOS and mMacOS. Her is a new Safari feature for hiding what Apple calls distracting parts on a Web site (called Distraction Control).

Users can hide individual components on a page, and also make them visible again. In theory, this also lets you hide ads, but Apple cautions that it is not an adblocker.

For example, if an ad overlaps a button or text, you can hide it with this. For hiding ads, this only works temporarily; upon reloading, the ad is simply seen again.

When the feature was leaked earlier this year, hundreds of advertisers and publishers expressed their concerns to Apple through their advocacy group News Media Association. The publishers feared that consumers would use that iOS feature to brush off advertising. On May 29, a group of French publishing associations sent a letter to Apple CEO Tim Cook, stating that Web Eraser could threaten 100,000 jobs in France

Interestingly, Aople did not announce the feature in June at its developer conference. Therefore, it seems the concept has been toned down. Hence apparently not using the name Web Eraser either.
