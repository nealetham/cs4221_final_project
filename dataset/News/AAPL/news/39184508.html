Dow Jones Falls 100 Points Amid Surprise Jobless Data; Cisco Dives 12% On Earnings; Apple Hits All-Time High, While Tesla Jumps 6%

Licensing
  • SCOTT LEHTONEN
  • 01:05 PM ET 08/13/2020

The Dow Jones Industrial Average fell more than 120 points before cutting losses amid surprise jobless claims midday Thursday, as the stock market rally continues. Apple jumped 2.6% to hit an all-time high, while Cisco Systems dove 12% on earnings.

Blue chip leader Apple (AAPL) rallied as much as 2.6%, while Microsoft (MSFT) moved up 1% in today's stock market. Dow Jones stock Cisco Systems (CSCO) plunged 12% after delivering weak earnings guidance late Wednesday.

Stocks on the move midday Thursday include Amazon (AMZN) and Tesla (TSLA), which raced 6% higher.

Among companies reporting earnings, IPO stock Azek (AZEK) jumped 3% and is at the top of a new buy range, while NetEase (NTES) slid almost 2% despite reporting strong results early Thursday, as a new buy point looms.

Stocks in or near buy zones include IBD Leaderboard stock Adobe (ADBE) and IBD SwingTrader stock Dollar General (DG).

Dow Jones Today: Surprise Jobless Claims

The Dow Jones industrials pared its early loss to 0.1% Thursday midday, while the S&P 500 traded up 0.15%. Nasdaq composite took the midday lead with a 0.9% gain, looking to add to Wednesday's 2.1% advance. The tech-heavy Nasdaq is close to hitting another record high.

Early Thursday, the Labor Department reported new U.S. weekly jobless claims of 963,000, well ahead of economist estimates. Jobless claims fell below 1 million for the first time since March 21, as the economy continues to recover from the coronavirus pandemic.

Among exchange traded funds, Innovator IBD 50 (FFTY) advanced 1.1% Thursday midday. The Nasdaq 100-linked Invesco QQQ Trust (QQQ) ETF moved up 1%. Meanwhile, the SPDR S&P 500 ETF (SPY) rose 0.15%.

The tech-heavy Nasdaq is up 22.7% for the year through Wednesday's close. Meanwhile, the S&P 500 is up 4.6%, while the DJIA is down 2.0% year to date, through the Aug. 12 close.

Coronavirus News

The coronavirus outbreak continues to spread across the U.S. According to the Worldometer data tracker, the cumulative number of confirmed U.S. cases topped 5.3 million Wednesday. On Sunday, daily new cases measured about 47,800, the lowest number since July 5. While daily new cases remain elevated, the recent surge has abated and there hasn't been a sustained rise in the number of daily deaths, a key metric to monitor.

The cumulative total of Covid-19 cases confirmed since the start of the outbreak worldwide topped 20.8 million Wednesday, with more than 748,000 virus-related deaths.

Coronavirus Stock Market Rally

According to IBD's The Big Picture, the coronavirus stock market rally remains in an uptrend after rebounding from lows over four months ago, on March 23. The major stock indexes confirmed the rebound as a new uptrend on April 2.

Per Wednesday's The Big Picture, "The Nasdaq and top-rated growth stocks rebounded Wednesday after three days of selling. With a light distribution-day count and plenty of bullish stock charts, Wednesday's market action showed why the stock market uptrend is still very much intact."

Stocks to watch include the top stocks on IBD Long-Term Leaders, which focuses on companies with stable earnings growth and price performance.

Stock Market ETF Strategy And How To Invest In The Current Uptrend

Dow Jones News: Cisco Earnings

Dow Jones stock Cisco Systems dove 12% after reporting weak guidance late Wednesday. The company also topped earnings and sales views for the fiscal fourth quarter.

Cisco stock had been shaping a cup with handle with a 48.39 buy point, but Thursday's action sends the stock plunging through its 50- and 200-day support levels.

Stocks In Or Near Buy Zones: Adobe, Dollar General

Wednesday's IBD Stock Of The Day, Adobe, continues to rebound from its 50-day line after bouncing from the key support level on Wednesday. Shares rallied 1.9% in morning trade. The stock is now buyable following a rebound from the 10-week line. With pullbacks investors can reduce their risk through appropriately sizing their position.

According to the IBD Stock Checkup, Adobe stock boasts a 98 out of a best-possible 99 IBD Composite Rating, an easy way to identify top growth stocks.

Adobe is an IBD Leaderboard member.

Dollar General is trading just above a recent 194.94 buy point in a flat base after Wednesday's decisive move to reclaim the entry. Shares slipped 0.3% Thursday morning and are in buy range. The 5% buy zone goes up to 204.69.

The discount retailer is a new addition to the IBD SwingTrader service.

Stock Market Earnings: Azek, NetEase

IPO stock Azek is at the top of the 5% buy zone above a 33.85 buy point in an IPO base amid Thursday's 3% rise on earnings. The 5% buy zone goes up to 35.54.

Early Thursday, the decking and building products supplier reported strong revenue and guidance.

Chinese video game maker NetEase slid 2% despite easily beating the Street's estimates early Thursday.

NetEase stock is nearing completion of a five-week flat base, which would offer a new buy point at 503.37. At its recent peak price, shares gained as much as 36% from a 367.62 buy point in a cup with handle. Shares are still holding above the 50-day line.

Stocks On The Move: Amazon, Tesla

Amazon rallied 1.3% midday Thursday, on pace to rise for second straight day. The FANG stock is putting the finishing touches on a potential flat base with a 3,344.39 buy point. Shares are about 4% away from the potential entry.

Tesla raced another 6% higher midday Thursday, following up on Wednesday's 13% surge after the company announced a five-for-one stock split.

Shares of the electric-auto maker are about 8% off their all-time highs.

Dow Jones Leaders: Apple, Microsoft

Among the top Dow Jones stocks, Apple stock rose as much as 2.6% midday Thursday, as the blue chip leader hits all-time highs. According to Bloomberg, Apple is set to launch new subscription bundles for several of the company's digital services. The new bundles are expected to debut as early as October.

Apple is more than 50% above a cup-with-handle's 288.35 buy point. Investors should take at least some profits, selling into strength to help avoid any downswing.

The blue-chip giant is the No. 1-performing Dow Jones stock in 2020, with a 53.9% advance through Wednesday's close. As a result, Apple's market cap is approaching $2 trillion, at more than $1.98 trillion. It is also an IBD Leaderboard stock idea.

Software leader Microsoft moved up 1% Thursday midday, as the stock again rebounds from its 50-day support level. Shares rallied 2.9% on Wednesday.

For 2020, Microsoft shares are up 32.7% through Aug. 12.
