The best outdoor adventure playgrounds in Kent to take the kids to this summer

The best playground around Kent to keep your little adventurers occupied

    The summer holidays have only just started and parents around Kent will be wondering how to fill those long, sunny days.

    With another scorching summer expected, outdoor activities will be the preferred option for most families.

    There are plenty of gorgeous walks to go on this summer as well as many activities geared towards entertaining your little ones.

    However, sometimes families just need a trusted classic - an activity which is both fun and easily accessible for the most part.

    Kent weather: Met Office day-by-day Kent weather forecast as temperatures set to soar to 33C

    That's where adventure playgrounds come in.

    What used to be a swing set and slides has now become massive complexes with assault courses.

    Here are some of the best outdoor adventure playground areas in the county that will entertain the kids for hours.

    Ashford Outlet Adventure Playground

    Kimberley Way, Ashford TN24 0SD

    This digitally-enhanced playground features immersive augmented reality play experiences and includes one of the UK’s tallest open space Robinia tree house towers.

    Designed and installed by Kompan, Europe’s largest provider of outdoor play equipment, the new playground at the Ashford Outlet has been customised to deliver a one-of-a-kind experience for younger visitors, featuring a bespoke ‘Garden of England’ theme.

    The SMART Playground’s innovative technology means that through QR codes placed on the playground equipment – or via the Apple or Android App Store – children can download the Hans Christian Andersen Play Gate app.

    When the app is opened children will enjoy digital versions of their favourite fairy tales, sparking their imaginations and opening the door for themed play. With augmented reality, The Play Gate app also allows fairy tale characters to 'magically' appears on the playground, where adults can encourage children to interact with the character and promote role play in the playground.

    The ‘Garden of England’ theme is further brought to life through bright flowers and butterflies, giant grass blades and an abundance of greenery.

    The new playground delivers a world-class play space for children of all abilities and includes wheelchair accessible equipment to ensure every child has equal access to the best play opportunities.

    Toddlers Cove Play Area, Westgate Parks

    Whitehall Rd, Canterbury CT2 8BQ

    This is a unique playground in that it was designed by school children from St Peter's Methodist Primary School and the Canterbury Academy back in 2014.

    The name is a little misleading as there is something on offer here for children of all ages.

    Kids will be able to enjoy swings, slides, and best of all, a zip wire!

    On warmer days, there is an ice cream van for children who might want a treat.

    Parents are advised to pack a picnic for the day and enjoy the park and play area.

    The Hop Farm Family Park, Paddock Wood


    Maidstone Rd, Paddock Wood, Tonbridge TN12 6PY

    Hop Farm boasts one of the most impressive outdoor play areas in Kent.

    There are rope ladders, slides, wobbly bridges and balance beams in their adventure area.

    Parents are also accounted for as the picnic area surrounds the adventure play area, allowing mums and dads a well deserved rest.

    They also have two "giant jumping pillows" which act like huge trampolines for kids to endlessly bounce away on.

    Other outdoor activities include a mini driving school similar to the much-loved attraction at Legoland as well as a laser tag game.

    Children can also take part in the pot-a-plant activity where they learn about plant growing and flowers. After they create their new hanging baskets, they can take them home for £5.

    More information can be found here .

    Lower Leas Coastal Park, Folkestone

    Lower Sandgate Rd, Folkestone CT20 2EX

    The Lower Leas Coastal Park in Folkestone is split into three sections: The Wild Zone, The Fun Zone and The Formal Zone.

    The Fun Zone does exactly what it says on the tin and will give your kids endless joy as it proudly hosts the largest free access adventure play area in the south east.

    Included are rocking boats, climbing walls, zip lines and slides.

    There is also an enclosed toddler construction area with sand diggers and a shipwreck play structure for the little ones.

    A dedicated set of wheelchair accessible equipment means this play area caters for everyone and will prove to be a great day out for everyone.

    Betteshanger Park, Deal

    Sandwich Road, Deal CT14 0BF

    Betteshanger Park in offers a whole host of events and things to do in Deal but the play area is what makes it so popular with children.

    An incredible swinging rope snake is the main attraction but with rope walks, slides, climbing frames, cradle swings, see-saws, swinging steps, stepping stones - there's something for everyone.

    The play area is also located near to the cafe and toilet facilities while there are also a number of benches and open grass areas available for picnics to make a day of it.

    Swanley Park, Swanley

    New Barn Rd, Swanley BR8 7PW

    Swanley Park has a wide range of different play activities for the kids to enjoy.

    This popular 60 acre space has a play area with swings, climbing frame and zip wire but also has a paddling pool and water splash area for the warm weather as well as a sand play section.

    There is also a large boating lake where you can rent out a boat to take to the water.

    Other attractions include mini golf and the recently opened Swanley New Barn Railway to take you around the park.

    Hever Castle

    Hever Rd, Hever, Edenbridge TN8 7NG

    Hever Castle might not be the first place that springs to mind when thinking of playgrounds but within the grounds lies a huge adventure land built only two years ago.

    The Tudor Towers Adventure Playground is a nine metre tall wooden play castle, bigger than a four bedroom house and complete with its own moat, drawbridge and three turrets.

    There are three towers to climb and two fireman’s poles to escape down after navigating the netted tunnels suspended high up in the air.

    Kids can even climb down the well and crawl along a hidden tunnel or find the secret dungeon.

    For younger visitors, there is a natural play area as well.

    It features as 2 metre high living willow structure, a giant sandpit, a grassy mound with three tunnels and a slide and a climbing frame.

    In the threes, there are also two lookout platforms linked by a clatter bridge and with a slide for making a quick escape.

    The play area blends with the Tudor Towers with a built in seating area for parents.

    Full information can be found here .

    Memorial Park, Herne Bay

    Ave of Remembrance, Herne Bay CT6 5RP

    Memorial Park in Herne Bay had a make over in 2015 and got a bunch of great new play equipment to keep the children entertained.

    While this may be a more traditional park than some of the other play areas on this list, the new and updated playground offers a wide range of fun equipment designed to test children's skills in balance and climbing.

    There are also lots of sport pitches in the park including tennis, basketball, cricket and football to be enjoyed.

    Grosvenor Park, Tunbridge Wells

    Upper Grosvenor Rd, Tunbridge Wells TN2 3GG

    Tunbridge Wells ' oldest public park is best known for its historic features but the kids absolutely adore the play features in Grosvenor and Hilbert Park.

    The outdoor adventure play area has some fantastic modern equipment which is more suited to older children.

    However, it also has all the usual slide and climb equipment for the little ones, as well as the swings as pictured.

    The large sand pit also goes down particularly well with those between 2 and 10 years old.

    Calverley Adventure, Tunbridge Wells

    Tunbridge Wells TN1 1SX

    If Grosvenor Park isn't enough, Tunbridge Wells also now offers the Calverley Adventure in Calverley Grounds.

    It was designed by local resident and award-winning landscape architect, Jennette Emery-Wallis, and was built in 2017 after the team raised an incredible £225k to fund it.

    Located in the town centre, Calverley Adventure offers little ones a space to let off steam with its wealth of swings, platforms, beams, slides and sand.

    Picnic benches are also located inside so parents can keep a watchful eye without losing their breath.
