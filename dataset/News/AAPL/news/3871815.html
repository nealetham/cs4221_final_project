Win a brand new electric car worth £30k - MG’s most high-tech vehicle ever

The latest family-friendly electric SUV from MG is up for grabs as part of our #Do1Thing project

  • Share

By
Joseph Dexter
  • 12:43, 17 JAN 2020
  • Updated14:09, 5 FEB 2020
  • Bookmark

    • *This competition has now closed*

    What better way to kick off the new year by driving away a brand new electric car with our brilliant guilt-free #Do1Thing giveaway?

    Whether you are on the hunt for a new car or just fancy a much needed-upgrade you can have peace of mind knowing that you are doing your bit for the environment as well.

    By collecting six passwords which will be printed in the Western Morning News between Wednesday 22 and Tuesday January 28 and you could be in with a chance of winning the new MG ZS EV.

    The exciting competition is all part of our upcoming #Do1Thing project which launches on January 22.

    We’re providing readers with all the local stories, expert advice and practical tips they need to be better informed and equipped to take positive action and be environmentally conscious.

    Tell me about the car

    MG ZS EV is the family-friendly electric SUV, designed for those who want all the advantages of zero emissions, without compromising on practicality or style.

    The latest model is MG’s most high-tech car ever, with the MG Pilot driver assistance suite setting new standards of specification and safety, entirely justified by its 5* Euro NCAP safety rating.

    Combining a spacious interior and stylish exterior, MG ZS EV Exclusive comes with a fantastic range of features.

    These include a panoramic skyroof, 17” alloy wheels, and an 8” colour touchscreen including sat nav, Android Auto and Apple CarPlay to name but a few.

    With a range of 163 miles (WLTP-certified) and frequent rapid charging capability in just 40 minutes, you can enjoy the freedom to go anywhere in the MG ZS EV.

    The entire package is wrapped up in MG’s renowned seven-year warranty, giving you peace of mind for many happy years, and miles, of motoring.

    To find out more about MG ZS EV or locate your nearest dealership, click here .

    How do I enter?

       
    • Pick up the Western Morning News paper from Wednesday January 22 to collect your first password
    • Collect all six passwords, printed daily in paper between then and Tuesday January 28
    • Fill in the form below with all 6 passwords before the closing date of 5pm on Tuesday February 4 2020 and you’ll be in the random draw
    • One overall winner will be randomly selected on Wednesday February 5 2020 from all correct answers submitted

    Please enter using the form below. Please be aware it may take a short while to load.

    Terms and conditions

    The prize consists of a brand new 69 or 70 plate MG ZS EV Exclusive in the winner’s chosen model colour. Plate will depend on chosen model colours immediate availability for delivery.

    The prize is non-negotiable, non-transferable and non-refundable. No cash alternative is available.

    The Prize will be delivered to the Winners nearest MG dealer partner within 90 days of competition closing date.

    This competition is only open to UK residents who are 18 or over at the time of the draw, except employees of MG Motor UK Limited, MG Dealer Partners and Reach and their affiliated companies, their families and agencies.

    Only one entrant per household will be accepted. All subsequent entries will be removed from the competition.

    If the Winner cannot be contacted by MG Motor UK within 7 days of being notified of their status as the Winner by e-mail, then MG Motor UK reserves the right to then select an Alternate winner, who will then have 7 days to accept the prize following the same routes of contact as the original Winner.

    This competition is syndicated across each of Birmingham Mail, Bristol Post, Burton Mail, Cambridge News, Coventry Telegraph, Daily Post - Wales, Derby Telegraph, Grimsby Telegraph, Huddersfield Daily Examiner, Hull Daily Mail, Leicester Mercury, Liverpool Echo, Manchester Evening News, Newcastle Journal, Nottingham Post, Paisley Daily Express, South Wales Echo, South Wales Evening Post, Stoke The Sentinel, The Chronicle - Newcastle, The Gazette - Teesside, The Herald (Plymouth), Western Daily Press, Western Morning News, Western Mail.
