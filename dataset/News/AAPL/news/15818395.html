What are the different types of American Express cards?

American Express credit cards fit into four main categories:

  • Cash back cards:American Express credit cards that earn cash back accrue Reward Dollars that can be redeemed for statement credits.
  • Amex Membership Rewards cards:You can use American Express Membership Rewards points to get cash back, buy merchandise and gift cards, book travel and more.
  • Business cards:Amex offers over a dozen cards for small business owners, including rewards credit cards, co-branded cards and cards with no preset spending limits.
  • Co-branded cards: American Express partners with Delta SkyMiles, Hilton Honors, Marriott Bonvoy and other brands to offer credit cards with tailored rewards.

How do American Express credit card rewards work?

The rewards you earn will depend on your American Express card.

With a co-branded card, you’ll generally earn points or miles through the partner brand. For example, Amex’s Delta credit cards earn Delta SkyMiles. These miles are deposited into your Delta SkyMiles account, not your Amex card account.

With a cash back card, you’ll earn “Reward Dollars” that you can redeem for statement credits. This is a bit of a bummer on Amex cards, because most cash back credit cards from other issuers offer additional options for redeeming rewards, such as a check or deposit to a bank account. That said, cash back does not come with a minimum redemption amount, though you can’t use your cash back to cover your minimum payment. Your cash back will never expire as long as your account remains active.

With a card that earns Amex Membership Rewards, you can redeem for travel, cash back, merchandise and more. Amex Membership Rewards points don’t expire as long as your account is open and in good standing, though you may forfeit points if you violate the program’s terms and conditions.

Minimum redemption amounts and point values can vary depending on the redemption option. For example, most point transfers have a 1,000-point minimum, and travel redemptions require at least 5,000 points.

Points are worth 1 cent each toward flights booked through Amex Travel or hotel stays booked with American Express Fine Hotels + Resorts, 0.7 cents each toward prepaid hotels, cars, cruises and vacations booked with Amex Travel, or 0.6 cents each to cover your charges, for instance.

A business card might be co-branded or earn either points or cash back.

Where is American Express accepted?

American Express cards are accepted at 99% of US retailers that take credit cards, according to a 2022 Nilson Report. You can search online to see if a particular US business will accept your Amex card.

Still, you should bring a Visa or Mastercard if you are traveling internationally, just in case a business doesn’t accept your American Express card abroad.

What are American Express cards’ benefits?

American Express cards provide different benefits, but, broadly, you can look for the following upsides if you have an Amex credit card.

  • Lucrative rewards: Most Amex cards come with a rewards program. If you choose the right card for your budget, spending habits and lifestyle, you could amass cash back, Membership Rewards points or rewards in a brand’s loyalty program.

How to apply for an American Express credit card

You can apply for an American Express credit card online or by phone (888-297-1244).

Applying for a credit card requires certain personal and financial information, such as your mailing address, employment status and gross annual income.

Amex’s “Apply With Confidence” feature allows you to find out if you’re approved for a personal American Express card with no impact on your credit score until you accept the card. Since applying for a credit card can lead to a small dip in your credit score, this feature can help you avoid hurting your credit if you won’t be approved for the card.

Which American Express card is easiest to get?

All of the cards on this page require good to excellent credit. On the FICO scale, that’s a score of at least 670. You’ll be hard-pressed to find an Amex card that’s “easy” to get. The issuer doesn’t offer student credit cards, secured credit cards or other options geared toward consumers building or rebuilding credit.

How many Amex cards can you have?

Amex doesn’t disclose a card limit on its website, but crowdsourced data indicates that you can generally have five personal or business American Express credit cards, plus additional Amex cards without a preset spending limit (such as the Amex Platinum or Amex Business Gold).

Are American Express credit cards worth it?

An American Express credit card can be a good choice for consumers with strong credit looking to earn rewards on their spending. As with other credit cards, you’ll want to pay your balance in full to avoid interest charges. If you need time to pay off a larger purchase, you can look for a card with a 0% introductory annual percentage rate (APR).

What are the interest rates on American Express cards?

The interest rate you’ll get on your Amex card depends on factors including your creditworthiness. If your card doesn’t come with a preset spending limit, you’ll see a pay over time APR in place of a purchase APR.

Welcome bonus value (25%)

A welcome bonus is a great incentive to open a card and try it out, and American Express is especially generous with some of its cards. We assessed these bonuses based on dollar value (for cash back cards) or considering average points values from travel site The Points Guy, with higher grades going to cards with bigger returns.

Rewards value (25%)

Beyond the welcome bonus, we evaluated Amex cards on their ongoing earning potential. Cards that don’t offer strong bonus categories or a low base rewards rate got lower rankings than those that come with spending categories that appeal to a broad range of customers.

Select fees (25%)

Benefits and annual credits/bonuses (25%)

American Express is well-known for providing generous benefits and an array of annual credits and bonuses tied to making certain purchases. Some of these are easy to take advantage of while others require some effort. Our rankings count the easy-to-use perks and ignore the more niche benefits.
