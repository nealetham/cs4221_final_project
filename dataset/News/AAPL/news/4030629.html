Freddie the Ferret, naked horse-riding and belching cows: Angus Young's 2020 predictions

A light-hearted look ahead to what might happen over the next 12 months

    There's a new year just around the corner so what does 2020 have in store?

    Will it be a year to remember or one to forget?

    Can we expect a few surprises along the way or just more of the same old same old ?

    In a light-hearted look ahead to the coming 12 months, Hull Live's Angus Young expects plenty of headlines about ambitious ferrets, water main bursts, burping cows and politicians clamouring for attention.

    So sit back, kick off your shoes and have that brand new diary handy to scribble down some important dates to look forward to.

    January

    After biting Labour's Karl Turner during the general campaign, Freddie the Ferret joins the Conservatives and is immediately installed as the party's candidate in the forthcoming Humberside police and crime commissioner elections.

    "There will be no weasel words from me," says Freddie at his first press conference.

    February

    Hull City announce the club's latest initiative to reach out to stayaway fans.

    On future match days vice-chairman Ehab Allam will work behind the bar at the Silver Cod before taking on a regular post-game karaoke slot at the New Griffin.

    March

    A new water main burst in the middle of the A63in Hull leaves St. Andrew's Quay stuck in the middle of the Humber.

    Yorkshire Water promise to lay on a regular ferry service for shoppers to get to B&Q.

    April

    The cows on Beverley Westwood are put on a strict diet of ginger tea as part of a drive to reduce methane emissions after concerns are raised over a build-up of greenhouse gas above the historic market town.

    A spokesman for the Pasture Masters said: "Beverley will soon be no place for belching bovines."

    May

    A record turnout at Hull City Council's elections is attributed to a ground-breaking sponsorship deal with Papa's which sees every voter being rewarded with a free bag of chips.

    A council spokesman denies it will drive up obesity rates in the city by claiming more voters are now jogging to their nearest polling station than ever before.

    Celebs & TV news

    June

    Freddie the Ferret secures an historic win in the PCC election but ruins a subsequent photo-call with Boris Johnson by suddenly disappearing down the Prime Minister's trousers before being suspended by the Conservative Party.

    After spending three hours in emergency surgery, Mr Johnson tells reporters: "Thank goodness for the 20,000 extra nurses and 40 new hospitals we have created since January."

    July

    Humber Street bosses enter a national competition to become Britain's trendiest street and immediately introduce a strict dress code for anyone heading for one of its bars or shops.

    From now on, only hipsters with beards, waistcoats and Peaky Blinders-style flat caps will be allowed in.

    August

    New Northern Powerhouse minister Jacob Rees-Mogg visits Hull but can't get any further thanWhitefriargateafter city council leaders erect a large wooden barrier and swear their undying allegiance to Jeremy Corbyn.

    A potentially lengthy stand-off is avoided when Mr Rees-Mogg returns to the Marina and heads off in his gold-crusted yacht vowing to never venture north of Watford ever again.

    September

    Thieves steal the Humberside Police pod just days before it is due to move out of the city centre.

    "We are looking for at least one man carrying a shipping container," said a force spokesperson.

    October

    Hull Fair is cancelled when the damaged caused by last year's water main burst in Walton Street opens up again and turns into a giant sink hole swallowing up most of the nearby fairground site.

    Yorkshire Water promise to lay on canoes for commuters heading between Anlaby Road and Spring Bank West.

    November

    Michelle Dewberry "steps away" from her starring role in the forthcoming New Theatre Christmas panto after Nigel Farage is confirmed as a late addition to the cast of Peter Pan playing the part of Captain Hook.

    "I'm out of a job as an MEP after leaving the EU so I needed the money," says Mr Farage.

    December

    Hull 2017 chair Rosie Millard emulates Lady Godiva by embarking on a 141-mile naked horse ride to Coventry to celebrate it becoming the next UK City of Culture in 2021.

    Equipped with a supply of pattie butties, she says: "It's going to be amazing."
