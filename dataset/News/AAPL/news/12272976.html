Hull duo will be hoping to wow judges on BBC One tonight - while sporting bright green suits

Glenn and Grant Davison will be performing on new show All Together Now

    Two brothers from Hull will be trying to wow 100 judges on BBC One tonight.

    The Gherkins will be performing on All Together Now, a BBC singing show where the way to make it through to the next round of the competition is to get as many of the 100 judges on their feet as possible.

    Glenn Davison, one half of The Gherkins and a Come Dine With Me winner, said: We are the guys in bright green suits that perform at events, weddings, parties, funerals, festivals and more.

    We sing barbershop-style oldies with a twist, and BBC One approached us last year to audition for the new show.

    After passing all of the auditions we finally got to the last point where we had to perform for the BBC executives and, to our surprise, we were selected from more than 2,000 people to make it to the final 60.

    As far as Im aware, were the only Hull contestants to make it through that far, so that makes us very proud.

    The Gherkins consist of Glenn and his brother Grant, who sing and play guitar and harmonica respectively, but they did used to be a quartet. However, due to other commitments, the brothers have carried on the project as a duo.

    We have had a fabulous journey getting training from world class voice coaches and a choreography team, said Glenn.

    Stuff that money couldnt buy - it was fab and really helped us to take our act to the next level.

    I often sing in my crepe van which is my business, The Crepe Company, and its very exciting singing whilst watching my crepe queues grow.

    All Together Now features former Spice Girl Geri Horner and comedian Rob Beckett, as they work alongside 100 judges who are all in the music profession. The aim of the fun, family entertainment game is to try and get as many judges on their feet as possible singing along.

    Glen said: Off the back of the TV show we now have a manager who is investing time and money into The Gherkins, so we feel very fortunate.

    We are hoping to get gigs this year and expand our set list with various new, dazzling songs.

    The Gherkins will be performing at The Adelphi on Wednesday, to raise money for a local animal charity.

    The duo, alongside other local acts, will be raising money for Not Just Pets on Chanterlands Avenue, with the gig starting at 7.30pm and entry costing 3 on the door. Donations of cat food are also welcome.

    All Together Now will air tonight at 7.15pm on BBC One.
