Big Brother: Brian Belo's life after winning the show from Hollyoaks to going off the grid

He's total Big Brother royalty - but what's he been up to since his win?

    Brian Belo won the eighth series of Channel 4's Big Brother in 2007. From acting roles to lawsuits, he has had a varied career since he left the house.

    Perhaps most will remember Brian's time on the show for not knowing who Shakespeare was. But as a winner of the show, he continued to ride out his fame after leaving and quickly made his stage debut in The Vegemite Tales.

    Soon after that he also made a cameo appearance in Hollyoaks. As part of Stratford-upon-Avon's Shakespeare Aloud initiative, Brian performed in productions of Shakespeare plays - a project to make the classic works more accessible to the public.

    Appearing on TV and then the stage, Brian also had a go at breaking into the music industry. He released his single 'Essex Boy' in July 2008, but it failed to chart.

    The 34-year-old also claimed that he came up with the original idea for TV show, The Only Way is Essex. Produced by Lime Pictures for ITV, the franchise made more than 1 billion.

    Brian claimed he pitched the show to them and had created a trailer for his version, Totally Essex. The Dulwich-born entertainer settled the 4 million lawsuit in the end but the sum was not revealed.

    He regularly starred on Harry Hill's TV Burp too, giving his opinions on the week's TV. Following this theme, Brian also became an internet columnist for Heat Magazine for the tenth series of Big Brother.

    Brian never strayed too far from his Big Brother roots, making various reappearances over the years. Chris Moyles sent him back to the house shortly after his series in Big Brother: Celebrity Hijack. He surprised his housemates wearing just a towel.

    For a while, he then regularly featured on the spin-off chat show, Big Brother's Big Mouth. To commemorate the tenth year of the hit Channel 4 reality show, he temporarily returned to the house again as well as taking part in Big Brother's Big Quiz.

    For Big Brother 16, Brian returned yet again in June 2015 as part of a 'Time Warp' twist. The star was joined by two other major celebs from the show, Helen Wood and the late Nikki Grahame.

    But Brian had a number of heated arguments with Helen, that led to him climbing out of the garden to get away from her. He even appeared in Charlie Brooker's Big Brother drama, titled Dead Set, as a zombie and as himself.

    Although he seemed to show a lot of interest in keeping involved with the show, he started to bad mouth it in 2018. Brian had said that the fact it was ending was 'not a great loss' because it was going in an 'awful direction'.

    Brian has also now deleted his Instagram account and doesn't have any public use of social media.
