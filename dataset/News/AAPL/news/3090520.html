Streaming has become part of life. Instead of downloading MP3s to your laptop or organizing DVDs on your shelves, most households have switched to streaming services. In fact, a recent Deloitte digital media trends report found that 88% of consumers have a paid streaming video subscription—and that’s not including subscriptions to music, gaming or other forms of streaming.

Whether you’re signing up for a new service or looking to maximize the subscriptions you already have, using the right credit card can turn your couch surfing into a rewarding endeavor. Let’s take a look at the best cards for streaming subscriptions.

Best Credit Cards for Customizable Bonus Categories

Cards with a customizable bonus category let you select the category where you prefer to earn bonus rewards. Whether streaming services make sense as a custom category depends on where you shop and how much you spend. If you spend significantly more in a quarter on fast food or home improvement, choosing streaming services could add up to a loss of rewards.

These cards often include rewards caps, so crunch the number to make sure the card works for your spending.

  • Bank of America® Customized Cash Rewards credit card ($0 annual fee): Streaming services is an eligible category to earn 3% cash back when selected.
  • U.S. Bank Cash+® Visa Signature® Card ($0 annual fee): Select two categories to earn 5% cash back, one of which can be TV, internet and streaming services. Applies to the first $2,000 spent in combined eligible purchases each quarter.
  • Bank of America® Customized Cash Rewards Secured Credit Card* ($0 annual fee): Streaming services is an eligible category to earn 3% cash back when selected.
  • Citi Custom Cash® Card ($0 annual fee): Earn 5% cash back on select streaming services when this is your highest spending category each billing cycle, up to $500 spent. Earn 1% cash back thereafter.
  • U.S. Bank Cash+® Secured Visa® Card* ($0 annual fee): Select two categories to earn 5% cash back, one of which can be TV, internet and streaming services.

Benefits of Credit Cards for Streaming Services

The most common benefit of a credit card for streaming services is earning elevated rewards on your subscriptions and other digital streaming purchases. However, some cards may also offer statement credits or discounts toward these types of purchases.

These credit cards may offer other benefits above and beyond those specifically related to streaming services—and these perks may work well with your lifestyle for a win-win situation.

How To Choose Streaming Service Credit Cards

Choosing a streaming service credit card is similar to selecting any card for personal use. Your creditworthiness will help dictate which cards you may be eligible for. Then, you’ll want to look at the card’s defining characteristics to see which ones fit your needs.

Though subscriptions add up, they’re unlikely to be the largest portion of your monthly budget. For that reason, selecting streaming services in a customizable category may not earn you the most rewards possible. It’s wise to consider cards that earn rewards or statement credits on streaming service charges, as well as other purchases you typically make. There may also be additional card benefits that appeal to you and help you choose a specific credit card.

Also, consider the terms and conditions of any card. Annual fees, interest rates, promotional rates and other fees will vary and may impact which card you choose. Some of the cards on this list include reward caps. It’s important to consider your potential spending to ensure you choose a card that won’t limit your potential rewards.

Credit cards with rotating categories do not disclose their categories in advance, so you take a bit of a gamble that the bonus category will be beneficial for your spending. These cards haven’t historically been rewarding for streaming services. But if you hold a rotating category credit card, keep an eye out—you could potentially receive 5% rewards in one quarter.

For many people, using a flat-rate 2% credit card may be sufficient to boost rewards without having to keep track of your category spending.

Make Sure Your Preferred Services Are Covered

Not all streaming services are created equal, and not all cards cover every service provider. Some cards only cover “popular streaming services,” which could mean your niche anime subscription isn’t covered.

Capital One, for instance, covers a solid list of steamers but excludes Prime Video, AT&T TV, Verizon Fios On Demand, as well as audiobook subscriptions and fitness programming. American Express offers a slightly longer list. Unfortunately, if your streaming service is bundled with your internet or billed through a third party like your cellphone provider or a car manufacturer, those services may not be eligible.

Before selecting a card to bolster your rewards, read the fine print. Some cards work great for select media companies but won’t serve as an all-around earner. For instance, the Blue Cash Everyday® Card from American Express offers a statement credit for the Disney Bundle with enrollment. That means you’ll need to purchase through DisneyPlus.com, Hulu.com or ESPNPlus.com to qualify. It also earns rewards for online retail purchases, but that does not include streaming services. Instead, the Blue Cash Preferred® Card from American Express earns rewards on selectU.S. streaming subscriptions.

How Much Can a Credit Card for Streaming Really Earn?

Your potential credit card rewards from streaming services will vary greatly based on your household spending. Using data from various government agencies, Forbes Advisor estimates that the 70th percentile of wage earners bring in $118,438 annually and spend approximately $679 annually on streaming expenses.

The reality is that for such a low annual spend, the rewards you could earn won’t likely be high enough to choose a credit card for that alone. In fact, most users will earn less than $34 in rewards, no matter what card they select.

Alternative Ways To Save on Your Streaming Services

Check Your Cellphone Subscription Options

A number of cellphone services include streaming services in their higher-tier subscriptions. T-Mobile has plans that include Apple TV, Netflix and Hulu. Some of the unlimited Verizon Wireless plans include the Disney Bundle or Netflix and Max. Mint Mobile even includes a few free months of Paramount+ for new customers with their unlimited plans.

While we don’t recommend switching phone providers or even plans to include a streaming service, check to see if you already have included entertainment.

Rotate Your Streaming Services

If you feel like your spending is out of control, you can rotate streaming subscriptions throughout the year. Admittedly, this option requires a little extra effort. If you feel like you’ve run out of new shows, canceling a subscription for a few months until the next season drops could save some serious cash.

This is a great strategy to consider if you carry several streaming subscriptions simultaneously. You might also want to give this a try if you have a busy season with school, sports or travel where you’re out of the house more than you’re home.

Shop for Deals

Keep an eye out for gift cards to your favorite streaming services. If they’re available at your local grocery store, picking up a gift card while earning top rewards with a grocery credit card may make more sense. Some grocery chains, like Kroger, even offer additional fuel points if you purchase gift cards on a select day.

The Target Circle™ Credit Card* will save you 5% on all Target purchases, including gift cards. Plus, Sam’s Club members can save on a number of streaming gift cards and earn on top of your savings with the Sam’s Club® Mastercard®*. Periodically, you can even find deals for streaming services on gift card discount sites like Raise.com.

A number of credit cards include merchant offers, like Amex Offers and Chase Offers. You can periodically find discounts available for popular streaming services, but make sure you read the fine print carefully. These offers may only be available for new subscribers, require a minimum purchase and often require you to add them to your credit card and purchase through the provided link. Many streaming services also offer discounts for students or military members. If someone in your household qualifies, make sure to put your streaming services in their name.

Bottom Line

Streaming services aren’t going away anytime soon, and the list of options seems to be growing regularly. In the coming years, we are likely to see even more services and subscriptions transition to streaming-only. That means it’s important to evaluate your spending and keep track of your subscriptions. We recommend periodic checks to make sure you’re not overcommitted.

You can easily earn rewards from your streaming services, but likely not enough to justify a dedicated credit card. Check your current cards to select the best option, or use a card like the Wells Fargo Active Cash® Card that earns a flat-rate 2% cash rewards on purchases.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Which is the best overall credit card for streaming services?

Your personal spending patterns, rewards preference and credit card usage will ultimately determine which card for streaming services is the best overall for you. Some customers will prioritize statement credits while others prefer to focus on the highest rewards. Either way, there are options to maximize streaming services on a variety of cards.

Do you need a streaming-specific credit card?

Households that only spend a small amount on streaming services may not need a credit card specifically for these purchases, but someone who spends on multiple streaming services may wish to choose a streaming-specific card. Keep in mind that even credit cards for streaming services may offer additional rewards or benefits, so shop around to find a card that fits your overall lifestyle.
