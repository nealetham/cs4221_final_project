GNC warns of bankruptcy, store closings as sales plummet amid coronavirus

  • Published: May. 13, 2020, 2:44 p.m.
By
  • Nicolette Accardi | NJ Advance Media for NJ.com

Nutrition supplement retailer GNC said on Monday that bankruptcy and store closings are looming as the coronavirus pandemic has plummeted its sales.

GNC says it may have to file for Chapter 11 bankruptcy to “implement a restructuring plan.”

The company reported its sales are down 10.1% in the first quarter year-over-year as a result of the pandemic.

GNC also says the financial impact of the coronavirus pandemic has caused the company to withhold its April rent for select stores. Payments of approximately $19 million in April and approximately $16 million in May have been withheld, according to the statement.

Approximately 1,300 (40%) of its U.S. and Canada stores are temporarily closed as of May 6. Some of the stores “may be closed permanently in the future,” GNC said in the statement.

“While this unprecedented situation has significantly disrupted our business and required us to make difficult decisions, we are focused on supporting customers with high quality products through our strong e-commerce channels and new buy-online-ship-from-store and curbside pickup services," Ken Martindale, CEO of GNC, said in the statement. “Our transformation into a true omni-channel brand continues and we are working diligently to reposition and restructure our business for the future.”

GNC has approximately 5,200 stores in the U.S., including about 1,600 Rite Aid licensed “store-within-a-store locations,” according to the statement.

Retailers that have also been slammed by the coronavirus pandemic are Neiman Marcus, J.Crew, and Nordstrom.
