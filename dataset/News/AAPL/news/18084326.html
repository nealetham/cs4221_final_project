    Reading manager Mark Bowen has issued a warning to teenage forward Danny Loader amid speculation linking him with a move away from the club.

    The 19-year-old is out of contract with Royals next summer and several clubs, including Wolves and Manchester United, have been linked with a move for him.

    Loader has made just 11 starts for Reading since breaking into the senior team almost exactly a year ago but has struggled to live up to the hype.

    Talk continues about a move away from Berkshire but Royals' boss has warned the youngster any move he does make must be the right one for his career.

    "Danny has got quality but whether he would get the chances at [a club like] Manchester United - it's all in the name," said Bowen.

    "If he keeps working hard and improving then he might but it's a situation Danny will personally have to deal with, with his family.

    "I dare say it's not a bad situation to be in and it's not bad to have the calibre of clubs interested in him.

    "When you see things in the paper about yourself you're only human in thinking 'wow' these clubs are in for me.

    "He comes across as a very level headed guy and player. He knows he still has a lot to learn. In terms of what he has done in his career he has not done a lot but it's all potential with him.

    "He's had some good performances in a Reading shirt and scored goals at U23 level and for the first team but he's got it all in front of him.

    "That only comes if his mentality is right so it's all potential at the moment."

    Loader has been just a bit-part player this season for Reading and made just one league start - on the opening day against Sheffield Wednesday.

    He continues to impress at U23 level, however, and scored for England U20s over the international break.

    The Reading-born attacker, who came through the ranks at the club, almost joined Wolves over the summer but the deal fell through in the final hours of the window.

    BerkshireLive understands talk of Manchester United's interest is wide of the mark, but Wolves maintain an interest in Loader and could well make a move for him again in January.

    Bowen says the situation is not ideal for the club or the player as Loader nears the final six months of his deal, but he admits he will not let the speculation affect his thinking going into games.

    "Personally as a manager you don't want this distraction," said the manager.

    "You want your players signed up long term and put to bed their contract situations.

    "Ultimately we can put a package together for Danny and offer it to him. The work he is getting in training excites him but it'll come down to him whether he does it for financial reasons or feels he has a better opportunity somewhere else.

    "In terms of picking my team and squad it is difficult because I have to try and put that to one side. I have to pick a team and a squad on a matchday which I feel can make a difference for us.

    "That can be hard because you have one eye on him being an asset and part of the club's future and another eye on what he can do in this present time."
