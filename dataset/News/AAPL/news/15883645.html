Apple’s iPad range continues to grow. Literally, in terms of the display size. And the latest rumor says the trend is about to continue. Two new iPad models are predicted, boasting the largest-ever displays seen on the Air and mini versions of the tablet.

ForbesApple AirPods Pro Upgrades Almost Stole The Show At WWDC 2020: Wait Until You Hear What They Didn't AnnounceBy David Phelan

Last year, the entry-level iPad, which had had a 9.7in display since the launch of the first model in Spring 2010, suddenly appeared with a 10.2in screen.

Now, according to reliable tipster Ming-Chi Kuo from TF International Securities, there’s an iPad coming with a 10.8in screen. This is not the first time such a screen size has been predicted, as you can read here. So, there’s an increasingly widespread belief that we can expect this bigger screen.

Outside the iPad Pro range, iPad displays have always been 9.7in until the 10.2in appeared. That’s apart from the current iPad Air which has a 10.5in screen.

Kuo, in his latest prediction which was picked up by Joe Rossignol at MacRumors, doesn’t say whether the 10.8in screen he’s banking on will be an entry-level iPad or a new iPad Air.

Let’s be clear: I’m certain it’s the Air.

To increase the size of the entry-level model, so soon after its once-in-a-decade screen bump to 10.2in last year seems completely unbelievable to me. Besides, that would mean the pricier Air would then have a smaller display than the entry-level model which is unthinkable.

So, for sure, the iPad that’s coming will be an iPad Air. Kuo says that it will be with us later this year.

My suspicion is that Apple will try to fit the larger display into a chassis that matches, roughly or exactly, the current one. In other words, it’s likely to be a bigger screen achieved with thinner bezels around the display.

Kuo had one other item to report: that an iPad mini with a larger display is also expected. This correlates with previous rumors, with Kuo suggesting it will have an 8.5in display. This will be the first time an iPad mini has sported anything other than a 7.9in screen.

That sounds great but, curb your excitement, because it’s not coming soon: Kuo says we should expect this biggest-ever small-screen iPad in the first half of 2021.

The final detail shared by the analyst is that both iPads will come with powerful 20W chargers.

With an iPad Pro still rumored to come soon, perhaps this year, the months ahead are proving to be important for the iPad family.
