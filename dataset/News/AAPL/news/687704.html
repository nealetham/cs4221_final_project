05/01 Update below. This post was originally published on April 28

Apple continues to win well-deserved plaudits after releasing thebest value smartphone of 2020(despitesome small doubts). But for millions of existing iPhone owners,things have been bad. And now they’re worse.

ForbesApple iPhone 12: Everything We Know So FarBy Gordon Kelly

Following Apple’s confirmation that almost all iPhones are vulnerable to apotentially serious flaw in the iOS Mail app, the company has now started work to fix an even more immediate problem which enables anyone to seriously crash your iPhone.

04/30 Update: major news for everyone who owns an iPhone, iPad, MacBook, Apple Watch and even Apple TV users afterGoogle's Project Zeroteam discovered "numerous new vulnerabilities" in iOS, iPadOS, macOS, watchOS and tvOS. Dubbed 'Fuzzing ImageIO', the Project Zero team explains that the security holes center on media files sent over messaging platforms (more information here). As is policy for Project Zero, it is only disclosing these flaws after contacting Apple and the company fixed them in its latest platform updates. The problem with this, however, is we now know updating to the latest versions of iOS, macOS and watchOS introduce the serious crash bug (detailed in this post below), which leaves users in a Catch-22 situation. To solve this, Apple is accelerating the release of iOS 13.5 (the new name for iOS 13.4.5, which it has been testing for several weeks) along with patches for the other platforms and it should arrive within the next week. Stay tuned.

05/01 Update:MacRumorshas confirmed what sources told me below, Apple has indeed patched both the messages hack as well as the iOS Mail vulnerability whichimpacts every iPhone released in the last 10 years. It seems Apple has significantly stepped up its plans for what was originally iOS 13.4.5. In addition to fixing both vulnerabilities, it contains the company'sCoronavirus exposure contact tracing system (which can be disabled). As such, the new name of iOS 13.5 makes a lot more sense. I'd also expect significantly more iOS 13 updates now as Apple refines its tracing system, before iOS 14 arrives in September.

Firstreported last week, we now know every iPhone running Apple’s latest iOS 13.4.1 software can be made to crash, often completely locking up the device and even forcing a restorevia DFU mode, if it receives a message containing characters in theSindhi language. Anyone can initiate the crash on another person’s iPhone just by sending a specific combination of these characters (excluded from this post for obvious reasons) to Message or Mail. iPads, Macs and even the Apple Watch can also be affected.

The good news is Apple is moving quickly to fix the damage. The official @AppleSupport has been working at full speed to help users (tip:turn off Mail and Message notifications, the crash stems from the notification not the message itself) and Apple has also been hard at work because I can confirm that the latest beta of iOS 13.4.5 fixes it.

In myiOS 13.4.1 Upgrade Guide, I warned users not to update due to a number of bugs (both introduced and ignored). That said, if you (understandably) couldn’t resist the lure of new software, you might not have long to wait. I understand that Apple is to release either iOS 13.4.5 or an iOS 13.4.2 emergency update this week, and it will contain a patch for thebig iOS Mail vulnerabilityas well.

Watch this space for developments.
