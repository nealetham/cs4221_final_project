A15 Bionic: How TSMC’s Performance-Enhanced 5nm Could Improve iPhone

According to a recent leak, Apple has already begun development work on its next iPhone processor, the A15. The chip will supposedly be manufactured using TSMC’s enhanced 5-nanometer process. This will result in higher performance, as is seen every year, but this new information provides a bit of insight into how much faster the next generation will be.

✕ Remove Ads

In 2018, Apple’s A12 was the CPU used in the iPhone XR and XS. This was made with a 7-nanometer process, meaning the space between transistors is at that scale, a nanometer being one-billionth of a meter. This size reduction aided the 40-percent jump in performance over the A11. 2019 brought the iPhone 11 and the A13 chip. While still using the same scale, 7-nanometers, the performance was increased another 20 percent, showing that there's more than just size involved in speedier chips.

Related: A14 Bionic Vs. A12Z: Apple's iPad Air 4 & iPad Pro Chips Compared

With the iPhone 12 just reaching customers, Apple is already working on next year’s version. What could safely be assumed will be the iPhone 13 will likely be powered by an A15 Bionic processor. According to a report by the China Times, the A15 is in development now and is expected to be manufactured with an enhanced version of the 5-nanometer process that was used for this year’s A14 Bionic chip. The performance-enhanced process is known as N5P. Apple will likely continue to work with TSMC, one of only two semiconductor manufacturers to mass-produce at 5-nanometers, the other being Samsung. TSMC has a denser 5-nanometer process node than Samsung, resulting in better performance and efficiency, which helps explain the overwhelming demand for TSMC chips that were in high demand this year.

✕ Remove Ads

How 5NP Will Improve The A15

Through optimizations in the transistor and interconnect layers, N5P by itself is expected to give either 7 percent higher performance over N5 or be 15 percent more efficient. This choice can be tuned by the designer, so Apple can choose to add a bit of each, tune purely for battery life or tune to maximize speed. Of course, benefits that come from manufacturing improvement and refinement are only part of the equation. Apple continues to customize its logic to add speed, stability, security, and efficiency with each new generation. Over the last several years, Apple has kept up a rapid pace, improving processor performance an average of 20 percent every year, including the shift from N7 to N7P for the A13 used in the iPhone 11.

✕ Remove Ads

More important than the expected performance and efficiency improvements is what Apple will do with the extra speed and battery life that N5P will offer. Augmented reality (AR) is clearly a big focus for Apple, with LiDAR becoming more common, featured in its iPad Pro and iPhone Pro line up. The Apple Glass rumors indicate Apple may launch its first AR headset in 2021, with a refined version coming in 2022. With ultra-wideband technology in the iPhone 11 and iPhone 12, they can track the location and orientation of other devices that contain an U1 chip, which would be quite useful for AR also. A more efficient iPhone 13 may be less reliant upon fast charging. Could this allow for the removal of the Lightning port? There is a whole year until the next iPhone is announced, but with continued manufacturing process improvements aiding Apple’s innovations, the iPhone will continue to provide exciting updates.
