15 things you’ll remember if you grew up going to Bluewater Shopping Centre

From using the Apple store for anything except buying expensive products to being split up by security guards...

    For people in Kent, Bluewater offers an unparalleled experience for shopping, eating and a day out.

    And for teenagers, it offers a social hub and a nice setting to often do nothing, but safe in the knowledge that plenty of your mates would be also be there with the exact same intentions.

    The shopping centre, built in an abandoned quarry, is the fifth largest in the UK and in the 21 years it’s been open, has turned from GCSE geography case study to iconic Kent landmark.

    Here is a list of things you’ll remember if you grew up socialising there as a teenager.

    17 things you’ll only know if you grew up in Gravesend in the 2000s

    Part-time job

    If you were after a part-time job, it is almost certain that Bluewater was your first port of call.

    You were sucked in by the bright lights of Apple, JD Sports and John Lewis.

    The moment you turned 16 you were firing off hundreds of CV’s, although you hadn’t got your GCSE’s yet, all you could do was put you were a ‘hard-working, motivated and confident individual’, in really large font.

    If you were lucky you might have a mates’ brother who would get you in at Topman.

    Huge cinema

    If you like huge cinemas with chronically overpriced tickets, then Bluewater’s cinema is the one for you.

    That being said, the Tango Ice Blast they do are unbelievable.

    Emo lake

    Stay with me on this one.

    Around the quarry that Bluewater is in, there are numerous lakes, this particular one has a park by it and it provided the perfect setting to walk around idly - if you’d got bored of doing that inside.

    No one quite knows why it was called emo lake, or how it got its name.

    I’d probably rather not know.

    Seating in the food hall

    So, you’ve just bought some food from the main food hall and you’d like to use the communal seating?

    That’s a shame.

    If you’d like a seat, you’ll have to adopt the busy car park etiquette of ominously hovering around people who look like they’re ready to move.

    Forgetting where you've parked

    The sheer size of the Bluewater site has meant there is normally an abundance of parking options.

    The issue is, it all looks the same.

    If you’ve ever forgot where your car is then you’re in the majority.

    Posh aisle

    The so-called posh aisle, home to the upmarket food court and more high-end and boutique brands.

    Not a Maccy’s or Primark in sight.

    Pretzel free sample

    Quite how this business made it’s money is beyond me.

    Their business model of giving away their product seems to have worked though.

    Who am I to complain?

    Christmas time

    There is no where I’d rather be less than Bluewater at Christmas time.

    Parking is atrocious, the shops are chaos, the traffic is at a standstill, it’s the most wonderful time of the year.

    Apple store

    The Apple store was the perfect way to kill time.

    Go in, log on to your Facebook, and whatever you do remember to log out again.

    I feel sorry for the poor workers there, seeing group after group of 13 year-olds walking-in, knowing full well that their pockets weren’t flush with the cash you’d actually need to buy anything from there.

    We were just there to take some sepia pics on their webcams.

    No one really knows what the kiosk shops do

    Belt buckles, massage guns, tea with bubbles in it?

    For a couple of years, Hollister was the place to go

    The shop used to have a huge queues outside, despite the fact it was so poorly lit you couldn’t see further than a foot in front of you.

    Maybe it had something to do with their recruitment policy…

    The queue outside Nando's

    There is one restaurant which understandably reigns supreme over the rest.

    There are two Nando’s in Bluewater, and if you try to go at lunch time or in the evening on the weekend, then expect a queue for your troubles.

    Actual activities

    Bluewater has been home to many different outdoor and indoor activities. Water-zorbing, adventure parks, trampoline parks, crazy golf.

    These seemed like a great idea and were probably criminally under-appreciated.

    Birthday at TGI Friday’s

    There is a 120 per cent chance that if you spent considerable time in Bluewater then you either had or have been to somebody’s birthday meal at the American themed restaurant.

    Don’t blame me, I’m just quoting facts.

    Being split up by security if you were in a ‘gaggle’

    The sight of a large group of teenagers was like a red-rag to Bluewater security guards.

    Unfortunately for them, this was a Tom & Jerry-esque storyline which played out every weekend.
