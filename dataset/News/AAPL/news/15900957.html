Coronavirus LIVE: Economy minister Ken Skates to give Welsh Government update

The Welsh Government has committed to another three weeks of lockdown

    Face coverings on public transport

    Mr Skates says it is vital that public transport is used to empower people and enrich their lives, rather than inhibit peoples movements. He says unpaid carers would be considered to be a priority for public transport.

    He says wearing face coverings on public transport will be up to the public. Mr Skates says if people wish to wear a face covering they can do so.

    • Share
    13:06Sarah Hodgson

    Three quarters of businesses being supported by furlough scheme

    Mr Skates says had the furlogh scheme not been extended then the impact on the Welsh economy would have been devastating. He notes that 74% of businesses in Wales have applied for help.

    • Share
    13:04Sarah Hodgson

    Concerns over cross-border working

    Mr Skates says concerns have been raised over cross-border working.

    He says if you cannot work from home, but your business can operate then you should speak to your boss.

    The minister says you are safe to cross the border for work purposes if you need to. He says he does not want to see people moving around Wales and over the border if they do not need to.

    Messages along the A55 are going to be adjusted, Mr Skates added.

    He urges people not to understake non-essential travel.

    • Share
    13:02Sarah Hodgson

    Easing lockdown too soon would damage economy

    The minister said we need to recognise the peak is coming at different times across the UK.

    He said it is essential we do not move away from lockdown measures until all parts of the UK are past the peak.

    He said it would be damaging for the economy and peoples health.

    • Share
    12:59Sarah Hodgson

    Policing of borders

    Mr Skates is asked if road blocks may be considered at the English-Welsh border.

    Mr Skates says the Welsh Government is working closely with police forces in England and Wales to minimise any irresponsible behaviour.

    He said it is not just the case of people coming over from England, but there are people in Wales who are not adhering to guidelines.

    Mr Skates says the police are doing an incredible job and if further support and powers are needed then that will be supported.

    He adds a patrol was done along the A5 in the Clwydian range over the weekend.

    • Share
    12:55Sarah Hodgson

    Plea to stay away from Wales

    Mr Skates is asked if he is worried about the relationship between England and Wales due to the difference in lockdown rules.

    He said you are going to be warmly welcomed to Wales when the crisis is over. He is urging people to stay at home and reminding people Snowdon is closed as are other beauty spots.

    The minister says please show respect to all parts of the UK and once the pandemic is over people will be welcomed back with open arms.

    • Share
    12:53Sarah Hodgson

    Plans to tackle unemployment

    Mr Skates says they are looking at how they can minimise unemployment and how they can grow the economy again.

    • Share
    12:45Sarah Hodgson

    Selling and buying homes

    Mr Skates says they are reviewing regulations regarding estate agents and the selling of homes. He says there are difficulties regarding social distancing.

    Unless it is essential that you work from home, you should not be doing it, he says.

    • Share
    12:42Sarah Hodgson

    Hopes of creating a greener economy

    Mr Skates says we must work smarter after lockdown eases and travel less. This is part of an effort to create a better, fairer and greener economy, he said.

    He says they will not help any businesses registered in tax havens.

    • Share
    12:41Sarah Hodgson

    Changes could be made to public transport after lockdown lifted

    Mr Skates says lockdown has been extended for three weeks to make sure the virus does not spread.

    He said the message is still stay at home and to only travel when and were is necessary.

    The minister said it is still too early to lift any other restrictions, but we must prepare for the future.

    When the time is right, Mr Skates says that the right measures need to be in place for people to feel safe and he says that is key for transport.

    He is meeting later today to make plans for transport.

    Mr Skates says we need to think about how to manage demand on public transport and social distancing.

    When easing of lockdown comes steps will be taken to manage demand. Steps include prioritising public transport for key workers, staggering shift patterns in the public sector, pre-booking travel. He says they will need the public sector and partners to show understanding.

    • Share
    12:37Sarah Hodgson

    Support for businesses

    Mr Skates says the Welsh Government has given 1.7b to businesses - saving 14,000 jobs in Wales.

    He says support with business rates has been given - and is worth more than a billion pounds.

    • Share
    12:36Sarah Hodgson

    Furlough scheme is essential

    Ken Skates says that businesses in Wales have been severely hit by Covid-19.

    He said the furlough scheme has been essential to give businesses security.

    Mr Skates says it has demonstrated the benefits of a four nation approach - and says it is essential the scheme is not stopped or scaled back too quickly.

    He said 74% have applied for the job retention scheme - which is the highest in the UK.

    He said he welcomes the announcement that the scheme will be extended until October.

    Mr Skates says any further discussions must be meaningful and need to include all four nations so that everyone can plan for recovery.

    The minister said that support is needed for businesses that cannot legally open such as tourism - and he recognises that they may need support for some time.

    12:25Sarah Hodgson

    Welsh Government briefing

    Ken Skates is giving an update on the coronavirus situation in Wales this afternoon. It kicks off at 12.30pm and well have all the latest updates from the briefing.
