New rules, regulations make MLB’s 2020 season unlike any other (podcast)

  • Published: Jun. 24, 2020, 1:49 p.m.
By
  • Joe Noga, cleveland.com
  • Paul Hoynes, cleveland.com

CLEVELAND, Ohio — Late Tuesday word broke that MLB and the players’ union had signed off on health and safety protocols for the 2020 season and that a schedule could be coming within the next three days. The plan calls for a 60-game regular season that will look unlike any other in MLB history, including the use of a universal designated hitter and other changes brought on by the COVID-19 pandemic. On Wednesday’s Cleveland Baseball Talk Podcast, Paul Hoynes and Joe Noga looked at some of the radical changes facing baseball in the coming months.

Other topics covered on Wednesday include:

  • Who will be the first Indians player to test positive for coronavirus, and how much will it hurt the club?
  • Does starting extra-inning games with a runner on second base damage the sport?
  • How can the Indians take advantage of a schedule that has them facing the NL Central in interleague play?
