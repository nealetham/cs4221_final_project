Chris Martin and Exeter - why Coldplay dropped album exclusive in city paper

It was at Exeter Cathedral School he formed his first band, The Rocking Honkies

    Celebrities can sometimes be accused of forgetting where they have come from, but not Coldplay who have gone back to their roots by unveiling their new album track listings by placing classified adverts in the papers where they grew up.

    Lead singer Chris Martin, who was born and raised in Exeter to accountant and magistrate Anthony and music teacher Alison, has paid homage to Devon by announcing details about the bands new album in the Express & Echo.

    Chris, one of five children, went to Hylton School and Exeter Cathedral School where he found his passion for music. It was there he formed his first band, The Rocking Honkies. He was also a talented cricket player.

    His parents then sent him to Sherborne School, a boys independent boarding school in Dorset. He attended University College London to study ancient world studies, where he met Will Champion, Guy Berryman and Jonny Buckland, who would become his bandmates.

    Back in Exeter, his family owned a caravan and motor home company called Martins of Exeter until his dad sold the business in 1999.

    It was founded by Chris' grandfather and was sold to an employee for 8,825,000, adding 5 million to the family fortune, already acquired from the sale of land around Countess Wear.

    His grandfather was Sheriff of Exeter and in 1968, Mayor of Exeter.

    Coldplay, Muse and Devon

    To reinforce the family's Conservative respectability, several of Chris Martin's cousins are grandchildren of Sir Winston Churchill through his Aunt Elizabeth's marriage to Julian Sandys, Churchill's grandson.

    The same year the business was sold in 1999, Coldplay sold out The Cavern in Exeter, many years before they were performing worldwide stadium tours.

    With Chris as lead singer, rhythm guitarist and pianist, Coldplay's debut album, Parachutes, sold five million copies after being released in 2000 and won a Grammy.

    The band has continued to enjoy phenomenal success and now regarded as one of the best bands in the world.

    Although Chris is said to spend a lot of his time in the US, he has been spotted enjoying a drink or two in the Royal Oak pub at Nadderwater when he visits his parents, who are believed to still live in Devon.

    In 2010, the Echo reported the childhood home of Coldplays Chris Martin was on the market for 1.35 million. He was raised in Whitestone House, near Exeter.

    The historic Grade II-listed Georgian six-bedroom property is in the tiny hamlet of Whitestone, perched 500ft above sea level overlooking the Exe Estuary.

    Only In Devon

    The family moved there in 1978, when the multi-million-selling artist was just a baby. He spent his formative years playing in the eight acres of sweeping lawns and clambering up and down its trees.

    The grounds include a tennis court, field and water trough suitable for horses or ponies.

    Chris married actress Gwyneth Paltrow in 2003 and they had two children, Apple and Moses. They are now separated.

    When they were together it was claimed Gwyneth visited the Barnfield Theatre in Exeter to see a performance by one of Chris' younger sisters.

    Chris campaigning zeal and clean-living lifestyle has always marked him out from the stereotypical rock n roll singer.

    He has always rejected the idea that refusing to dress up in leather and trash hotel rooms makes him less of an artist, claiming, it is about independence of mind and spirit.

    In 2016, Chris returned to Exeter to perform a homecoming gig as part of Radio 1s Big Weekend in Exeter at Powderham Castle.

    He said at the time: "I was born and raised in Exeter so being part of Radio 1's Big Weekend this year means a huge amount to me personally.

    "I lived the first few years of my life less than 500 yards from where we'll be performing so this couldn't be more of a homecoming for me."
