Apple AirPods Max are the newest deluxe beauty from the Apple stable and they look and sound sensational. But there are now reports that users are experiencing battery drain, as spotted by 9to5Mac. Here’s what we know.

ForbesAirPods Max & AirPods Pro May Be About To Get Even BetterBy David Phelan

Overnight drops are steep in some cases

One user at MacRumors forums says their AirPods Max had 85% charge at night, but only 5% in the morning, despite being safely stored in the headphones case.

Though reports are growing, it’s still not a common experience, it seems

I should say that my review sample is showing no sign of having any battery issues at all and they have been used a lot. The cans have strong battery life and keep their charge pretty consistently, whether or not I remember to put them in the headphones case.

That case, as you’ll know, is designed to switch the AirPods Max to Ultra Low Power mode, so that the level of drain is very minimal. In fact, even if I leave them out of the case overnight, the charge level is pretty much identical the next day. So, it’s not every pair.

And there are conflicting reports

Another user found that the charge was fine, it was the displayed charge that was confusing. What does that mean? Open Bluetooth on a MacBook, say, and the list of devices, if it includes AirPods Max, will show the battery life. This is helpful, however, if the headphones are not nearby or in the case or in some other situation, the displayed figure can be the last recorded one. So, an earlier low figure could fool you into thinking the battery on board is low, but actually picking up the headphones and connecting to the MacBook will see the figure jump back to its real level.

Why not just turn them off?

There’s no power button on AirPods Max – just as there isn’t on AirPods or AirPods Pro. Apple wants you to just pick them up and they’re ready to go. As such, they move to Low Power Mode automatically after a period of not being use, but are still ready to connect the moment you pick them up. Apple has designed them so that the battery won’t drain fast, whatever you do with them.

Is it the firmware?

It’s not clear, though this seems the most likely culprit. There has been a firmware upgrade in recent weeks, on December 18 and it seems that many of the reports stem from after that date. To check if you have the latest firmware, connect your AirPods Max to your iPhone, go to Settings, About and AirPods Max. It’ll then show the firmware on board. Latest is 3C16, previous version was 3B71.

AirPods (of any stripe) update their firmware automatically, you don’t have to action it.

Dutch website AppleTips says the problem isn’t occurring with headphones connected to an iPhone but rather to a non-Apple device or to Apple TV.

So, what are the solutions?

This isn’t clear yet. A force restart by pressing and holding the Digital Crown and button has done the trick for some, as has restoring the headphones to factory settings. A manual disconnect via Bluetooth has helped some, or turning off the super-neat feature of auto-switching from one device to another.

One thing’s for sure, Apple will be watching, listening and investigating. If it seems to be anywhere near widespread, a firmware update will follow.
