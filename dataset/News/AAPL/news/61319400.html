Apple’s New Thread Could Level Out The Smart Home Playing Field

The new HomePod Mini, which was announced by Apple last month and is on sale now, packs in a Thread radio, the first smart speaker to do so... sort of.

I say sort of because the Google Nest Hub Max actually has the low power mesh technology built in, but it’s not been put to use yet.

And that means that Apple has stolen a march on its rivals with regards to getting Thread-enabled smart home devices working within its ecosystem; Nanoleaf and Eve are the first brands out of the gate in terms of adding Thread connectivity to its HomeKit devices.

However, while Apple might be leading the pack at the moment, Nanoleaf CEO Gimmy Chu believes that Thread, and the big Project CHIP movement it is part of, could allow the likes of Amazon and Google to catch up with Apple’s smart home platform.

“When everything is local, and HomeKit has actually done a really good job of that, and it's built into every smartphone, every MacBook, every iPad… people undervalue that,” the Nanoleaf founder told smart home site The Ambient.

"In terms of voice assistants, Google and Alexa are definitely kicking Apple’s butt. But, when it comes to the local smart home, Apple is currently winning.

"But when CHIP comes out, they lose their advantage. I think Apple loses the most. With the introduction of CHIP, it just allows everyone to catch up quite quickly."

Launched back in December 2019, Project Connected Home over IP (CHIP) was launched within the Zigbee Alliance, with the goal of enabling plug-and-play consumer IoT devices. It’s led by the likes of Amazon, Apple, Google and Samsung SmartThings.

Thread is set to play a key role for the project and is a big part of the first specification release of CHIP.

According to The Thread Group...

“Thread solves the complexities of the IoT, addressing challenges such as interoperability, security, power, and architecture requirements. It is a low-power wireless mesh networking protocol based on the universally supported Internet Protocol (IP), and built using open and proven standards. Thread networks have no single point of failure and include the ability to self-heal. They are simple to set up and use and auto-reconfigure when a device is added or removed. And Thread ensures end-to-end communication — device-to-device, device-to-mobile, and device-to-cloud — reliably and securely connecting hundreds, and even thousands, of products.”

Thread is a great fit for CHIP as it supports an array of IP app layers and can actually be enabled on existing hardware via a firmware update.

So while Apple has clearly backed a winner, it might not be flying solo for long.
