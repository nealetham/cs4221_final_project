Plymouth cats and dogs home left without heating and hot water

Gables Cats and Dogs Home is experiencing unexpected difficulties amid the coronavirus pandemic

    Cats and dogs in Plymouth are struggling with no heating and hot water, after the commercial boiler at one of the city's biggest pet homes and rehoming centres broke down.

    Staff at Gables Dogs and Cats Home are having to use heat pads and plug-in radiators to try and keep their 150 unwanted and abandoned cats and dogs warm - but admit this is only a short-term solution.

    Management say the boiler "broke down beyond repair" under the strain of the new extensive hygiene practices brought in to combat COVID-19, such as more frequent hand-washing and washing hands for at least 20 seconds.

    While this is essential advice, it has led to a disaster at Gables, staff say.

    The cost of replacing the broken commercial boiler is £4,700 so the animal home is already starting to suffer financially, while events to raise much-needed funds for the centre have had to be cancelled amid the coronavirus pandemic.

    Plus, people who help the centre out by "boarding" cats and dogs, ie. looking after the pets temporarily, have been cancelling their bookings.

    The home is urging the people of Plymouth to support the pets and donate anything they can.

    Deputy manager Ruth Rickard said: "We know times are hard for everyone at the moment and this breakdown couldn't have come at a worse time.

    "It is such a large amount of money to have to find so we are asking our wonderful supporters to donate just a pound or two so we can reach our goal. It doesn't have to be a huge donation to make a huge difference."

    If you can help by making a contribution to this campaign, labelled "Boiler Bummer," call 01752 331602 or click hereto donate online.

    If Gables is lucky enough to raise more money than is needed, funds will go towards helping the animals in their care, staff say.
