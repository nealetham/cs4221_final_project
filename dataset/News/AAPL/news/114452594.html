Here's what Wall St. experts are saying about these media names ahead of results
Fly Intel
Jessica de Sa-Mota - email
Goldman Sachs analyst Brett Feldman cut Paramount to Sell on macro headwinds

Comcast (CMCSA, CMCSK) is scheduled to announce quarterly results on July 28, while Warner Bros. Discovery (WBD) and Paramount (PARA) are expected to report earnings on August 4. What to watch for:

PESSIMISM ON PARAMOUNT:On Tuesday, Goldman Sachs analyst Brett Feldman double-downgraded Paramount to Sell from Buy with a price target of $20, down from $37, which represents 21% downside potential. Macro headwinds are likely to intensify pressures on Paramount's EBITDA and free cash flow through at least 2023, which increases the risk that the company will be able to execute its streaming strategy in a market that is already seeing intensified competition, Feldman told investors in a research note. The analyst believes the market is unlikely to underwrite the company's long-term potential in the shares as the near-term operating environment becomes more difficult. With Paramount trading at a premium to its media peers, Feldman sees "limited near-term valuation support."

Last week, Morgan Stanley analyst Benjamin Swinburne also downgraded Paramount to Underweight from Equal Weight with a price target of $22, down from $32. The pivot to streaming has not reduced the risk to media estimates from a slowing economy, Swinburne argued. Advertisers and consumers are likely to pull back in a recession at a time when some media these stocks have outperformed the market year-to-date thanks to already low multiples, the analyst added. He is "incrementally cautious" on the TV/streaming group saying the pivot to streaming "is about to get more difficult." Swinburne sees rising churn risk from consumers trimming their streaming portfolios in a more difficult economic environment. In addition, Apple (AAPL), Amazon (AMZN) and Netflix (NFLX) "appear to be increasingly eager to peel away" major U.S. sports from the pay-TV bundle, the analyst contended.

OPTIMISM ON WARNER BROS. DISCOVERY:On Tuesday, Goldman Sachs analyst Brett Feldman reinstated coverage of Warner Bros. Discovery with a Buy rating and $22 price target, which represents 50% upside potential. The merger of Discovery and WarnerMedia positions Warner Bros. to achieve "material scale as a global streamer while also fortifying its linear networks business and driving significant cost synergies," Feldman told investors in a research note. The analyst believes merger integration risks and cyclical pressures on its linear advertising revenue are more than fully reflected in the stock at current levels.

TARGET CUTS AHEAD OF RESULTS:On Wednesday, Evercore ISI analyst Vijay Jayant lowered the firm's price target on Paramount and Warner Bros. Discovery to $28 and $25, from $36 and $40, respectively, as he takes "a first cut" at updating his estimates for a macroeconomic slowdown. In legacy media. Jayant said he continues to favor Disney (DIS) and Warner Bros. Discovery, citing the former's "credible streaming strategy" and the latter's "synergized growth story." He called out Paramount and Fox Corp. (FOXA) as the companies he covers with estimates that are most sensitive to changes in advertising revenue and the pace of Pay TV industry subscriber declines. Jayant kept an In Line rating on Paramount shares and an Outperform on Warner Bros. Discovery.
SOLID GROWTH POST LOCKDOWN: Earlier this month, Daiwa analyst Jonathan Kees initiated coverage of Comcast with an Outperform rating and $43 price target as he believed media names "should continue to deliver solid growth post lockdown." The analyst told investors that he likes Comcast's business diversification, which he says can generate free cash flow and dividends "no matter the economic environment."
