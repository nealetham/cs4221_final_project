5G phone masts measuring 20 metres tall could be installed in Wigston

Vodafone have made inquiries about two sites in the town

    Requests for permission to put up two 5G phone masts in Wigston have been made to the council.

    Vodafone will operate the two masts - that will both measure 20m - and will be sited on Leicester Road near to the Sainsburys Local at the old Royal Oak pub, and another on the industrial estate near to the Tesco and Lidl supermarkets near the old Premier Drum.

    A letter included with both applications state that masts are already in place at both sites. The paperwork explains that Vodafone cant use the existing masts because of the way 5G technology works so must install new masts to support the expansion of the network.

    A statement that makes up part of the Leicester Road application states: The pavement on the western side of Leicester Road at the junction with Granville Road hosts an existing streetworks telecommunications installation, providing vital local coverage and capacity in this busy part of Wigston. There is now a requirement to upgrade the site to provide improved coverage and capacity, most notably in relation to 5G services.

    It says that the site has been carefully selected, set against a backdrop of commercial development being in front of a car washing facility and a local convenience store.

    The application also says that the site is in a position that can provide necessary coverage whilst being situated as far away as possible from residential properties.

    Five other sites were explored as options to site new masts but were discounted for different reasons.

    A statement that makes up part of the Magna Road application, says: The proposed upgrade site has been carefully selected in a position surrounded by commercial development in the heart of an industrial estate.

    The site benefits from the masking effects associated with the rooflines of nearby industrial units.

    A community information sheet included with both applications reads: As 5G technology is deployed across the country more and more services will become available and our lifestyles, the economy and even the way we commute will be transformed.
