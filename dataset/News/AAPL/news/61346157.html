iPhone 12 discounts and deals: Where to order iPhone 12 and iPhone 12 Pro

You might just get a free phone out of this.

ByGianni JaccomaUpdated Oct 28, 2020 10:26 a.m.

Smell that in the air? No, not the smoldering remains of our national dignity; it’s the smell of iPhone season!

Apple has four different flavors of its flagship handset to choose from this year. iPhone 12 and 12 Pro were released on Oct. 23, 2020, while iPhone 12 Pro Max (the larger version) and iPhone 12 mini (the smaller one) will be available for preorder on Nov. 6 with a release date of Nov. 13.

Depending on where and how you buy your iPhone 12, you’ll get varying degrees of discounts and flexibility. For example, if you buy direct from Apple you can get up to $500 off by trading in an eligible iPhone, and you can purchase an unlocked version that can be used on any carrier. If you buy from AT&T, you’ll get up to $800 off for a trade-in, but you’ll be locked in with AT&T.

It’s worth noting the fine print, here: All of the carrier trade-in deals are provided in the form of monthly bill credits, and are contingent on maintaining a line of service for the duration (generally 30+ months) of those credits. If you cancel your contract, and your trade-in doesn’t quite cover the cost of the phone, you’ll be on the hook for the remainder of that balance.

Unsure which option is right for you? Check out the best discounts below, along with the price differences for each phone.

T-Mobile

T-Mobile currently offers the highest savings potential, giving you up to $850 in credits for an eligible phone trade-in when you start a new line of service and purchase an iPhone 12 or 12 Pro. Note, however, that these phones are currently on backorder and won’t ship before Nov. 17.

Sprint

Sprint is offering up to $829.99 in credits for eligible phone trade-ins when you add a new line of service and purchase an iPhone 12 or iPhone 12 Pro.

You can also sign up for an 18-month flex lease, which would give you the option to trade in and upgrade to the inevitable next iPhone model at month 18.

AT&T

AT&T is offering up to $800 in credits if you buy a new iPhone 12 and trade in an older eligible phone, provided you either port in a line from another carrier or upgrade an existing line. Note that the deal requires you to activate an unlimited data plan.

Eligible trade-ins include iPhones as old as the iPhone 6s, along with select devices from brands like Samsung, Google and LG.

Verizon
Verizon is offering up to $550 in credits for iPhone 12 models when you start a new Unlimited plan and trade in an eligible device. If you’re an existing customer, you can get up to $440 off when you trade in an eligible device and upgrade your current line of service, and there are also options to get $100 off an Apple Watch or $150 off an iPad when you purchase iPhone 12.

Apple

Apple is offering up to $500 in credits for used iPhones through its trade-in program, as well as up to $360 for eligible Samsung models and $265 for Google Pixel phones. You might think the makers of the iPhone would have the best trade-in deals, but this is Apple we’re talking about.

The main upside with buying direct from Apple, though, is the ability to buy an unlocked phone that can be moved to whatever carrier you choose.

Best Buy

Defying its name, Best Buy is offering up to $350 in credits for eligible phone trade-ins. You also don’t get the option to purchase an unlocked iPhone 12, meaning you’ll have to get one of the carrier-locked models, and at that point you’d get a better trade-in deal going directly to the carriers themselves. Still, if you’ve got a Best Buy rewards account with a bunch of points to spend, this could be worth it for you.
