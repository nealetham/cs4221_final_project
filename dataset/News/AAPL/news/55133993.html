Apple

Founded on April 1, 1976 by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne, Apple is one of the world’s largest multinational technological companies, and among the most iconic. Its best known devices are Apple iPhone smartphones, Mac range of computers, iPod media player and iPad tablets.

Apart from selling these consumer products, Apple offers online services (iCLoud, iBooks, iTunes Store and App Store), consumer software (OS X, iOS, iTunes Media browser) and productivity suits (iLife and iWork).

Apple is known worldwide for its brand loyalty. Fortune magazine named Apple as the most admired company in US in 2008.

As of 2014, Apple has 72,800 permanent full-time employees, 437 retail stores in fifteen countries, and operates the online Apple Store and iTunes Store.

Financially, Apple's five-year growth average is 39%. In 2012, Apple broke Microsoft’s record and became the highest nominal market capitalization, ever reached by a publicly traded company since 1999.

In 2014, Apple became the first US company to be valued at over $700 billion. It has been variously ranked as the best brand and most valued company from time to time.

In August 2011, Jobs resigned as CEO due to health complications, and Tim Cook became the new CEO. Two months later, Jobs died of pancreatic neuroendocrine tumor.

The company employs 116,000 full-time employees as of October 2016 and maintains 498 retail stores in 22 countries as of July 2017.
To celebrate its 10th anniversary, Apple unveiled its anniversary smartphone, the iPhone X. With a brand new design that includes an edge-to-edge screen, it's the biggest change to the iPhone Apple has made in years. It also launched iPhone 8 and iPhone 8 Plus, the successor of its existing smartphone iPhone 7.
