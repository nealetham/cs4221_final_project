July 13 and July 14 updates below. This post was first published on July 12, 2020.

The Apple Watch is about to get its first-ever public beta software, so Watch users will be able to try out new features such as Sleep Tracking long before the final software is available. Previously, there have been early beta releases for everyone to try on their iPhone, iPad, Mac and Apple TV. But Apple’s other operating system, watchOS, has always had to wait until the final software launches in the Fall. Until now.

July 14 update. Apple batteries are in the news just now, not least because Apple is paying out money to owners of certain iPhones as part of a legal settlement concerning previously throttled older batteries, in what’s come to be known asBatterygate.

But we’re talking about the Apple Watch here, as you know, and watchOS 7 has an app incoming which will tell you how the battery in your Watch is doing. Jason Cipriani has a useful guide to how this will work over atCNet. Here’s my take on it.

When the new software is here, you open Settings on the Watch, and then find the entry for Battery. This shows you details of when the battery was last charged and to what percentage. This is something that will provide interesting data when you’re using Sleep Tracking features which may require you to charge the Watch on a different routine from before.

Tap Battery and you’ll come to Battery Health, which offers an explanation that all batteries degrade over time, followed by an analysis of how your particular battery is doing. Ideally, you’re looking for something that says Maximum Capacity 100%, but after a year or two you may have to settle for a little less.

To make the most of the battery life, you’ll then need to turn to the next element on the screen: Optimized Battery Charging. With this turned on, the Watch can learn your routines and know to only charge the battery to 80% at first, and only top up to 100% just in time for you to use the Watch again.

July 13 update. The Workout app is one of the most popular on Apple Watch. The changes the Workout and Activity apps will undergo when Force Touch vanishes are detailed below. But exactly how much effort went into updating the app for watchOS 7 is becoming clearer thanks to an in-depth interview with Apple’s Julz Arney, an executive who demonstrated the Dance setting in the Workout app during the WWDC keynote. Arney, talking toThe Hindustan Times, revealed how the new Dance activity was added to the Workout app and how it’s tuned to recognize four dance styles, Bollywood, cardio dance, hip-hop and Latin.

"Creating an algorithm to give accurate calorie credit for dance was a real challenge for a few reasons,” Arney said in the interview.

She also explained the challenge Apple faced:“Movements in dance aren’t always repetitive or predictable like they are in walking and running, so what the sensors can detect strictly from the wrist is not necessarily the whole story.

Suddenly, the Watch needs to work out how the whole body is moving just from your wrist movement and heart rate.

There’s a gyroscope as well as an accelerometer in the Apple Watch, so monitoring with these sensors reveal your movements more clearly. The interview is interesting and well worth a read.

It all chimes with my understanding of the app from my own conversations with Apple and, I suspect, you may not have read about the following details either.

First, although the dance sessions in Workout were validated using the four styles above, while you’re dancing it won’t know which kind of dancing you’re actually doing. Instead it will give you credit for dancing, whatever style is your penchant. In other words: don’t worry if your dancing is terrible, you’ll get the same credit in the Activity app as if you were the greatest dancer.

One final thing about the Dance setting that you may not have heard. Some workouts are recognized automatically by the Watch, such as an outdoor run or walk. Although the app will give you a reminder to check when you’ve finished, it’s up to you to start the workout manually.

So, should you update to the beta if you can, and are there any things you need to know?

‘Things Nobody Has Told You...' is my occasional column touching on tangential, but interesting, details that have been lost or under-reported in the news. Sometimes they are ground-breakers, sometimes pieces of trivia that will enliven a dinner party conversation…

The next software for Apple Watch has a bunch of cool, new features, from sleep tracking to a clever app that launches automatically when it senses you’re washing your hands. There are advanced features for Maps including cycling directions and more. You can read about seven of the best innovations here.

1. Not your main device and you need iOS 14, too.

Apple always specifies with the iOS public beta that the software is not final and may have bugs so it shouldn’t be installed on your main iPhone, the one on which you rely. Not everyone heeds this advice, but it’s true that on the iPhone you’d often find incompatibilities with third-party apps.

But one complication with this public beta that not everybody has realized is that before you can update the Watch to watchOS 7, you must also install iOS 14 on the iPhone to which it is a companion device. Could this mean you need multiple Apple Watch models as well as another iPhone?

Note that watchOS 7 is only compatible with Apple Watch Series 3, Apple Watch Series 4 and the latest Apple Watch Series 5, so it’s possible you may not have a compatible old Apple Watch knocking around.

2. Goodbye, Force Touch and what it means

I’ll be honest, I’m sad to see this go. Force Touch, where a heavier press on the display invokes a secondary menu, is no more in watchOS 7.

Workout and Activity are two of the most popular apps on the Watch, and they work in tandem. You do the exercise using Workout, whether that’s running, cycling, yoga or the upcoming Dance option. Then you turn to the Activity app to see how much credit you’ve earned for your endeavors.

Until now, Force Touch has meant that when you force-press on the display when the Activity app is live, you get two options, to display your weekly activity summary or change your move goal. In watchOS 7, the same mechanic has no effect.

What’s more, in one instance outside Workout or Activity, it yields a quite different result. That’s in the home screen view where the apps reside.

Apps on the Watch can either be seen as a constellation of circular icons or an alphabetical list. To switch from Grid to List view, a Force press was needed. Now that the pressure response has been turned off, a heavy press is read as a regular press. Hold down your finger on the Grid view for too long, however forcibly, and it’ll be understood as a long-press. In every version of watchOS, in Grid view this makes the apps jiggle so you can move or delete them.

In order to switch views of the apps, you now need to open the Settings app, choose App View and pick the option you want.

Back to the Activity app. If you want to change your Move goal in the Activity app, you must scroll down to the very bottom of the app’s information and there you’ll find both Weekly Summary and Change Move Goal buttons. You can perform the same change using the App View entry in the My Watch section of the companion iPhone’s Watch app.

If you do install watchOS 7, be aware of this change as, at first, it’s a little confusing.

3. Wake up to birdsong? It’s real birds, recorded on an iPhone

Seriously. I know Apple is famous for attention to detail but the new watchOS includes some extra alarm sounds, found in the Alarm section of the Clock app on the iPhone. Apple told me that the alarm sounds are designed its alarms so they’re not disturbing, gentle enough to wake you up without a sudden jarring feeling. Instead, they build so that after a while they do feel more like an alarm. In other words, they will wake you up.

But, at first, they’ll sound gentle and agreeable. One of the new alarms is Bird Song and the reason it sounds realistic is that it is. The bird songs were recorded from real nature. This required an Apple engineer getting up at five o’clock in the morning and going outside with their iPhone. The bird songs were recorded on the coast.

Only Apple.

This was a part of the sound capture and studies the company did into how to create a successful audible wake-up experience. As you can imagine, the company also looked into ensuring the Taptic Engine was tuned to deliver a gradually increasing tactile experience if you want to wake to a silent alarm.
