App shares which supermarkets have no queues and what items they have in stock

Motorsport web developer Paul Hulatt came up with the Supermarket Check-In app after being furloughed – he used his own software to build the app from his bedroom

    A cutting-edge app will help Brits beat supermarket queues by revealing the best time to visit – with real-time updates about what items are in stock.

    Supermarket Check-In was built by furloughed web developer Paul Hulatt to “relieve the headache” of shopping in the coronavirus crisis.

    Brainy Paul used his own software to build the app from his bedroom.

    He claims it will rank supermarkets by queue length and stock availability using data supplied by shoppers.

    Users are able to log their visit after shopping to update others.

    Motorsport programmer Paul, from Kent, said: "Given all the issues there have been with supermarket queues and stock availability, I wanted to help.

    "After being placed on furlough, I decided to use my free time to keep my development skills up to date - and 'Supermarket Check-In' is the result!"

    He boasted that none of the crowdsourced information in his app is publicly available.

    He added: "This kind of socially-generated content is a really great way of communities coming together and helping each other get through this crisis.

    "None of this information is publicly available elsewhere so I came up with the idea of creating a way for people to easily 'check-in' to their local supermarket after doing a shop so that others in their local community could benefit.

    "This will not only help you shop at a quieter time, but also minimise the time spent shopping by helping you choose a time when the products you need are most likely to be in stock."

    The app shows a list of all nearby supermarkets within a three, five, 10, or 25 mile range.

    It also tells you when the last check-incas recorded so you know the latest data is accurate.

    It is available on Apple’s app store and Google Play.
