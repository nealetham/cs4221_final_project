    What’s common to US, Indian equities? Returns, polarisation and valuations

    Synopsis

    India’s equity market capitalisation is just 6 per cent of the US, the world’s largest equity market.

    India’s equity market capitalisation is just 6 per cent of the US, the world’s largest equity market. Despite the huge difference, the US and Indian equity markets are moving in a similar trajectory in terms of the performance, polarisation and valuation. Benchmarks in each of the two markets are notching fresh records while analysts are cagey due to their disconnect with macro indicators.

    The US market trades at the richest valuation since the internet bubble of 2000; while Indian benchmarks have a forward price-earnings (P/E) multiple of nearly 19 making them one of the most expensive among the top global markets. On the other hand, corporate earnings in the US increased by just 1.5 per cent in 2019 and are expected to fall for the second quarter in a row. On the domestic side, the Nifty earnings grew by just 3 per cent annually in the past five years.


    For the next two years, analysts expect earnings growth of 9 per cent and 10 per cent for US equities, while more than 20 per cent for the Indian market. The elevated P/E in times of wobbly economic conditions reflects the market’s hope of better growth in the future. In addition, each of the markets are driven by a rally in large caps. The market cap of the top five US companies accounts for 18 per cent of the benchmark’s capitalisation. The large-cap stocks rose the most compared with the small-caps in the US in a decade.

    Similarly, the contribution of the ‘A’ share in the total BSE capitalisation reached to nearly two-decade high in India. The contribution of the top 10 stocks in the Nifty 50, weight at 60.6 per cent, is at a record high. Apple, the largest US company by market value, contributed 22.7 per cent to the total gains of the Dow Jones Industrial Average, while its Indian counterpart RIL contributed 23 per cent to the Sensex’s gain in the past 12 months.
