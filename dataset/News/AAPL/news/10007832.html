Donald Trump to extend European flight ban from UK and Ireland to America

The president held a press conference on Saturday at the White House

    United States president Donald Trump said a European travel ban will be extended to cover the UK and Ireland and officials said it would come into effect at midnight on Monday (Eastern Standard Time).

    The US president had originally excluded Britain and Ireland from its 30-day ban on travellers from 26 continental European countries that took effect from midnight on Friday.

    Asked about reports that the travel ban will be extended to the UK and Ireland at a press conference on Saturday, he said: "We're looking at it very seriously, yeah, because they've had a little bit of activity unfortunately, so we're going to be looking at that.

    "We've actually already looked at it and that is going to be announced."

    It comes as more than 1,140 people in the UK tested positive for coronavirus and 21 people have died of coronavirus.

    President Trump told the White House on Saturday that 50 people had died in the US of coronavirus.

    He said that American citizens can get free coronavirus tests "so people don't have to worry about that."

    Mr Trump said he had been tested for coronavirus, but he did not know the results of the test yet.

    He declared a national emergency on Friday.

    He said there will be paid medical leave for people who are ill and we are "using the full power of the federal government to combat coronavirus."

    President Trump said masks are being made "by the millions and millions."

    "We always say worst case scenario - that's where we are going," he said at the press conference.

    "We're in this together it's something that nobody expected.

    "It came out of China. We hope we solve this problem and solve it well. I closed up our country to China.

    "The American people have been incredible if you look at people and sports leagues.

    "For the most part, they want to get it over with and they want to get it over with quickly with as few deaths.

    "As of this moment we have 50 deaths. You know that Europe is declared a hotspot yesterday."

    He praised Apple for closing its stores and he said it was "good" because they were "keeping people away."

    "We want this thing to end and as quickly as possible," he added, as he defended his decision to shake hands with people.
