Apple’s ‘Hi, Speed’ iPhone 12 virtual event takes place on October 13, 2020 at 10 a.m. Pacific Daylight Time. That’s 1 p.m. on the East coast. Apple has penciled in two hours for the event, but it did the same with September’s ‘Time Flies’ event, even though that one only lasted an hour or less. As a result, the length is not necessarily indication of what to expect or how many products will be announced. Speaking of which, there have been plenty of rumors over the last few weeks regarding updates to the company's iPhone and HomePod lines, as well as suggestions of new products like AirTags and MagSafe chargers.

Apple typically holds an end of year event to announce the newest iPhone and usually a few other product updates as well. Due to the global pandemic, this year’s event has been split into two and possibly three separate events taking place several weeks apart, likely to allow for additional production time. Everything has slowed including sourcing components, production and shipping. Despite all of this, Apple has already launched two new iPad models and two new smartwatches. The iPad Air 4 is a bit of a marvel, rivaling the iPad Pro models at a much lower price. The iPad 8 was an incremental change, but with significantly more power than the previous generation budget iPad. The Apple Watch Series 6 saw small but satisfying improvements and a $279 Apple Watch SE was announced, making Apple's take on a smartwatch available to even more people.

With the iPhone 12 still coming and the promise of an Apple Silicon Mac computer this year, it is shaping up to be one of the greatest years ever for Apple announcements. The event will be available to view on October 13 via Apple’s website and on YouTube. In the meantime, there have been plenty of rumors and speculation on what to expect, including four new iPhone 12 models, the HomePod mini, wireless chargers, ultra-wideband location beacons, and Apple-branded headphones.

iPhone 12 mini, Pro & Pro Max Rumors

✕ Remove Ads

The iPhone 12 is clearly going to be the event's main attraction and many details have already leaked, including sizes, prices and colors. Even camera details, 5G capability and screen specifics. Much of what was rumored from months ago remains consistent with the latest information lending further credence. The latest information was posted to Weibo by Kang, further confirming there will be four iPhone 12 models coming at Tuesday’s event. All are slated to have full 5G support, with both the faster mmWave and the wider-reaching Sub-6 supported.

While there was some mixed information about this in the past, the consensus now is that both forms of 5G will be supported. All four models are said to feature OLED display, but he rumored 120Hz refresh rate seems to have been delayed for a future iPhone. TrueTone displays combined with the bright and colorful HDR power of OLED will likely satisfy most users. Images shown are conceptual renders as no completed iPhone 12 photos have leaked so far. This render of the iPhone 12 Pro is courtesy of The Apple Post.

iPhone 12 Sizes, Prices & Colors

The render of the iPhone 12 mini above with early color guesses was provided by svetapple.sk. Actual colors are described by Kang as black, white, red, blue and green. The Pro models are to come in gold, silver, graphite and blue. The iPhone 12 is expected to arrive in four sizes, one at 5.4-inches, two at 6.1-inches and the largest at 6.7-inches. The iPhone 12 mini should sell for $699, though some suggest $649, while the iPhone 12 will start at $799.

✕ Remove Ads

These standard models will come with 64-gigabytes, with up to 256-gigabytes optional. The iPhone 12 Pro will match the 6.1-inch size of the iPhone 12, but feature 128-gigabytes of storage, an extra telephoto camera and LiDAR for $999. The iPhone 12 Pro Max is expected to start at $1099. For the extra $100, Kang suggests the top model will add a 47-percent larger image sensor, a longer telephoto and the biggest iPhone display ever.

HomePod mini

The HomePod is a well loved smart speaker with incredible sound quality. One of the best features of the HomePod is its ability to continuously optimize sound, according to its environment. The closest competitor is Google Home Max, which has a very similar ability and equally high price. Once again the details are from Kang’s Weibo post. The HomePod mini price should be a much more affordable $99 versus the $299 cost of the full size HomePod. Rumored to be one third the size it may have a 3.3-inch diameter. No mention has been made of sound quality or speaker details, though some concessions will likely have to be made to accommodate the lower price.

✕ Remove Ads

It was only a matter of time before Apple would feel compelled to compete in the budget smart speaker space before too much of the market is captured by rival brands. This follows Apple’s trend of several recent budget priced options including the iPhone SE, Apple Watch SE and the two new low-price but powerful iPad models. All of these cost-saving Apple devices have been released this year and so it would make perfect sense to add a less expensive HomePod to the lineup as well. This HomePod mini conceptual render was created by MacRumors.

MagSafe Wireless Chargers

✕ Remove Ads

Another possible product that could be on show at the event is a wireless charger that uses magnets to align devices for fastest charging. The leak from Kang referred to this product with the name MagSafe. An older Apple product, called MagSafe, was a magnetic power cord for a MacBook. Not wireless at all, the magnetic attachment prevented accidental damage to the notebook caused from tripping over the power cord. The new MagSafe, may solve an issue plaguing many wireless chargers, requiring precise placement for best charging. Apple’s AirPower intended to allow charging anywhere on a mat, but was rumored to use several overlapping coils and never came to market. If the MagSafe charger is real, it would gently guide the user to optimal placement through the use of magnets.

This elegant and intuitive approach aligns better with Apple's thinking than the brute force approach alleged with AirPower. MagSafe wireless charging is rumored to be 15-watts so not quite the 18-watts required for fast charging, but still better than the 5-watt wired chargers that are included with the iPhone 11. The MagSafe photo leak was provided by Jordan Kahn via Twitter.

AirTag Location Beacons: Delayed

The AirTag is a completely new category of product almost certain to be announced, just not at this event. While AirTags were originally expected to be announced alongside the iPhone 12, the latest rumors suggest the product has now been delayed. The AirTag is rumored to be a new kind of location beacon that uses ultra-wideband technology rather than Bluetooth, resulting in much great accuracy, as well as directional and range information as well.

The potential of AirTags has yet to be fully explored, as it could serve AR and fitness tracking purposes, in addition to the more mundane ability to locate lost or misplaced items. Jon Prosser claimed to have seen a video of the actual product and then commissioned renders based on the design that had been seen, such as the image above.

AirPods Studio Over-The-Ear Headphones: Delayed

Another new type of product from Apple, AirPods Studio are over-the-ear wireless headphones. Of course, Apple already makes the very popular AirPods wireless earbuds and also owns Beats, a company that specializes in headphones. However, the company is also rumored to be working on a new set of Studio headphones with indications pointing to two models, priced at $350 and $599. Jon Prosser, the same source for the AirTag delay, shared the pricing information along with the bad news that AirPods Studio are now not expected to make an appearance at Tuesday’s 'Hi, Speed' event.

✕ Remove Ads

Apple Silicon Mac

Apple promised an Apple Silicon powered Mac computer by the end of this year. This device will have the ability to run iPad apps natively as well as compatibility with macOS apps. For Apple fans that use both types of computers, this means a much smoother and easier transition between devices. Every iPad app purchase will be usable on an Apple Silicon Mac as well, without anything required from the developer. Mac apps will still be made and the best experience on a Mac will still come from macOS specific apps, but it will simply allow more freedom for the consumer and a wider user base for app developers. Leakers suggest the Apple Silicon Mac will not be seen at Tuesday’s event, but could be announced at a separate event in November.

✕ Remove Ads

To sum up, it should be quite an exciting Apple Event. Most agree that the name of the event, 'Hi, Speed,' is referring to both the much faster wireless network speeds possible with 5G and also the increased speed possible with the new A14 processor that's expected to be included in the new iPhone 12. The A14 was first announced in September and it is the same chip that powers the impressive performance of the iPad Air 4. It actually outperformed the more expensive iPad Pro models in some recent benchmarks, so having this much speed in a hand-held device should result in an impressive experience. Overall, it will be fun to see what is possible with the new iPhone 12 and learn about all the other new products that are expected to be showcased during Apple's October 13 'Hi, Speed' event.
