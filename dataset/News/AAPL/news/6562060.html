You can now spend the night in Harry Potter’s home just 90 minutes from London

Even if you're not a Potterhead, it's a lovely home in a dreamy village

    If you've already done the studio tour and had a photo at platform nine and three quarters, you can now immerse yourself in the magical world of Harry Potter even more.

    You can visit the scenic village that was Godric's Hollow and spend the night in the home that was where Harry Potter himself was born - and where his parents died at the hands of Lord Voldemort.

    Remember that wonky medieval house in Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows Part One? Now you can actually sleep there (minus the attack from He Who Must Not Be Named).

    De Vere House in Lavenham, Suffolk, is its actual name, and you can rent a room there on Airbnb. It used to be a Five Star and Gold Award bed and breakfast, so you can be sure you'll have a great stay.

    There are two bedrooms with four-poster beds available to book and the house has a bunch of pretty period features, including timber beams, wall paintings and a spiral stone staircase.

    But don't worry, it's not like stepping completely back in time - you can still get WiFi and there's a TV, plus you can enjoy your full English breakfast in the dining room, all for £110 per night.

    Lavenham is one of the best-preserved medieval villages in the UK and the Potters’ abode is one of 340 listed buildings in the area. You can visit the village's stunning medieval Church, tea rooms, museums and art gallery before picking from one of the several restaurants to refuel.

    Just a 90-minute drive from London, it's the perfect place for a weekend getaway - Potterhead or not.
