The 17 Leicester City players at risk of summer clear-out

Brendan Rodgers will want to streamline his squad ahead of next season with the Leicester City manager preferring to work with a smaller group

    Leicester City will have two jobs this summer.

    Not only will they have to add three or four high-class players to improve the squads quality and depth, particularly in the case of Champions League qualification, but they will also have to streamline.

    While there is usually a focus on bolstering the numbers ahead of a European campaign, with an already-hectic schedule reaching new levels of busyness, Rodgers wants just 25 senior players in his squad, which he will supplement with prospects from the development set-up.

    The City boss has always favoured a smaller squad, and said last summer: If youre trying to manage 26 or 27 outfield players, its not good for the training because within that you have five or six players that feel theyre too far away.

    My feeling is to work with a tight group, a small group, so everyone feels really connected. And then if they are out, they know theyre not too far away.

    So as Rodgers looks to clear out his squad, who is in danger of being cut? We take a look.

    LIKELY TO LEAVE

    Andy King

    After a 16-year association with the club, King is expected to finally head for an emotional exit this summer.

    Since promotion to the Premier League, the Wales international has slowly moved further and further away from the first team and has spent the past few years away on loan at Swansea, Derby, Rangers, and now Huddersfield.

    He will return from the Terriers for a farewell, but then he will be gone for good.

    Papy Mendy

    The Frenchman has played more often than many expected over the past two months, filling in at the base of midfield, but that is unlikely to increase the likelihood of a prolonged stay at the King Power Stadium.

    Mendy is still third-choice defensive midfielder behind Wilfred Ndidi and Hamza Choudhury, and, entering his prime years, Rodgers expects him to leave at the end of his contract this summer to find a club where he will be a regular.

    Hes probably pretty clear that hell look elsewhere, with the age that he is at, Rodgers said in January.

    Its about the age hes at, its about game-time and hes a very good player who in a lot of teams could play week in, week out. Hes getting to that age where he probably wants that.

    Islam Slimani

    City have been trying to offload their one-time record signing for a while now, and may finally do so this summer.

    This year, on loan at Monaco, hes having his most productive season since joining City for 30m, having failed to fit in at the King Power Stadium, and having struggled in loan spells at Newcastle and Fenerbahce.

    With eight goals and seven assists in Ligue 1, there are likely to be suitors for the Algerian, even if Monaco do not enact the buy clause in his loan contract.

    Adrien Silva

    Another misfit who never truly caught up with the pace of the Premier League, Silva also looks good for a permanent switch to Monaco, having won over new manager Robert Moreno to take the captain's armband.

    However, unlike Slimani, there is no buy clause in the Portugueses loan contract, so it may be a trickier transfer to sort.

    However, if Monaco turns their attention elsewhere, with only a year left to run on his City contract, Silva is quite likely to be picked up, with former club Sporting regularly linked.

    Fousseni Diabate

    Claude Puels first signing briefly looked to be a bargain buy after impressing in his first few months at the club.

    However, he quickly fell by the wayside and with City well-stacked for wingers, and possibly looking at more this summer, Diabate wont force his way back in, no matter how well he does at Amiens in the run-in.

    A permanent departure seems likely.

    Rachid Ghezzal

    Destined to struggle under the weight of Riyad Mahrez comparisons, Ghezzals City career never took off.

    Sidelined upon the arrival of Rodgers, his form on loan at Fiorentina will not have made the manager regret that decision.

    If City can offload Ghezzal this summer, they will.

    Bartosz Kapustka

    Despite receiving praise from Rodgers for his efforts and skill in training, Kapustka may have reached the end of the road at City.

    Excluded from the Premier League squad despite there being a space for him, and no longer a young prospect at 23, City are likely to offload the one-time wonderkid, who may be hoping to make a fresh start and get his career back on track.

    Sam Hughes

    Plucked from non-league Chester where he was the clubs youngest-ever captain, Hughes impressed in his first season at City before a back injury ruled him out for much of his second campaign.

    After a few appearances here and there on loan at Salford, Hughes is now back at City for what is likely to be his final few months at the club.

    Hes 22 and heading towards the end of his contract, and with no foreseeable route to the first team, he is set to be released.

    George Thomas

    The Wigston-born midfielder did a fine job for the Under-23s in the first half of this season, but it is unlikely to earn him a new contract when his current one runs out in the summer.

    Until then, he will try to help Alan Pardews ADO Den Haag avoid Eredivisie relegation.

    Ryan Loft

    A bit-part player on loan at League Two strugglers Carlisle this season, it would be a major surprise if Loft is offered a contract extension at the expiry of his deal this summer.

    COULD POSSIBLY LEAVE

    Eldin Jakupovic

    The third-choice stopper has not played a first-team game for more than 18 months and his contract is up at the end of the season.

    Its possible that Daniel Iversen or Viktor Johansson will step up to fill his gloves, but if the club prefer to get the young Scandinavians game-time out on loan, they may want to keep Jakupovic around, with Rodgers keen to have three senior keepers.

    Daniel Amartey

    A recent injury setback means Amartey hasnt played for City since breaking his ankle back in October 2018.

    During his time out of the side, he has been overtaken in the pecking order in both defensive midfield and at right-back, either by academy graduates or new signings, so a loan away at the least is expected for next season, provided he returns to fitness.

    Matty James

    He is back in the Premier League squad and has received heaps of praise from Rodgers, but James is still way down the pecking order in midfield.

    If he cannot re-establish himself as even a bit-part player between now and the end of the season, a move away could be on the cards this summer.

    DECISION TO BE MADE

    Wes Morgan

    The skippers City career looked to be winding down, but he was handed a one-year contract extension upon Rodgers arrival, and the boss is keen to keep Morgan for a further year.

    Morgan has said he feels fit to continue but has suffered a couple of injury setbacks over recent months that could affect contract talks.

    City will have to decide if they go through with the offer of a new deal.

    Christian Fuchs

    For Fuchs, the decision will be his. The Austrian left-back has greatly impressed Rodgers on and off the field and has proven to be a reliable back-up to Ben Chilwell.

    Still as fit as a fiddle at 33, an offer of a contract extension seems likely. But will Fuchs finally want to move to the US and join his family over there?

    AT RISK IF REPLACEMENT FOUND

    Marc Albrighton

    The title-winning winger has made just two Premier League starts this season, and with Rodgers keen for his wide-men to get regular goals and assists, Albrightons gradually declining output puts him at risk.

    But, that is only if City strengthen their side with another winger this summer. If not, Albrighton will keep his place in the squad.

    Demarai Gray

    Like Albrighton, Gray has only made two Premier League starts this term. However, he has been called upon more often from the bench.

    Despite improvements, there are still question marks over his decision-making and final ball, and if a new winger was signed, his age may make him easier to move on than Albrighton.
