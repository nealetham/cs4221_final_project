STO’s Jensen Lewis, former Tribe reliever, visits The Cleveland Baseball Talk Podcast

  • Published: Jun. 16, 2020, 7:01 p.m.
By
  • Paul Hoynes, cleveland.com

CLEVELAND, Ohio -- Will they play or not? That is the question and it has never been more murky than right now.

MLB Commissioner Rob Manfred said last week that he was 100% sure baseball would be played in this coronavirus-delayed season. On Tuesday he told ESPN he wasn’t certain that he would exercise his power to implement a season in the neighborhood of 50 games because the MLB Players Association could hit the owners with a big grievance.

To discuss the matter The Cleveland Baseball Talk Podcast invited Jensen Lewis to join cleveland.com’s Jamie Turner and Paul Hoynes. Lewis is a current broadcaster for SportsTime Ohio and a former Indians right-handed pitcher. He was a third-round pick of the Indians in 2005 and helped them win the AL Central and reach Game 7 of the ALCS in 2007 with his work out of the pen.

Topics discussed included

*The importance of a fast start in a 50-game season.

*The Indians tendency for lukewarm starts under manager Terry Francona.

*The possibility of the owners and players overcoming their rancor and playing some kind of season this year.

*Lewis, a Vanderbilt alum, applauding the Tribe pick of Vandy right-hander Mason Hickman with their fifth and final pick of last week’s draft.

*How last week’s draft -- shortened from 40 to five rounds -- puts amateur players in a bind.

*How the lack of a minor league season could hurt teams such as the Indians if the MLB season ever starts.
