9 things Reading commuters could buy for the price of an annual season ticket to London Paddington

getreading takes a look at what else you could get for £4,228

getreading
  • Bookmark
  • Share
News
By
Nathan John Hyde
  • 14:26, 9 AUG 2016
  • Updated08:38, 10 AUG 2016
  • Bookmark

    • If you're a regular on the morning Reading Station to London Paddington rail service it probably won't come as much of a surprise to hear that some passengers are quite annoyed.

    Commuters from Reading to Paddington shell out the staggering sum of £4,228 for an annual season ticket and many of them are less than impressed with the service.

    According to a survey carried out by consumer watchdog Which? Reading rail passengers are some of the most discontent in the country due to pricey tickets, seating shortages and poor punctuality.

    If you are one of those commuters who is fed up of forking out thousands for annual season tickets then you may want to see what else that money could get you.

    So before you resort to working from home and swap all your suits for a few pairs of pyjamas take a look at getreading 's list of season ticket alternatives.

    Olympic tickets

    If you act fast you could buy more than 10 tickets (£289.22 each) for top-quality seats to watch the finals of a number of eagerly anticipated track and field events on the penultimate day of the 2016 Rio Olympics.

    You'd also have enough money left to buy yourself a return flight to Rio (with British Airways) and a cab ride from the airport to the Olympic Stadium.

    A stay at the Ritz

    You could get three nights in the Deluxe Suite at the word-famous Ritz Hotel in London, and still have enough left for a three-course meal in the restaurant.

    Your stay would be complete with a bottle of complementary champagne, a full English breakfast each morning and the "pressing of one garment for each guest per stay".

    Dozens of European getaways

    Fancy a trip to Amsterdam? This week you could fly from Gatwick to the capital of the Netherlands and back for £83.98 (including tax) through EasyJet.com .

    That means you could pay for 50 return trips to Amsterdam and still have some spending money left.

    Cover Brexit losses

    Before the British people decided to leave the European Union the Centre for Economic Performance issued a warning.

    The think tank told voters Brexit would result in a long-term decline in UK trade and that would translate into a fall in GDP of at least 6.3 per cent, or £4,200 per household.

    Hire an island

    For two days you could have this picturesque 400 acre island in Scotland all to yourself. It includes a private beach, three cottages and its own restaraunt.

    Known as Sanda Island, the idyllic retreat is just 20 miles from Northern Ireland and a mile away from Mull of Kintyre.

    Overnight stays at one of the cottages are £185 but visitors can rent out the whole island for £2,000 a day.

    Rent

    Outside of London, the average UK rental value is £779 per month according to the latest HomeLet Rental Index.

    This means you could cover five months rent with your season ticket fare and still have £333 left.

    But if you decided to uproot to London that cash would not get you as far. The average rental value in the capital stood at £1,599 in July.

    A trip to the Apple store

    If your clawed back your season ticket cash you could treat yourself to an array of Apple gadgets.

    It could get you no less than four iMacs (£1,049 each) with 21.5 inch monitors and 2.8GHz processors or four iPad Pros (£1,019 each), each with the maximum amount of storage.

    Or you could choose to buy nine iPhone 6s (£459).

    Thousands of bus trips

    If you refrain from renewing your season ticket you can splash out on a 365-day Reading Buses pass (£830) which will allow you to travel on any route, any time.

    You would also have enough to buy another four of these passes for friends or family.

    A whole lot of coffee

    For many commuters a strong cup of coffee is the cornerstone of any breakfast and if you didn't have to pay for an annual season ticket you could by 2,168 large cups of filter coffee from Starbucks.
