Apple has now officially confirmed it will be hosting a 'Hi, Speed' event on October 13, 2020, and it is expected that the tech giant will reveal the new iPhone 12 at the event. While Apple seldom reveals any details about upcoming products, a big hint is usually provided with the title of the event.

✕ Remove Ads

Apple typically announces the newest iPhone models at its end of year event. This year is somewhat different than usual, due to the pandemic. Once held in front of a large audience at Apple Park, this year has seen two virtual events. The Worldwide Developers Conference was live-streamed in June, introducing iOS 14, and the 'Time Flies' event came just last month, with Apple announcing the iPad Air 4, iPad 8 and two new Apple Watch models, the Series 6 and the Apple Watch SE.

Related: How Apple Watch Is Helping Create The Next Wave Of iPhone Owners

Apple's announcement of its next  event came via the events page on its website and also via emails for those that subscribe to its mailing list. The contents had minimal information, leaving it to the imagination, but the title, 'Hi, Speed,' is a hint about the nature of the announcement. The date of the event is Tuesday, October 13 and the event is due to begin at 10:00 AM Pacific Daylight Time. Apple includes a button to add to the Apple Calendar app. And there is a note to watch the livestream on Apple's website. Of course, the event will also be available on Apple's YouTube channel as well.

✕ Remove Ads

What To Expect At Apple's Event

The graphic present on Apple's event page is an AR object. That means on AR-enabled devices, tapping the graphic will open the AR view and allow viewing of an animation within the user's real world space. The animation shows the orange globe opening and expanding into multiple smaller globes while the date 10.13 is revealed in the center. It's fun to play with this for a few seconds and see the shadows cast. With the previous 'Time Flies' event, a similar AR object was included, so everyone knew to check for the 'Easter Egg' this time around as well. The animation can also be seen on a white background as a standard 3D object, by tapping the 'Object' tab.

✕ Remove Ads

Apple will almost certainly introduce new iPhone models at the October special event. The title, 'Hi, Speed,' is likely referring to the new iPhone 12 featuring 5G network support. Most other smartphone manufacturers have already released 5G phones and Apple will surely now join those ranks. In total, there are 4 new iPhone 12 models expected and possibly a few completely new categories of products. Rumors of AirPods Studio, over the ear headphones with an Apple brand, have been circulating. Also, the long overdue AirPower wireless charger may see the light of day. Finally, a location beacon, called the AirTag may also be revealed at the next Apple event.
