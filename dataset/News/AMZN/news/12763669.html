Where Broadchurch cast are now - surprise big names who starred including Oscar winner

The star-studded Broadchurch cast featured two Doctor Who stars, a Harry Potter legend and Fleabag favourite Phoebe Waller Bridge

It's nearly over... Broadchurch comes to an end in tense finale
By
Kyle O'SullivanAssistant Features Editor
  • 21:00, 22 Jun 2020
  • |
  • comments

Broadchurch came to a dramatic conclusion in April 2017 - and the star-studded cast have been up to lots ever since.

The who-dunnit murder series kept us on the edge of our seats when it originally aired in 2013 as police searched for an an 11-year-old schoolboy's killer on the Dorset coach.

The award-winning crime drama was only meant to be a one-off series, but with nine million viewers tuning in each week it was extended for another two.

The second series revolved around a harrowing court case, while the third saw the detectives uncover a rapist.

Award-winning stars David Tennant and Olivia Colman played the detectives on the hunt for the child killer in a slow-burning series full of sensational twists and turns.

But a host of other big names starred across the three series, including the first female Doctor Who and BAFTA and Oscar winners.

David Tennant

Doctor Who star David Tennant played the crime-solving hero who arrived in Broadchurch hoping for a quiet life.

But DI Hardy was quickly thrown into leading the investigation into the murder of schoolboy Danny Latimer in series one.

Hardy struggled to deal with the murder because he was haunted by the failed Sandbrook murder case, which he took the blame for despite his wife being at fault.

The experienced detective was relieved from duty after suffering from heart problems, but secretly solved the Sandbrook case by locking up those he suspected were involved.

However he couldnt get justice for Danny as Joe Miller walked free despite confessing to killing the young boy.

After leaving Broadchurch in a taxi at the end of series two, Hardy was back three years later to once again team up with DS Miller to solve a chilling rape case.

The 10th Doctor portrayed villain Killgrave in Marvel series Jessica Jones on Netflix and recently starred in Good Omens.

David was be reunited with some of his former Broadchurch crew for true crime show Deadwater Fell, which aired in January this year, while also gaining his first executive producer credit.

Later this year he will be back on our screens as serial killer Dennis Nilsen in ITV series Des.

Olivia Colman

British superstar Olivia Colman was already a massive star in her own right when she joined the cast of Broadchurch.

She played DS Miller, the local detective who found it hard to suspect her close-knit community of murdering Danny Latimer in the first series, who was friends with her son Tom.

Under the guidance of Hardy she learnt not to trust anyone, but was distraught when she discovered her own husband Joe was the murderer.

The ultimate betrayal led Ellie to beat her beloved husband, which made his confession inadmissible and threw the trial into chaos.

She was demoted and transferred to Devon, but helped Hardy solve the Sandbrook case and rebuilt her reputation.

Joe got away with the murder but Ellie reconciled with the Latimer family and banished her husband from ever returning to Broadchurch.

The Peep Show star won a BAFTA award for Best Actress for her performance in Broadchuch in 2013.

She won a Golden Globe for her role in The Night Manager in 2016 and appeared in Fleabag with another star in this lsit.

Olivia's biggest awards success came last year when she won an Oscar for Best Actress for The Favourite.

Jodie Whittaker

Jodie played the grieving mother or murdered Danny in all three series of Broadchurch.

Beth was overwhelmed after the death of her son in series one and felt guilty for not checking him when she went to bed on the night of his murder.

She was close friends with DS Miller but their friendship broke down as she accused her of deliberately sabotaging her husband Joes confession by attacking him.

Beth discovered in front of the whole town that her husband Mark had planned to leave her before Danny was killed, which lead to the breakdown of their marriage.

She gave birth to a second daughter in series two and tried to move on with her life and help others through counselling in the third series.

In July 2017, it was announced that Jodie would become the 13th and very first female Doctor in British classic Doctor Who.

Doctor Who producer Chris Chibnall cast Jodie after working with her on Broadchurch.

After replacing Lewis Capaldi, she played the Doctor for two series and has signed up for a third.

Phoebe Waller-Bridge

Many people will have forgotten that Fleabag creator Phoebe Waller-Bridge starred in Broadchurch.

Back in 2015, Phoebe joined the cast of the second series as Abby Thompson, a junior barrister on murderer Joe Miller's defence team.

Joe surprised everyone, including Abby, by pleading not guilty to Danny's muder.

Is there anything that needs to be said about the amazing rise of Phoebe ever since?

She has won three Prime Time Emmys, two Golden Globes and a BAFTA for her writing and acting in Fleabag.

As well as being writer and executive producer on the first series of Killing Eve, Phoebe has voiced a robot in Star Wars: A Solo Story.

Last year she co-wrote the screenplay for upcoming James Bond movie No Time to Die.

Lenny Henry

Lenny Henry made a move away from comedy to star in the dark drama for series three.

His character Ed Burnett was arrested in relation to the rape of Trish after blue twine in his shop matched the twine used in the sexual assault.

The police discovered that Ed was madly in love with his employee but let him go.

Stand-up comedian Lenny went on to appear in an episode of Doctor Who alongside Jodie Whittaker.

The Comic Relief co-founder and presenter, who starred in Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban as a talking shrunken head, also hosted the BBC's The Big Night In to raise money amid the coronavirus pandemic this year.

Vicky McClure

Vicky McClure had already filmed her first series of Line of Duty when she swapped the police for journalism.

Her character, Karen White, was a reporter for national newspaper the Daily Herald and knew about DI Hardy's secret failure during the failed Sandbrook case.

Karen's desire to stop "polishing press releases" and become an investigative reporter brought her to Broadchurch on the hunt for the killer.

While her Broadchurch character failed to uncover the killer, Vicky has been taking down many criminals in Line of Duty as DI Kate Flemming.

When Vicky has not been uncovering corrupt police officers, she has starred in the film and TV versions of This Is England.

Her next big upcoming series is Amazon Prime's Alex Rider, where she will play Mrs Jones.

David Bradley

Harry Potter star David Bradley played a mysterious newsagent owner in the first series of Broadchurch.

Murdered schoolboy Danny delivered newspapers for Bradley's character, Jack Marshall, so they were together on a daily basis.

With his past a mystery to most in the town, and given his relationship with Danny, he quickly became a suspect but was innocent.

David, who is best known for playing Filch in the Harry Potter film series, won a BAFTA for Best Supporting Actor for his performance in Broadchurch series one.

Another actor with a link to Doctor Who, he starred in an episode in 2013 then returned to play the First Doctor in a 2017 Christmas special.

The Hollywood star has also appeared in Hot Fuzz, Captain America: The First Avenger and HBO hit series Game of Thrones as evil Walder Frey.

Most recently he played Ricky Gervais' on-screen dad in both series of Netflix's Afterlife.

Tanya Franks

EastEnders favourite Tanya Franks starred in Broadchurch during one of her stints away from the BBC soap.

The Rainie Branning actress was Olivia Colman's on-screen sister Lucy Stevens, who was also the mother of journalist Olly Stevens.

Gambling addict Lucy disgustingly demanded money to reveal information regarding schoolboy Danny's death in series one.

DS Miller's sister returned in series two and witnessed Joe destroying evidence in the Latimer case.

Tanya joined EastEnders in August 2007 for a week-long stint but repeatedly returned until 2011.

Rainie made a surprise return in June 2014 for almost a year, then made a full comeback in April 2018 as Max Branning's shock new wife.

Away from Albert Square, Tanya has starred in the West End and many British TV favourites including Family Affairs, Skins, The Bill and Holby City.

Julie Hesmondhalgh

The third series of Broadchurch focused around the rape of Trish Winterman at her friend's party.

Former Coronation Street star Julie Hesmondhalgh gave a powerful portrayal of Trish, who bravely rebuilt her life after a horrifying attack.

The detectives had many theories over who raped Trish, including fears it may have been her ex-husband or boss who was madly in love with her.

They later discovered that Leo Humphries had forced Michael Lucas to rape Trish after knocking her out at the party.

Julie is best known for playing lovable Hayley Cropper in Coronation Street - playing a British soap's first ever transgender character.

Having started on the cobbles in 1998, Julie bowed out at the start of 2014 when Hayley sadly died following a long battle with cancer.

After Broadchurch, Julie starred in Doctor Who, Catastrophe and played Joe's teacher in the third series of The A Word this year.

Arthur Darville

Arthur Darville's local vicar, Paul Coates, was a suspect in series one when he failed to produce an alibi to the detectives.

Dannys murder brought tonnes of worshippers to his failing church, where he comforted the residents in their dark hour.

He also had access to Danny as he teaches the schoolboys IT - while also hiding that is a recovering alcoholic.

Paul caused outrage in series two by visiting Joe in prison, before helping to organise his escape from the town.

Arthur first made a name for himself starring as Amy Pond's husband Rory Williams in Doctor Who from 2008.

In 2015 he was cast in the main role of Rip Hunter in DC's Legends of Tomorrow - starring in the comic book-based series for three seasons.

He has also starred as the lead male role in musical Once on Broadway in New York and the West End.

Pauline Quirke

Nosy neighbour Susan was an unfriendly caravan owner who lived alone by the beach - played brilliantly by Pauline Quirke.

Suspicion was turned to her as she lived near where Danny's body was found, but she claimed to have seen his body being dragged there.

The sinister and highly observant woman had run-ins with Nige, who we discovered is her son, and she also threatened newspaper editor Maggie to stop digging into her past.

Her pet Labrador, Bailey, also had a starring role in Broadchurch

Pauline became one of the nation's favourite comedic actresses playing Sharon Theodopolopodous in Birds of a Feather from 1989 to 1998.

She returned alongside close pals Linda Robson and Lesley Joseph when the series was revived from 2014 to 2016.

Sarah Parish

Sarah Parish was brought into the Broadchurch cast in series three as Trish's best friend and colleague at the farm shop.

Their relationship was strained after Trish was raped at Cath's party - and suspicions turned to her husband.

But Cath battled to help her friend and the pair made amends before discovering who the rapist was.

Sarah has appeared in a very long-lost of British TV shows including Doctor Who, Mistresses, Merlin and W1A.

After starring in the final series of Broadchurch, she played Supt Elizabeth Bancroft in ITV show Bancroft.

*Broadchurch airs Mondays on ITV at 9pm
