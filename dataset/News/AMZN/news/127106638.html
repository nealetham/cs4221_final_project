What You Missed On Wall Street On Friday
Mid/Close Wrap
Sam Martinelli - email
Five top five lists with the news and calls moving stocks, powered by The Fly.

Get caught up quickly on the top news and calls moving stocks with these five Top Five lists.

1. STOCK NEWS:

  • U.S. nonfarm payrolls rose 254,000 in September from the prior month, with the unemployment rate falling to 4.1%
  • Rivian Automotive (RIVN) announced a"production disruption"and revised its annual production view
  • Spirit Airlines (SAVE) is in discussions over the terms of a bankruptcy filing,WSJ reports
  • Coeur Mining (CDE) agreed to acquireSilverCrest Metals (SILV) in an $11.34 per share transaction
  • A Chicago court has upheld the$45M mesothelioma verdict against Johnson & Johnson (JNJ)

2. WALL STREET CALLS:

  • TD Cowen upgradedCVS Health (CVS) to Buy
  • HSBC upgraded Morgan Stanley (MS) to Buy, says underperformance may be ending
  • UBS downgradedMobileye (MBLY) to Neutral
  • GE Vernova (GEV) downgradedat HSBC, here's why
  • Dine Brands (DIN) downgradedto Hold from Buy at Truist

3. AROUND THE WEB:

  • Tencent (TCEHY) and the Guillemot family are considering options including a potential buyout of Ubisoft (UBSFY) after the company lost more than half its market value this year, Bloomberg says
  • At least two dozen B. Riley Financial (RILY) brokers, almost half of the roster at its wealth's branch office in Boca Raton, Florida, moved this week to Kestra Advisory Services, Bloomberg reports
  • The Supreme Court said it will hear Smith & Wesson Brands (SWBI) efforts to toss out a lawsuit filed by the Mexican government accusing the company of facilitating firearm sales to people connected to Mexico's drug cartels, Bloomberg says
  • Stellantis (STLA) is taking "drastic measures" to shore up finance, WSJ reports
  • Apollo Global (APO) is in talks to acquire Barnes Group (B) and prevail over other buyout firms that were also vying for the company, Reuters reports

4. MOVERS:

  • Diamondback Energy (FANG) higher after BMO Capital upgraded the stockto Outperform from Market Perform
  • Albemarle (ALB) and Arcadium (ALTM) gains in New York after The Australian reported Rio Tinto (RIO) may be looking at M&A in the lithium space and these companies are the two targets thought to be in the company's sight
  • TeraWulf (WULF) falls after reporting production and operating highlights for September
  • Warner Music (WMG) lower after BofA downgradedthe stock to Underperform
  • ZIM Integrated (ZIM) declines after WSJ reported dockworkers have agreed to end a three-day strike after port employers offered a 62% increase in wages over six years

5. EARNINGS/GUIDANCE:

  • AbbVie (ABBV) provided guidance for Q3 and FY24
  • Conmed (CNMD) reported preliminary Q3 results
  • Lifecore (LFCR) reported Q1 results and reiterated its guidance for FY25
  • Apogee Enterprises (APOG) reported Q2 results and raised its guidance for FY25
  • Phoenix Motor (PEV) announced Q1 results, with EPS higher year-over-year

INDEXES:

The Dow rose 341.16, or 0.81%, to 42,352.75, the Nasdaq gained 219.38, or 1.22%, to 18,137.85, and the S&P 500 advanced 51.13, or 0.90%, to 5,751.07.
