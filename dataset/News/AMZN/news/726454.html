BOUNDED RATIONALITY AND CONTEXT INFLUENCE IN DECISION MAKING (Is a consumer always rational?)

NISHIT ABHAY PATIL

·

Follow

5 min read
·
May 31, 2020

--

To answer the question of a consumer always being rational, we first need to understand what is rationality.
Well, rationality has many many meanings across various fields. In general rationality implies the conformity of one’s beliefs with one’s reasons to believe, and of one’s actions with one’s reasons for action. In simpler terms, it means thinking sensibly or logically. Rationality has further specialized meanings in fields such as psychology, philosophy, sociology and economics.

For this discussion we will focus on the economics appellation of the term.
As per economics, rationality is simply means that when you make a choice, you will choose the thing you like best.Now, how do we exactly define “choosing the best”? Well in layman's terms, it means selecting the good we want the most or which gives us the most complacency which consumption.
Now, being rational is not only selecting what gives you the most utility, it is doing so by having perfect knowledge of the market, of the ancillaries of the commodity and managing the budget wisely.

What is Bounded Rationality?

Now that we know what rationality is, it is obvious that one question comes to mind, Is it possible that a consumer is always rational?
Well bounded rationality is the exact opposite of rationality. It was made in counter action of the rationality theory. It says that the decisions we make are rational, but we make it within the limited knowledge or information available to us.
It might be easier to understand with an example. If we are to buy a new phone and after some searching and research, we decide the model which we want. We then procced to a local mobile shop and buy the phone. Later when you show the phone to your friends and they tell the exact same model with the exact same specs is available for half the price on Amazon, we may regret our decision. Hence, we acted rationally, but our knowledge was limited. So, Bounded Rationality is a state in which the consumer believes that he/she has made the best decision and will achieve maximum satisfaction from the choice made by them. There is no way that the consumer can have all knowledge at all times and is hence also making a decision based on their limited knowledge.

What is Contextual Influence?

It’s actually very natural. Whatever we decide we feel it’s the best decision.
Now is this decision making process affected in any way? Yes it may be affected by a lot of facets. It maybe that we see something with one of friends and we immediately want the same thing or in some case we might want something even better than what our friend has. Like for example if my friend has recently bought a very advanced laptop, it may be possible that after seeing it I would want a even better or the same laptop even if I have no current need of the item. And I may make an irrational decision and buy a laptop for no particular reason.
Also, sometimes what we buy and from where we buy can also be influenced by the society. If I am to buy a simple chocolate, I could do so from my local grocery store. Or if the chocolate I like is not available there I might be willing to travel a bit farther to get it. But lets say its a chocolate which is not well likes by most of the population but I like it. So it might be possible that when I reach the store to buy the chocolate and I meet a few people who I already know in the store, it might be so that I would quickly change my decision to buy the chocolate as I would feel that my decision would be made fun of by others. In very blunt terms, in most situations people make decisions to please others and to be in the good books of the society and maybe that’s what given them maximum satisfaction but in the end, its an irrational decision. Buying recklessly or for achieving utility from pleasing others is not considered rational.

So, contextual influence is a psychological aspect which affects our decision making. It is basically the external factors (the chocolate example) or our own internal feelings (the laptop example) which give rise to irrational decision making.

Role of human psychology in decision making?

When people are in similar situations, their decisions are fast and automatic. Like, if we are out to shop for our monthly groceries, we know what we want, the quantity that we want and the brands which we want.
But if we happen to go out shopping for cloths which we don’t do quite often, then we will take time in decision making as we will look across shops and brands to see which one will give us the most utility.
To make rational decisions according to psychology, one needs to keep a open mind and should be able to figure which decision is made out of need and which decision is made out of want.

Conclusion

No, a consumer is not always rational and has limited knowledge and information. It is not possible for a consumer to have perfect knowledge at all. And all consumers are hence rationally bounded. The best a consumer can do is observe, collect, and utilize the information from his/her surroundings to their best benefit.
