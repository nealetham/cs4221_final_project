    The Nasdaq is back below its 21-day moving average, with the S&P 500 rapidly approaching that same key shorted-dated trendline. Stocks and indexes always come back down to key moving averages before they can restart their march higher.

    The recent red wave helped send the Nasdaq from some of its most overbought RSI levels in the last five years in late December to below neutral. Every investor should pay attention to how the Nasdaq and the S&P 500 interact with the 21-day, the 50-day, and the 200-day in January and throughout the year since they often prove to be lines in the sand the bulls attempt to defend.
