    Tyrone Mings has responded to a minor backlash suffered as a result of a recent article.

    In an interview with The Daily Mail, the Aston Villa defender claimed that players like himself were perhaps viewed as commodities in the Premier League's Project Restart plan, and said that a return to action is financially motivated.

    However, Mings suffered quite the verbal barrage as fans refused to look beyond the headline.

    "Go work at Amazon then, they are hiring."

    "Who does he think he is?"

    "Loves a moan this bloke."

    "Funny I didnt get consulted about going back to work either. Get on with your job."

    "Thats what happens with businesses. Youre an employee Mr Mings, you dont get a say. Same in most professions. My job never paused during this pandemic, I never got a say in it. Welcome to life as normal."

    Naturally, there were plenty of positive responses - but Mings acknowledged the impact of his words on Sky Sports' Football Show today.

    "That article seems to have ruffled a few feathers but it was my honest opinion about our standing in the game," said Mings speaking to Graeme Souness and Jamie Redknapp.
