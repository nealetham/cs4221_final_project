    The Walking Dead 10: Negan's exit 'confirmed' as star makes move away from series

    THE WALKING DEAD is set to conclude its thrilling tenth season later this year. However, as the action builds up to an epic final showdown, one of the series' major players may not be sticking around.

    By Lucas Hill-Paul
    23:31, Sun, Jul 5, 2020 | UPDATED: 23:12, Thu, Jul 9, 2020
    Link copied Bookmark

    The Walking Dead: Dramatic teaser for season ten finale

    Jeffrey Dean Morgan has impressed fans with his performance as the morally complex Negan since his introduction in the season six finale of The Walking Dead. Ahead of the show’s delayed finale in 2020 due to coronavirus, new casting news surrounding the AMC star have indicated his character’s time is coming to an end. Reports have suggested Morgan will soon be joining an acclaimed new series on Amazon Prime Video, as well as the DC Extended Universe.

    Episode 16 of the still ongoing tenth season of The Walking Dead, A Certain Doom, was met with significant delays amid the outbreak of coronavirus.

    With the risk still high but lockdown restrictions beginning to ease in some US States, some fans are anticipating the season’s conclusion to air sometime soon.

    Meanwhile, recent casting reports have suggested Jeffrey Dean Morgan, who has played the unpredictable anti-hero Negan since season 6, may be leaving the series.

    So far, the current instalment of TWD has been bittersweet for many devoted viewers, as showrunner Angela Kang’s return to form has also included several cast departures.

    Following series lead Rick Grimes (played by Andrew Lincoln) leaving last season, the latest outing also featured the exit of Michonne (Danai Gurira) and the brutal death of Laura (Lindsley Register).

    After publicly campaigning for a role on Amazon Prime Video’s superhero series The Boys, the show’s head writer Eric Kripke confirmed the two are currently in talks.

    Season two of The Boys will be released on Amazon on September 4th 2020, but ongoing reports suggest Morgan could join the cast in season three.

    Based on a comic by Garth Ennis and Darick Robertson, The Boys follows ex-CIA agent Billy Butcher (Karl Urban) as he attempts to take down a corrupt league of superheroes.


    The Walking Dead: Will Jeffrey Dean Morgan join the cast of The Boys? (Image: Amazon Prime Video)

    Season one featured rave reviews and was considered a darkly thrilling antidote to film and television’s standard superhero fare.

    An interview with the showrunner for Collider confirmed the production team already has a character in mind for Jeffrey Dean Morgan in season three.

    Kripke said: "There's one role we're already talking about. He has to, uh, we have to like coordinate. Because you know, he's on The Walking Dead, so he has another home.

    "But we already talked about one role, and there might be a potential other that we're talking about.”

    Although the casting news has not been confirmed, the writer continued by assuring things are making headway ahead of the season two premiere.

    He added: "But we are, just this past week we were literally texting back and forth about trying to figure out how to get him on the show.

    "I don't think it's a done deal yet, but the will is there, and we're both talking about it."

    Jeffrey Dean Morgan is also known for playing sleazy superhero The Comedian in the precursor to The Boys, Watchmen, as well as roles in Rampage and Supernatural.

      His heavily rumoured role in upcoming superhero blockbuster The Flash would see Morgan star alongside Ezra Miller as an alternate version of Thomas Wayne who dons the Batman identity following the death of his son, Bruce.

      With Jeffrey Dean Morgan’s filming schedule filling up, some TWD fans are concerned the star’s role as Negan is set to come to an end at some point in season 11.
