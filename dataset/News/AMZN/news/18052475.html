CEOs Of Amazon, Apple, Facebook And Google Agree To Testify At House Antitrust Hearing

Rachel Sandler
Former Staff
I cover the world's richest.
Jul 1, 2020,08:44pm EDT
Updated Jul 1, 2020, 08:44pm EDT
This article is more than 4 years old.
  • Share to Facebook
  • Share to Twitter
  • Share to Linkedin

TOPLINE

The CEOs of Amazon, Apple, Facebook and Google will testify together before the House antitrust subcommittee, escalating scrutiny from lawmakers as the tech giants also face antitrust investigations from federal and state officials.

KEY FACTS

A date has not been set and it hasn’t yet been decided if the hearing will be virtual because of the pandemic, according to the New York Times.

In mid-June, Politico reported Sundar Pichai of Google, Mark Zuckerberg of Facebook and Jeff Bezos of Amazon had all agreed to testify on a four-CEO panel, but Cook was the only hold out at the time. Apple did not immediately respond to a request for comment from Forbes.

The hearing will be the first time Bezos appears before Congress for testimony.

As the big four—Google, Facebook, Amazon and Apple—grow in size, revenue and prominence, lawmakers have been asking if these companies have made too many acquisitions, unfairly block out smaller companies in favor of their own services, and ultimately harm consumers.

The House has been investigating the power and influence of large tech giants since 2019 and the results of the probe may inform changes in antitrust laws that would make it easier to break up these companies.

Tech companies have also been facing backlash outside of antitrust issues, including Facebook’s handling of misinformation and hate speech and Amazon’s response to pandemic inside its warehouses.

Key background

 Both Pichai and Zuckerberg previously testified before Congress in 2018 when lawmakers questioned both CEOs about privacy, misinformation and bias. Zuckerberg was pressed on Russian meddling in the 2016 election and the Cambridge Analytica scandal. Cook, meanwhile, hasn’t appeared before Congress since a 2013 hearing where a bipartisan group of Senators grilled him on Apple’s tax strategy, accusing the company of stashing cash overseas to avoid paying billions of dollars in U.S. taxes.

News peg

The Department of Justice and Federal Trade Commission are conducting separate antitrust investigations into Amazon, Apple, Facebook and Google. Though each probe is still ongoing, the Wall Street Journal reported in May that the Justice Department and a group of state attorneys general are looking to file antitrust lawsuits against Google as early as this year, focusing on the company’s advertising business.
