Stock Splits Shouldn’t Matter. Investors Love Them Anyway.

Published: March 11, 2022 at 4:50 p.m. ET

Mathematically, stock splits create no value. Splitting your stock 20-for-1—a move Amazon announced last week—isn’t really any different than swapping a $20 bill for 20 singles. Your wallet might be a little fatter, but you aren’t any richer. The fact that split announcements often spur stock prices to rally isn’t logical. That said, there is academic research showing the split effect is real and could signal management confidence.

Sure enough, Amazon.com(ticker: AMZN) rallied after it announced its split, along with a largely symbolic $10 billion stock repurchase plan. Apple(AAPL), it’s worth noting, has been returning nearly $10 billion to shareholders every month in the form of buybacks and dividends.
