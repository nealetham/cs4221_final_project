My team has thoroughly tested over 30 providers to find the best VPN in the UK. After these tests, we’ve deduced the best VPN is NordVPN. The provider has a strict no-logs policy, boasts servers in over 111 countries and comes with numerous features to increase your online security.

If NordVPN isn’t right for you, we have detailed nine other VPNs in this guide to help you find the best provider for your needs.

NordVPN overview

NordVPN is our pick of the best VPN for most users because it excels in a wide range of scenarios and earned the highest scores across the board in our testing. 

While it doesn’t offer a free trial, it’s available from £2.69 per month and you can take advantage of its simple 30-day money-back guarantee, which allows you to test NordVPN for yourself with no risk.

NordVPN is based in Panama, which is especially privacy-friendly, and it offers over 6,300 servers to pick from across 111 countries. This extensive network has more locations for an IP address than any of the alternatives I’ve listed below.

Having conducted over 500 recent speed tests in the latest shake-up of our methodology, NordVPN was the undisputed fastest VPN on Windows and placed in the top three overall in terms of performance.

Streaming fans will be glad to hear that it aced our content access tests, and worked well with pretty much any UK platform you can think of. NordVPN also earned the highest scores overall for criteria such as privacy and security (9.00), usability (9.00), and features (9.68), giving it a weighted average score of 8.81 out of 10.

Overall, NordVPN provides a reliable streaming solution with an independently audited no-logs policy, which guarantees that NordVPN doesn’t track or record its user’s online activity.

NordVPN features

NordVPN offers unique features to boost user speeds and increase protection. NordVPN Threat Protection is a proprietary malware protection solution which eliminates third-party applications on users’ devices and prevents user tracking. 

The Dark Web Monitor feature scans the internet for credential leaks and will notify you immediately if your linked email address is spotted. NordPass is also an effective password manager, making it ideal if you don’t want to use a third-party service. Meshnet allows users to create an encrypted network for up to 10 devices on your account.

Finally, NordVPN released its own VPN protocol with NordLynx. Built using the WireGuard protocol, NordLynx aims to offer faster connection speeds while still protecting your online identity.

Surfshark overview

You can invest in Surfshark’s cheap deals from just £1.49 per month. Surfshark is one of the top providers on the market and comes with some of the fastest speeds and no device limitations.

Unlike the majority of affordable options, it comes with an audited no-logs policy, and it’s adept at accessing the most popular streaming services. During my tests, I found it worked well with Netflix and BBC iPlayer, although it struggled with Now TV.

It proved to be the fastest VPN overall during testing, which is especially impressive given that it’s available for a fraction of the cost of the likes of ExpressVPN. Surfshark also scored well in terms of features, as it provides support across MacOS, Windows, iOS, and Android devices.

With this VPN, you can connect with an unlimited number of devices simultaneously, allowing you to share an account with friends and family at no extra cost.

As with any reputable provider, you can try Surfshark risk-free thanks to its 30-day money-back guarantee.

Surfshark features

You can expect classic features like AES-256-bit encryption, an automatic kill switch and multihop support from Surfshark. Multihop adds another layer of encryption by routing your data through two secure servers, making it harder to uncover your IP address. Surfshark also boasts a massive network of more than 3,200 servers in 100 countries.

Some features specific to Surfshark include CleanWeb, which blocks ads, trackers and malware for further peace of mind. CleanWeb can be used via the Surfshark app and comes included in the Starter plan.

If you need to access websites while in other countries, Camouflage Mode and NoBorders allow secure access to the internet from anywhere on the planet. For those concerned about privacy, Surfshark uses Alternative ID to generate an online profile. Alternative Number is a paid add-on which also creates an online phone number, keeping your actual information obscure when you sign up for online services. These two features are truly unique and set Surfshark apart from the crowd.

ExpressVPN overview

ExpressVPN is our top streaming VPN and comes in at £3.97 per month. ExpressVPN combines fast speeds and strong streaming support to great effect. The provider has been independently audited 12 times between 2018 and 2022, more than any of its competitors, proving its reliability as a secure provider. 

As with NordVPN, ExpressVPN has recently upped its game, increasing the maximum number of simultaneous connections from five to eight while significantly expanding its network of servers to include 105 countries.

ExpressVPN stands out as one of the best VPNs for content access, acing all of our streaming tests across 10 streaming platforms. The provider recorded the fastest overall UK VPN server speeds via MacOS (402.00 Mbps) and Windows devices (269.19 Mbps).

It also scored high marks jurisdictionally, as it’s based in the British Virgin Islands. The British Virgin Islands are based outside of the Five Eyes, an intelligence-sharing agreement between Australia, Canada, New Zealand, the UK and the US. The Five Eyes is not very transparent about its operations and can share and request information about these countries’ citizens. Because of this, working with a VPN based outside the Five Eyes promises more security than working with those within its jurisdiction.

It also is ideal for anyone who wants to be covered by some of the best customer services in the business. ExpressVPN’s response times via live chat averaged less than a minute (51 seconds); that’s the time it takes to speak to a real human, as opposed to the bot support seen elsewhere.

You won’t be left feeling short-changed if you decide to sign up with ExpressVPN, and it comes with a no-quibble 30-day money-back guarantee just in case you’re not fully satisfied.

ExpressVPN features

While it’s no longer a clear frontrunner overall, scoring 8.46 out of 10, ExpressVPN manages to keep up with the leading pack. It offers unique features such as Lightway, which aims to use

less battery and create a more effective connection to your VPN for a smoother experience.

ExpressVPN went above and beyond with its ShuffleIP feature, which automatically changes the IP address assigned to you for every web server you access, making it harder to link your online activity back to you.

Post-quantum protection is available as long as you’re using the Lightway protocol and can protect your computer against quantum computing. While it’s not a feature you need right now, it aims to protect your data in the event that more powerful computers in the future manage to bypass the encryption protocols currently in place, making ExpressVPN more future-proof than its competitors.

Proton VPN overview

ProtonVPN is one of the best VPNs for those concerned about customer service (10). In our tests, it took an average of just 21 seconds to receive a response from a human representative, the shortest time of any provider on this list. All three questions we asked were answered satisfactorily, earning ProtonVPN the highest customer service rating of all the providers we tested.

There aren’t many providers who focus on security and privacy to the extent that Proton VPN does. Given that it also delivers the goods in terms of speeds and content access, it’s especially useful if you don’t mind paying a slightly higher price. 

Its server numbers are always increasing, from 2,700 to 8,100+ over the past 18 months. While it previously offered connections to servers in 67 countries, it is up to 112 at the time of writing.

Proton VPN performed reasonably well in our speed tests when we connected to nearby servers in the UK. However, we did see a slowdown while testing the Windows app via London-based servers (126.68 Mbps), especially when compared to MacOS (402.00 Mbps) and iOS (405.00 Mbps) Proton VPN apps. As a result, the VPN Accelerator seems like more of a gimmick than anything else.

If you’re willing to spend a little more on your VPN subscription, Proton VPN is undeniably one of the best providers on the market. It misses out on a top three spot by the finest of margins (0.02), but it’s still our top pick as the best VPN for customer service.

ProtonVPN features

Proton VPN comes with AES-256 encryption, split tunnelling and a kill switch. The latter allows you to send certain traffic through a VPN and others through a local network, while the latter will automatically disconnect your device from the internet if your VPN connection drops.

The NetShield feature protects users from ads and stops trackers from monitoring their browsing history and VPN Accelerator claims it has the ability to boost your connection speed by up to 400 per cent, smoothing out buffering time when streaming and improving connection stability. 
Finally, ProtonVPN is one of the few providers to offer a free version of the app, giving you the chance to try before you buy. Just like the paid edition, it has a strict no-logs policy and offers unlimited bandwidth, although the servers are prone to slower speeds.

What is a VPN?

A virtual private network, or VPN, allows you to create a secure connection between your device and a server in a location you’ve selected. Predominantly used for privacy and security, a VPN masks your IP address, making it tough for anyone to track your online activities. You’ll be assigned a new IP address that matches the server location you’ve selected. This can be used to access geo-blocked websites and services. VPNs generally use a paid subscription model and expect the entire sum upfront when you sign up. VPNs are a form of software, although you can buy a VPN router if you’re looking for a hardware solution.

VPNs tend to be fairly easy to use, with free and paid options to choose from. They allow the user to access public Wi-Fi safely, so a VPN can be helpful while you’re travelling, especially if you want to access banking apps. VPNs allow the user to torrent safely, and you’ll find apps for almost any device or operating system you can think of.

How does a VPN work?

A VPN creates a secure tunnel between your device and the internet by encrypting your data and routing it through a remote server. This means your online activities are kept private, as your data is not visible to anyone monitoring your connection. When you connect to a website, your data is sent through the VPN’s server, hiding your real IP address and location while protecting your data from being intercepted. This will help to keep any interested parties such as your internet service provider (ISP), at bay, as they will only be able to see that you’ve connected to a VPN server, rather than all of the traffic sent to and from your device. 

VPN encryption is especially important to ensure there are no data leaks to worry about. The same is true for no-logs policies, as the VPN itself could still have access to your data, and needs to get rid of it accordingly. Rather than taking logging claims at face value, look for a provider with an audited no-logs policy to remove the need for any guesswork.

VPNs we wouldn’t recommend

I would strongly advise not to opt for the first ‘free’ VPN service that pops up when you open your browser or device’s app store. For example, the ever-popular Hola VPN openly admits;

“Like any P2P network, Hola is a “value exchange” network — you get the Hola free limited service (on PC, Mac or Android), and in exchange you provide a certain amount of network and processing power when your computer is not in use.”

It’s best to stick with an audited VPN, especially as the provider has access to your personal data. After all, it’s not unheard of for some VPNs to sell user data, or to include hidden malware. Urban VPN is another popular VPN that I wouldn’t recommend, given it logs a heap of user data in the process.
