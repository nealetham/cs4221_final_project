Boston Dynamics'robot dog, Spot, is now available to buy for $74,500. This marks the first time Spot has gone on general sale to anyone who wants to buy one. While the high price tag might rule out the average buyer, this robot dog could prove useful to a number of businesses, and in a variety of different industries.

✕ Remove Ads

Until now, Boston Dynamics' robot dog had only been available to select buyers. Amazingly, Spot even played a role during the COVID-19 crisis by helping frontline workers treat those who were contagious. Spot would ensure that frontline medical workers remained at a safe distance by adding the ability for infected individuals to be treated, remotely. For example, hospitals would attach a tablet to the autonomous robot to provide patients with virtual face time, without being physically present. In the past, the robot dog has also joined forces with the Massachusetts State Police and was even tested by the U.S. Marines as a possible addition to its operations.

Spot comes in a robot fleet of three. Depending on your needs, you can choose from the Explorer, Enterprise or Academic version, with all three displayed on the Boston Dynamics website. However, it is only the Explorer edition that's listed as an 'add to cart,' option along with the $74,500 price tag. The other models require the buyer to contact the company to discuss sales and purchase options. Even though Spot is now available to buy, consumers are advised that the product is designed for industrial or commercial use. In addition, the robot dog is not certified for home use or designed for use near children. The overall package includes everything anyone would need to get started with Spot, including a tablet controller, all the necessary software, a storage case, and even an additional battery.

Spot Can Do More Than Just Run

✕ Remove Ads

Some of the tricks or features that Spot can do include, climbing stairs and traveling through rough terrain with ease. Spot can also support remote operation and has autonomous sensing, making it ideal to navigate remote environments, investigate unknown locations, and collect data while on its travels. As mentioned, Spot does come with a tablet controller, and this enables the creation of basic autonomous movements, as well as a way for the user to pre-program Spot'sroute to avoid any obstacles. Lastly, at 65 lbs, Spotis also small enough to move around indoors when needed.

While individuals might see a certain personal value in owning a robot like Spot, where the robot dog can really prove valuable is in areas where human life could be at risk. For example, healthcare workers could use Spotto continue to help patients by delivering food, medicine or disinfecting rooms. Alternatively, Spotcould assist with public safety by inspecting hazardous situations or packages from afar, or in the mining industry by navigating tunnels and ensuring safe working conditions.
