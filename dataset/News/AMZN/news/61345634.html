Smart speakers can make great tech gifts this holiday season and there are many available that won't break the bank. While it is true that holiday shopping can be a challenge, a smart speaker can make an excellent choice for gift-givers in a bind. Even though lots of people aren't familiar with smart speakers, they are not only for tech-savvy or well-off households. No matter who consumers need to give a present to, smart speakers have plenty to provide. What's more, many of them even cost $99 or less, so the decision to choose a smart speaker as a gift can be a low-risk one as well as an easy one.

✕ Remove Ads

One of the main appeals of smart speakers is using them to control other smart devices wirelessly in the home, such as the TV, lights, locks, blinds, and so on. With the smart speaker's voice assistant, you give the speaker a command, the speaker communicates with the smart device, and the device obliges - no switches or buttons necessary. Smart speakers can also provide audio output from a TV, smartphone or other device, and they can communicate with one another to relay messages between rooms of a home. Many consumers, though, only buy a smart speaker to use its voice assistant for simple tasks, contemplating other possibilities only after they've begun using it. Some of the most notable smart speaker manufacturers include Amazon, Google, Apple, Sonos, JBL, and Bose.

The best recommendation for the average user would be the Amazon 4th Gen Echo Dot, going for just $59. This speaker is small, has an appealing globe shape, and comes in black, white, or blue. It looks more like a souvenir in the home rather than a misplaced music speaker. As Amazon smart speakers come with the Alexa voice assistant, there are many smart products available that users already might own or can buy so that this speaker can connect to them all. Setting up an intercom system is also a cinch. Homeowners can purchase Echo Flex for $24.99 each and plug them into power outlets for easy and cheap communication. The basic Amazon 4th Gen Echo is priced at $99 and, while it has better audio capabilities, there is still enough to be satisfied with the Dot in order to pay less.

Buy Echo Dot Speaker (Amazon)

Apple HomePod Is The Most "Crisp"

✕ Remove Ads

For homeowners or recipients that want excellent sound, the best choice in that regard is the Apple HomePod. With the best sound quality available in a smart speaker, the HomePod can create a rich sound experience for music streaming or movie watching — almost as good as professional sound speakers. This is also a great option for Apple customers that might already own an iPhone and other Apple products. They can all be connected through smart assistant Siri. While the HomePod hasn't received a permanent price drop from $299 yet, shoppers can expect big sales around the holiday time. The HomePod mini, meanwhile, is a new release this holiday season at just $99, but the sound quality cannot be compared, nor can they connect to regular HomePods for stereo listening.

Buy HomePod (Apple)

✕ Remove Ads

All smart speakers will make very serviceable gifts, but if shoppers are looking for the best value or the best sound, the Echo Dot and the HomePod, respectively, will be the best choices. Among these two, though, the Echo Dot may give homeowners a better smart home experience. While Apple is looking to make Siri available in more smart products, the number of compatible devices isn't as big as it is for Amazon's Alexa at the moment. Homeowners can also purchase five Echo Dots for the price of one HomePod, so each member of the family could get their own tailored Alexa experience while laying the groundwork for an enjoyable smart tech home.
