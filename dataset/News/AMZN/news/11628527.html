The top 50 films of all time according to IMDB

A cinema trip might be out of the question for a while but there's a whole world of film at your fingertips

    Lockdown is here yet again, and with opportunities to leave the house now limited largely to shopping and a daily dose of exercise, many of us will be settling down in front of the telly.

    A cinema trip might be out of the question for a while but there's a whole world of film at your fingertips on streaming platforms like Netflix, Amazon Prime, Disney+ and NowTV.

    These are the 50 top rated films according to IMDb's average user ratings. How many can you tick off your list while you're stuck at home?

    The Shawshank Redemption (1994)

    Locked up for a double murder he didn't commit, innocent banker Andy Dufresne (Tim Robbins) is facing life in prison. There he meets Red (Morgan Freeman), whose friendship - together with his own hope and determination - offers him a way out.

    The Godfather (1972)

    Francis Ford Coppola's genre-defining gangster classic stars Marlon Brando as mob boss Don Corleone, the head of a powerful Sicilian mafia family in America.

    The Godfather: Part II (1974)

    The Oscar-winning sequel to Coppola's mafia masterpiece continues the saga of the Corleone family, charting the rise of a young Don Vito, played by Robert De Niro, and Michael (Al Pacino) as the new Don.

    The Dark Knight (2008)


    Heath Ledger won a posthumous Oscar for his defining portrayal of The Joker in this Batman blockbuster. Christian Bale stars as the caped crusader as he teams up with Lieutenant Jim Gordon (Gary Oldman) and district attorney Harvey Dent (Aaron Eckhart) to destroy organised crime in Gotham City forever.

    12 Angry Men (1957)

    This classic courtroom drama follow the murder trial of a poor Puerto Rican teenager accused of stabbing his father to death. Twelve white male jurors will decide his fate; eleven believe he's guilty but one man is convinced of his innocence and sets about making a case for acquittal.

    Schindler's List (1993)

    Steven Spielberg's Oscar-winning historical drama tells the true story of German industrialist Oskar Schindler (Liam Neeson), who saved the lives of more than 1,000 Jews during the Holocaust.

    The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King (2003)

    The final instalment of Peter Jackson's big screen adaptation of J.R.R. Tolkien's fantasy epic sees hobbit Frodo Baggins (Elijah Wood), his loyal friend Sam and the wretched Gollum journey to Mordor to destroy the Ring of Power, while armies amass for a final battle that will decide the fate of the world.

    Pulp Fiction (1994)

    Quentin Tarantino's postmodern masterpiece is one of the most influential films of its era. John Travolta and Samuel L Jackson star as a pair of low rent hit men alongside Uma Thurman as their boss's wife and Bruce Willis as a desperate prize fighter in the film, which weaves together three interrelated stories and a pastiche of genres.

    The Good, the Bad and the Ugly (1966)

    Clint Eastwood stars in this classic spaghetti western as one of three drifters searching for a Confederate cash box containing $200,000 during the American Civil War.

    The Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring (2001)

    The first chapter of the J.R.R. Tolkien adventure sees shy young hobbit Frodo Baggins (Elijah Wood) inherit a gold ring with mysterious power. Now he must join forces with a wizard, an elf, a dwarf, two men and three loyal hobbit friends to save their land and people.

    Fight Club (1999)

    Edward Norton stars as an insomniac opposite Brad Pitt as a soap salesman in this Chuck Palahniuk adaptation. Together they establish an underground fight club where young men come to thrash out their frustrations - until Marla Singer (Helena Bonham Carter) shows up and things get seriously complicated.

    Forrest Gump (1994)

    Tom Hanks stars as the endearing Forrest in this sentimental, tearjerking film that takes viewers on a ride through some of the most monumental moments in American history during the second half of the 20th century.

    Inception (2010)

    Christopher Nolan's mind-bending sci-fi thriller stars Leonardo DiCaprio as a thief who steals corporate secrets from people's subconscious while they dream - and then takes on the inverse challenge of planting an idea into the mind of a C.E.O.

    Star Wars: Episode V - The Empire Strikes Back (1980)

    After the Rebels are defeated by the Empire on the ice planet Hoth, Luke Skywalker travels journeys to the planet Dagobah to train with Jedi Master Yoda. Meanwhile Darth Vader lures young Skywalker into a trap in the Cloud City of Bespin in a bid to lure him to the dark side.

    The Lord of the Rings: The Two Towers (2002)

    Part two of the Tolkien trilogy sees Frodo and the Fellowship continue on their quest to destroy the One Ring. Their voyage takes them to the Orthanc Tower in Isengard, where dark wizard Saruman awaits, and Sauron's fortress at Barad-dur, deep in Mordor.

    The Matrix (1999)

    Keanu Reeves stars as Neo, a hacker who discovers his life has been a lie - constructed by artificially intelligent machines who have imprisoned humans in a virtual world.

    Goodfellas (1990)

    Martin Scorsese's big screen adaptation of Nicholas Pileggi's true-crime best seller Wiseguy tells the darkly funny but brutal story of life in the mob. Ray Liotta, Robert De Niro, Joe Pesci and Lorraine Bracco are among the all-star cast in what quickly quickly became a classic of the gangster genre.

    One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest (1975)

    Based on Ken Kesey's novel of the same name, this five-time Oscar winning film stars Jack Nicholson as Randle Patrick McMurphy, a small-time convict who fakes a mental illness so he can be transferred from a prison farm to a state mental institution.

    Seven Samurai (1954)

    A group of villagers hire seven warriors to protect them against the bandits that attack them every year, in Akira Kurosawa's genre-defining classic.

    Se7en (1995)

    Morgan Freeman and Brad Pitt star as homicide detectives hunting a psychopath who chooses his victims based on the Seven Deadly Sins in David Fincher's disturbing thriller.

    City of God (2002)

    Set in one of Rio de Janeiro's most notorious slums, this Brazilian drama follows the true story of a young man whose ambition to be a photographer may be his only way out.

    Life Is Beautiful (1997)

    Set during the Second World War, this Italian classic follows Jewish book seller Guido and his family who live a fairy tale life until German forces arrive and they are sent to a concentration camp. There, Guido must rely on his powers of imagination to help his son survive the horrors of the Holocaust.

    The Silence of the Lambs (1991)

    Jodie Foster stars as FBI trainee Clarice Starling who is thrust into the unsettling world of a maximum security asylum to pick the brains of psychiatrist-turned-cannibalistic serial killer Dr. Hannibal Lechter (Anthony Hopkins).

    It's a Wonderful Life (1946)

    This feelgood festive favourite stars James Stewart as a businessman pushed to the brink of suicide on Christmas Eve - until an angel arrives to show him what life would be like for his family and neighbours if he never existed.

    Parasite (2019)

    This South Korean black comedy thriller - about a poor family who con their way into the lives of a rich household - swept the board at this year's Oscars, winning Best Picture, Best Director, Best Original Screenplay and Best International Feature Film.

    Star Wars: Episode IV - A New Hope (1977)

    As they try to take over the galactic Empire, the Imperial forces capture Princess Leia and hold her hostage - until Luke Skywalker and Han Solo team up with R2-D2 and C-3PO to rescue her and restore peace.

    Saving Private Ryan (1998)

    Steven Spielberg's Oscar-winning World War Two film stars Tom Hanks as an Army captain leading a rescue mission to save a young private whose brothers have all been killed in action.

    Spirited Away (2001)

    Hayao Miyazaki's Oscar-winning animated adventure is the highest-grossing Japanese film in history. Young heroine Chihiro wanders into a mysterious world ruled by a sorceress, where she meets all manner of monsters and creatures.

    The Green Mile (1999)

    Based on the Stephen King novel, this big screen adaptation stars Tom Hanks as a death row prison guard who discovers one of the inmates, a convicted murderer, has mysterious healing powers.

    Interstellar (2014)

    As time on Earth comes to an end, a team of explorers travels to another galaxy in search of a safe future for mankind in this Christopher Nolan sci-fi flick starring Matthew McConaughey, Anne Hathaway and Jessica Chastain.

    Léon (1994)

    Luc Besson's slick and stylised action thriller stars a young Natalie Portman as 12-year-old Mathilda, who joins forces with her hitman neighbour Léon (Jean Reno) when her family are murdered by corrupt DEA agent (Gary Oldman).

    The Usual Suspects (1995)

    When five criminals are hauled in for questioning over a hijacking none of them committed, they hatch a plan for revenge - which leaves 27 people dead and a ship up in flames.

    Harakiri (1962)

    This Japanese jidaigeki (period drama) is set in the 1600s during the rule of the Tokugawa shogunate, and tells the story of Hanshirō Tsugumo, a masterless samurai who arrives at a palace and requests to commit seppuku - a form of ritual suicide - in the hope of receiving alms. There, he learns the brutal fate of another like him - and reveals how their pasts are intertwined.

    The Lion King (1994)

    Disney's classic coming-of-age adventure follows lion cub Simba as he struggles to find his place in the Circle of Life following the death of his father Mufasa, the great King of Pride Rock.

    American History X (1998)

    Edward Norton stars as a neo-Nazi who comes to realise the error of his ways by the time he is released from prison for murder. Now he must save his younger brother from going down the same extremist path.

    Terminator 2: Judgment Day (1991)

    Arnold Schwarzenegger returns as the T-800, a cyborg sent from the future to protect teenage rebel John Connor (Edward Furlong), the future saviour of the human race. But to do so he must face the T-1000 (Robert Patrick), a new and improved terminator made of liquid metal.

    The Pianist (2002)

    Based on the memoirs of Polish concert pianist Wladyslaw Szpilman, this powerful film tells the real life story of his survival of the German occupation of Warsaw during the Second World War - with the unlikely help of a sympathetic German officer.

    Back to the Future (1985)

    This 80s classic sees teenager Marty McFly sent back in time to 1955 - where he must repair the damage his sudden appearance has caused to history by rekindling his parents' romance... and then find his way back to the future with the help of inventor Doc Brown.

    Modern Times (1936)

    Charlie Chaplin's silent comedy follows a factory worker who is driven mad by the monotony of his assembly line job. After being thrown out on the street, he befriends a young, homeless orphan girl and becomes her protector.

    Psycho (1960)

    Alfred Hitchcock's landmark thriller is set in the creepy Bates Motel, run by the twitchy Norman Bates (Anthony Perkins) and his housebound, unseen mother. Runaway thief Marion Crane (Janet Leigh) soon comes to regret checking in when she decides to take a shower.

    Gladiator (2000)

    Ridley Scott's five-time Oscar-winning modern classic stars Russell Crowe as a Roman army general whose family is murdered by a corrupt prince (Joaquin Phoenix). He escapes but is captured and forced to fight as a gladiator in the gory public spectacles staged in the Coliseum - and then his time comes to exact revenge.

    City Lights (1931)

    Charlie Chaplin's directorial debut in silent films follows a penniless man who falls in love with a flower girl.

    Untouchable (2011)

    When Florida's most powerful lobbyist finds out his daughter has been seually abused, he wielded his political influence to pass some of the strictest sex offender laws in the USA. This enlightening documentary charts his crusade and its impact on 800,000 people on the registry.

    The Departed (2006)

    Leonardo DiCaprio and Matt Damon star in this drama, which sees police send an undercover cop to infiltrate South Boston's Irish Mafia - not realising the syndicate has done the same to their force.

    Whiplash (2014)

    The tyrannical conductor of a prestigious music school jazz band focuses his ferocious attention on talented drummer Andrew in this breathless story of drive and discipline.

    The Prestige (2006)

    Hugh Jackman and Christian Bale star as two magician friends who become enemies when their biggest trick goes terribly wrong. Michael Caine and Scarlett Johansson also star.

    Once Upon a Time in the West (1968)

    Regarded as one of the greatest in the genre, this spaghetti western is the tale of a notorious outlaw and a harmonica-playing gunslinger who join forces to save a beautiful young widow from a ruthless railroad tycoon.

    Joker (2019)

    Joaquin Phoenix puts in an Oscar-winning performance as the misanthropic Joker in this origin story that delves deep into his psyche and examines how society treats outsiders.

    Grave of the Fireflies (1988)

    Set in Japan during the Second World War, this animated film follows Seita and her little sister Setsuko, who must fight for survival after their mother is killed in an air raid and with their father serving in the navy.

    Casablanca (1942)

    This old Hollywood classic stars Humphrey Bogart as American ex-pat and bar owner Rick Blaine, who is torn between love - his old flame Ilsa (Ingrid Bergman) - and virtue - helping her Czech Resistance leader husband escape from Casablanca to fight against the Nazis.
