True to today's online rumblings, the price and release date of the PlayStation 5 seem to be imminent, with Sony's next-gen console having suddenly gotten a Amazon placeholder landing page. While it's possible that the vague Holiday 2020 release window will continue to hang around for a little longer, the price of the console will absolutely come to light if PS5 pre-orders open up today.

✕ Remove Ads

With Sony and Microsoft carefully playing their cards close to their chests before kicking off the next console generation, the price of the PS5 and Xbox Series X have remained a mystery as either side waits for the other to make the first damning move. Although Sony has public interest and a handy current console generation victory at its back, Microsoft has the resources to eat cost and undercut the price of either version of the PS5 (which comes with a disc-drive or digital-only). Earlier today, murmurs have slowly grown into a deafening din after Amazon pages for the PS5 - sans price or release date, of course - began popping up around the world, signalling an incoming reveal by Sony.

Related: PS5 vs PlayStation 5 Digital: What Are The Differences?

✕ Remove Ads

Now, Amazon US has its own PS5 listing, complete with the expected rundown of models, features, and accessories that can be expected of a big hardware release. The release window listed alongside each version of the console is still "Holiday 2020," and a price is so far nowhere to be seen at the time of writing. However, that's liable (and probable) to change at any given moment, as this sort of landing page generally precedes the opening of pre-orders. Listed at the end of the landing page are the known PS5 exclusives Marvel's Spider-Man: Miles Morales, Horizon Forbidden West, Ratchet & Clank: Rift Apart, and more, none of which are linked to their own listings as of yet.
