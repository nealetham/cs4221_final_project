Amazon is paying customers to choose the new ‘no rush’ delivery option

Amazon is now able to offer next day delivery on non-essential items again, but has added an alternative incentive for Prime Members

  • Share

By

    Amazon has announced that they have returned to business as usual with the delivery of non-essential items, which means the next day option is back for Prime Members.

    The retailing giant had previously prioritised orders made on ‘household staples, medical supplies and other high-demand products’ in its response to the ongoing Covid-19 pandemic.

    Amazon has also hired somewhere in the region of 170,000 new employees since the beginning of March in a bid to cope with the high increase in demand for online products.

    Coronavirus in Scotland

    All eligible orders are now available for next day delivery, however, the Amazon website has added a new incentive to encourage customers to select a later delivery slot.

    At checkout, Amazon Prime customers who choose the new 'No Rush' delivery option are being given a £1 credit on orders made over £10.

    The Fineprint

    The ongoing offer is open to Amazon Prime Members only and is available until May 14.

    Credit is applied to orders made through the Amazon marketplace - not through orders made to third-party sellers - and excludes alcohol, baby and infant formula, gift cards and items sold within warehouse deals.

    Amazon is also still encouraging customers to shop responsibly, adding that they are still seeing "a lot of community demand for basic household items".

    They advise customers not to stockpile "and perhaps offer to help older neighbours, family or friends by sourcing items for them".
