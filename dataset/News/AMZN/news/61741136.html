Sky Q And Now TV Get Amazon Prime Video Support

ByJohn ArcherSenior Contributor
Following
Dec 14, 2020, 09:57am EST
This article is more than 3 years old.

British subscription satellite broadcaster Sky has revealed today that it’s done a deal with Amazon to bring the Amazon Prime Video app to Sky Q digital receivers and Now TV boxes. In fact, the update that adds the Amazon Prime app to Sky devices has already started rolling out across Europe as of this morning (December 14).

Happily, too, the Amazon Prime Video app will support 4K and HDR via Sky Q right out of the gate, joining similar levels of AV support from the Netflix and Disney+ apps that have also been available on Sky Q boxes for some time now.

Do remember, though, that in order to enjoy HDR from a Sky Q box, your Sky Q will need to be a compatible model, and your TV will need to be able to handle the HLG HDR format over its HDMI ports. Note, too, that there’s no support via the Sky Q receiver for the ‘premium’ HDR10+ or Dolby Vision formats that Amazon Prime Video is capable of delivering to compatible TVs via their built-in Amazon apps. 

Amazon Prime Video was the last of the main streaming platforms in the UK not to have a berth on Sky Q, so adding it now means the broadcaster has taken another big step towards its goal of turning its Sky Q satellite receivers into one-stop home entertainment centers, where subscribers can go to access all their streaming as well as broadcast needs.

Amazon Prime is a particularly useful addition for Sky, too, since the streaming service now carries a substantial number of live Premiership football matches. In fact, if you subscribe to Amazon Prime, BT Sport (which is also available on Sky Q) and Sky’s own Sports package, your Sky Q box will now give you access to every live Premiership football match being delivered under current licensing arrangements.

The Amazon Prime Video deal applies to Sky subscribers in the UK, Ireland, Germany, Austria, and Italy. Once your Sky Q Box has updated to receive Amazon Prime Video, shows and films from Amazon’s service will start to show up on the Sky Q ‘recommended’ page, alongside Sky Originals, Netflix and Disney+ highlights.

The new Sky Q/Amazon deal turns out to be a two-way affair, too, in that as well as finding Amazon Prime Video turning up on Sky Q and Now TV devices, the Now TV service is also arriving on Amazon Fire TV devices for the first time. In fact, Now TV should be added to the first-gen Fire TV Stick Lite, second and third generation Fire TV Stick, and Fire TV Stick 4K devices by the time you read this. It will be landing on other Fire TV devices in the UK, Ireland and Italy from early 2021, alongside the Sky Ticket service in Germany.

Stephen van Rooyen, EVP & CEO UK & Europe Sky, says this of the new Amazon deal:

“Sky Q customers are receiving an early Christmas present on their boxes this year in the form of Amazon Prime Video. Anyone who needs to get stuck into something new after The Undoing on Sky Atlantic will now be able to flip right over to The Boys on Prime Video, without ever leaving Sky Q. Amazon Prime Video TV shows, movies and more will sit alongside Sky Originals and Netflix, as well as apps like Disney+, BBC iPlayer, FiiT and much more. This will make it even easier for you to access everything you love, in one place.”
