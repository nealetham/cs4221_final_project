Is Amazon Going Shopping at the Mall?

Amazon would benefit from becoming a Mall Rat -- a brick and mortar presence could offer some distinct advantages to the online retail giant.
  • Author:
    Ed Ponsi
  • Publish date:
    May 12, 2020 8:30 AM EDT
Comments

On Monday morning, shares of AMC Entertainment Holdings (AMC) shot higher on rumors that Amazon (AMZN) was interested in purchasing the movie theater operator. AMC rocketed higher by over 60% in premarket trading, but pulled back and closed Monday with a 36% gain. Even after that gain, AMC is down over 22% for the year.

AMC is not only taking a hit from social distancing, it is also fighting with movie studios to continue to show films on a first-run basis. Universal Studios recently bypassed movie theaters when it released its Trolls World Tour movie on a pay-per-view only basis, and now AMC is threatening to ban the Comcast (CMCSA) subsidiary's new releases from its theaters.

The obvious benefit to Amazon would be an additional outlet for its films and programs. However, I'm focused on the secondary effects of any potential deal. For example, many of AMC's outlets are located in shopping malls, and the beaten-down names in that sector may present an opportunity.

Shopping malls had a questionable future even before the Covid-19 pandemic, but do they have a future? This chart of Simon Property Group (SPG) , the largest mall operator in the U.S., isn't pretty, but the REIT does appear to have found support in the high-$40s to low-$50s area (shaded). This REIT is universally hated right now, but so was AMC until recently.

Source: TradeStation

Simon Property Group is currently yielding over 15%, and you can bet that dividend will be suspended and/or cut. However, this is already priced in to some degree, as the REIT has fallen by 62% year-to-date.

Amazon doesn't have to buy AMC to establish a mall presence. Many U.S. malls have vacancies where Sears formerly stood. There are plenty of former Macy's (M) and JCPenney (JCP) locations available as well.

Every crisis creates an opportunity. For Amazon, one current opportunity is to establish a foothold in malls across the U.S. at a moment when it can negotiate from a position of strength. Malls are dying, and Amazon's involvement could save the American mall as we know it.

There are a variety of reasons for Amazon to move into malls. In addition to giving the retailer an outlet for its films, Amazon could use an increased mall presence to grow its Whole Foods Markets supermarket chain. It could also expand its Amazon Go store cashier-less shopping concept.

Perhaps most important, a mall presence would give Amazon shoppers a physical location to return products. This would be beneficial because a large percentage of customers who return products to a physical location go on to make additional purchases.

The lack of a major physical presence is a disadvantage for Amazon in its battle with Walmart (WMT) . Amazon currently accepts returns through Kohl's (KSS) , but would be much better served by actual brick-and-mortar locations.

At the time of publication, Ponsi was long WMT and AMZN.
