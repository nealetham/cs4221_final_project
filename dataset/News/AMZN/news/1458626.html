    SpaceX landing: Watch the moment Falcon 9 nails a high-speed landing in the Atlantic

    WATCH SpaceX's Falcon 9 rocket ace a landing in the Atlantic ocean just minutes after boosting two NASA astronauts into orbit.

    By Sebastian Kettley
    00:07, Wed, Jun 3, 2020 | UPDATED: 10:25, Wed, Jun 3, 2020
    Link copied Bookmark

    SpaceX: Live stream loses signal during Crew Dragon launch

    Elon Musk's SpaceXcompany has pioneered the aerospace industry with its reusable Falcon 9 rocket and now the launch of the Crew Dragon spacecraft. On Saturday, May 30, a solitary Falcon 9 blasted off from Cape Canaveral, Florida, carrying NASAastronauts Bob Behnken and Doug Hurley to the International Space Station (ISS). The launch marked the first flight of US astronauts into orbit from US soil in nine years.

    Trending

      The launch also marked a significant milestone for SpaceX as it was the first time astronauts flew into orbit aboard a private-build spacecraft.

      About two minutes and 40 seconds into the launch, the Falcon 9's main engines were cut off and the first stage separated from the second stage.

      As the second stage pushed the Dragon into a stable orbit, the first stage plummeted towards the Atlantic Ocean, aiming for a robotic landing platform dubbed Of Course I Still Love You.

      Part of the landing was caught on NASA and SpaceX's online broadcast on the day.

      In the SpaceX stream, you can see the Falcon 9 falling through the atmosphere, guided by latticed aerobrake fins on its sides.
