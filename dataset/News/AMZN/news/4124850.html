Our Verdict

The Discover it® Cash Back credit card is a solid cash-back card with a few limitations. Its customer service and first-year Cashback Match™ stand out as its best features, but its capped bonus rewards on rotating cash-back categories make it a second-tier rewards card.

The Discover it® Cash Back is a great card during the first year for almost everyone, due to its unique welcome bonus. Beyond the first year, this card is a good option for those who will remember to activate the card’s elevated cash-back categories and are okay to juggle this card with another card that offers a high flat-rate cash back on unbonused categories.

Discover it Cash Back Introduction

The Discover it® Cash Back is a great first jump into rewards-earning credit cards. The card offers solid rewards: Earn 5% cash back on everyday purchases at different places each quarter up to a quarterly maximum of $1,500 in spending when activated. Plus, earn unlimited 1% cash back on all other purchases. Discover will also match all cash back earned at the end of your first year as a cardholder, which could be more valuable than a traditional welcome bonus depending on how much you spend. The Discover it® Cash Back does not charge an annual fee.

Another benefit of carrying the Discover it® Cash Back is that there’s no minimum cash-back redemption amount. The card also comes with a 0% introductory APR for 15 months on purchases and eligible balance transfers. Then, a standard rate of 18.74% - 27.74% variable applies. A balance transfer fee of up to 5% of the amount transferred applies.

Who Is the Discover it Cash Back Best For?

The Discover it® Cash Back stands out in the first year with its appealing welcome bonus, making it an excellent choice for virtually all users during this period. After the initial year, it remains a solid choice for those who are diligent about activating and utilizing the card’s 5% cash-back quarterly rotating categories, up to $1,500 in spending each quarter. The card may continue to be beneficial for users who pair it with another card that provides a high flat rate of cash back on purchases that fall outside the bonus categories.

Discover It Cash Back Rewards

Earning Rewards

The Discover it® Cash Back earns 5% cash back on everyday purchases at different places each quarter up to a quarterly maximum of $1,500 in spending when activated. Plus, earn unlimited 1% cash back on all other purchases. Categories have historically included gas stations, restaurants, Target, grocery stores and more.

Additionally, Discover will automatically match all the cash back earned at the end of the first year as a cardmember. There's no minimum spending or maximum rewards.

Redeeming Rewards

Cash back can be redeemed for any amount at any time in the form of a statement credit or electronic deposit. Cash back can also be redeemed for charity or as gift cards with an added bonus on every card or directly at Amazon.com and PayPal. Cash-back rewards never expire.

Rewards Potential

To determine the rewards potential of the Discover it® Cash Back card, we have to look at the earning categories and calculate what an average American household might spend in those areas. Forbes Advisor uses data from various government agencies to determine both baseline income and spending averages across various categories. According to this data, the 70th percentile of wage earners brings in $118,438 annually and has $39,337 in spending they can charge to a credit card. We base the rewards potential on that number. In this scenario, we can assume the cardholder will spend up to the quarterly maximum of $1,500 to maximize the 5% cash back earned after activation.

Discover it Cash Back Introductory Rates

This card offers a competitive introductory APR period: 0% introductory APR for 15 months on purchases and eligible balance transfers. Then, a standard rate of 18.74% - 27.74% variable applies. A balance transfer fee of up to 5% of the amount transferred applies.

Discover It Cash Back Benefits

  • Freeze it®. Discover offers easy ways to freeze your card, should you lose it. Cardholders can turn their card on and off directly from the Discover app or online.
  • Monthly credit score. Your FICO Score is printed on your monthly statements and can be accessed anytime via the Discover app or website.

How To Maximize the Discover it Cash Back

The easiest way to maximize the Discover it® Cash Back is to use the card in its bonus categories up to the quarterly limit. For all other purchases, the Discover it® Cash Back can be tied in the first year as a cardholder with a 2% cash back card, and beat by the same card after the first year as a cardholder.

How the Discover it Cash Back Stacks Up

Discover it® Cash Back vs. Chase Freedom Flex®

The Chase Freedom Flex® also offers rotating 5% bonus categories but adds a few fixed bonus categories as well. Cardholders earn 5% cash back on up to $1,500 in combined purchases in categories that rotate quarterly (requires activation), 5% cash back on travel purchased through Chase Travel℠, 3% cash back on dining and drugstores and 1% cash back on all other purchases. The Freedom Flex also doesn’t charge an annual fee. The welcome offer on the Freedom Flex is more traditional, so you can’t maximize it in the same way as the Discover it® Cash Back: Earn a $200 bonus after spending $500 on purchases in the first 3 months from account opening. Similar to the Discover it® Cash Back, the Freedom Flex offers a 0% intro APR for 15 months from account opening on purchases and balance transfers, then a variable APR of 19.99% - 28.74% applies. Balance transfer fee of up to 5% (min. $5) of the amount of each transfer applies.

When paired with a premium Chase card like the Chase Sapphire Preferred® Card or Chase Sapphire Reserve®, points earned from the Freedom Flex are even more valuable as they can be transferred to partners like World of Hyatt and United’s MileagePlus or redeemed with a bump in value through Chase Travel℠.

The Freedom Flex is a much stronger offering when it comes to additional card perks. It has purchase protection, extended warranty protection, trip cancellation/interruption coverage, cellphone protection and a secondary auto rental collision damage waiver. Between the two cards, you can likely earn more cash back using the Freedom Flex and have greater flexibility when it comes to redemptions, especially if you have a Sapphire card or the Ink Business Preferred® Credit Card.

Discover it® Cash Back vs. Citi Double Cash® Card

The Citi Double Cash® Card is a great cash-back card for those who value simplicity. It offers 2% cash back on all purchases—1% when purchases are made and another 1% when they’re paid off, and earn 5% total cash back on hotel, car rentals and attractions booked on the Citi Travel℠ portal through 12/31/25, without charging an annual fee.

The Citi Double Cash card offers a 0% intro APR on balance transfers for 18 months. After that, the standard variable APR will be 18.49% - 28.49%, based on creditworthiness. An intro balance transfer fee of either $5 or 3%, whichever is greater, applies to transfers completed within the first 4 months of account opening. After that, the fee will be 5% of each transfer (minimum $5). If you are looking for a simple way to earn unlimited cash back, the Citi Double Cash is an attractive option.

Outside of earning cash back and a low intro APR period, the Citi Double Cash card doesn’t provide many benefits so you might get more value out of the Discover it® Cash Back. As we mentioned before, these cards could work very well in tandem so you might not need to choose between them.

Discover it® Cash Back vs. Blue Cash Preferred® Card from American Express

The Blue Cash Preferred® Card from American Express (Terms apply, see rates & fees) has a $0 intro annual fee for the first year, then $95. Some may find that the rewards structure more than makes up for the cost after year one. Cardholders will earn the following rewards: 6% cash back at U.S. supermarkets on up to $6,000 per year in purchases (then 1%), 6% cash back on select U.S. streaming subscriptions, 3% cash back at U.S. gas stations and on transit (including taxis/rideshare, parking, tolls, trains, buses and more) and 1% cash back on other eligible purchases. Cash back is received in the form of Reward Dollars that can be redeemed as a statement credit or at Amazon.com checkout.

The Blue Cash Preferred® Card from American Express offers a welcome bonus: Earn a $250 statement credit after spending $3,000 in eligible purchases within the first 6 months. Plus, it offers a 0% introductory APR for 12 months on purchases and balance transfers requested within the first 60 days of account opening, followed by a variable APR of 18.49%-29.49%. A balance transfer fee of $5 or 3%, whichever is greater, applies.

To help justify the annual fee, the Blue Cash Preferred offers a $7 monthly statement credit after spending $9.99 or more on an auto-renewing subscription to the Disney Bundle (enrollment required). In terms of insurance coverage, you can enjoy car rental loss and damage insurance¹, purchase protection², return protection² and extended warranty². Blue Cash Preferred cardholders can also benefit from Amex Offers.

Between the two cards, you can likely earn more cash back from the Blue Cash Preferred without having to worry with activating quarterly categories. Plus, you can mostly offset the annual fee with the up to $84 annual Disney Bundle credit. However, if you don’t want to subscribe to the Disney Bundle, you might find it hard to justify the annual fee, even considering the elevated earnings.

Pro Tip
Depending on your spending patterns, the Discover it Cash Back and Blue Cash Preferred Card from American Express could be a solid card combination.

Is the Discover it Cash Back for You?

If you’re looking for a no annual fee rewards card that can earn high rewards in certain areas of spending, the Discover it® Cash Back card could be a worthy option. But this is a card best paired with one that earns higher rewards than 1% on all your spending outside of the quarterly bonus categories.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Is the Discover it Cash Back a hard card to get?

We estimate most applicants will require a good or better credit rating to receive approval for the Discover it® Cash Back card. This means a hopeful applicant should apply with a credit score of 670 or better on the FICO scale.

How long does it take for Discover it Cash Back to get approved?

Some applications may return a near-instantaneous result, approving or denying your request within a few minutes. If for any reason your application requires review or a closer look, you’ll receive a decision in writing within 30 days.

Does Discover It Cash Back offer a cash-back match every year?

Discover’s Cashback Match is an alternatively-styled welcome bonus—it may only be earned for the first year of card ownership.

What is the cash back limit on the Discover it Cash Back?

While there’s no specific limit to the overall amount of cash back you can earn, the rewards earned in quarterly rotating bonus categories are capped. The maximum reward you may earn annually in the quarterly rotating bonus categories is currently $300.

What is the Discover Cash Back Calendar?

The Discover 5% Cash Back Calendar illustrates the rotating quarterly categories, showing the categories the card will earn 5% in for each quarter of the year.
