When is Black Friday 2019? Date, the UK stores with deals and why it’s called Black Friday

The biggest day of discounts and deals will be back this year. Here's everything we know so far

By Sally Hickey
November 28, 2019 8:51 am(Updated October 14, 2020 5:24 pm)

Another year brings with it another Black Friday, so if you’re on the hunt for a bargain here’s everything you need to know.

Black Friday is a day of discounts for high street shops and online retailers. It originated in the US, where folklore tells of American retailers spending the first 11 months of the year losing money (“in the red”), and only turned their losses into a profit on the day after Thanksgiving. Their balance sheets would then be “in the black”, leading to the name Black Friday.

Although it’s not officially linked to Christmas, there is an emphasis on buying your Christmas presents on the day. According to a 2018 YouGov poll, 29 per cent of Britons plan to purchase their Christmas gifts in November.

It has become slightly less popular in the UK over the past few years though, with credit card data from Barclays – which processes nearly half of the UK’s credit and debit card transactions – showing that there was a 12 per cent drop in the amount spent on Black Friday in 2018 compared to 2017. There was, however, a 10 per cent rise in the number of transactions.

When is Black Friday 2019?

This year, Black Friday is on 29 November. It’s always the day after Thanksgiving, which is the American public holiday on the fourth Thursday of the month.

The sale originally lasted for 24 hours, but now most retailers have a week of discounts starting the Monday before. There’s also Cyber Monday on 2 December, which will see further reductions online.

Black Friday is an American shopping tradition, where retailers begin their Christmas season discounts the day after Thanksgiving, which is given to some workers as an additional day off.

It has only caught on in the UK over the past few years. Amazon was one of the early adopters, running discounts way back in 2010. It’s sometimes credited (or accused) of bringing Black Friday to the UK.

Which shops normally take part?

All British supermarkets, with the exception of Marks & Spencer, and the majority of high street retailers offer deals on Black Friday. Argos, Amazon, John Lewis, Tesco and Very all take part.

M&S no longer offer special discounts on this day, with CEO Steve Rowe stating that all it does is “suck sales forward”. M&S added that there are a huge range of deals and special offers on the website in the approach to Christmas though.

What deals to look out for

There will be plenty of deals in the week leading up to Black Friday, with Amazon often starting discounts at least seven days in advance. Argos, John Lewis, Currys, Tesco and Very all usually have good discounts available too, on everything from laptops to TVs to kettles, while Boots and Next usually have great fashion and beauty offers.
