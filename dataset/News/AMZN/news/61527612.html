Cyber Monday deals 2024: Key info

When is Cyber Monday 2024?

Cyber Monday 2024 will fall on December 2nd but there will be plenty of deals to consider well ahead of this date. For the record, Cyber Monday always takes place after the weekend following Black Friday - which itself immediately follows Thanksgiving.

Both Black Friday and Cyber Monday started as one-day-events but in recent years we've seen a gradual shift towards retailers offering much more expansive sales. Right now, for example we've already seen early deals from Amazon, Samsung, Dell, Target, and many other retailers.

Generally speaking, some of these early Cyber Monday deals can be worth your time and we usually recommend our readers start shopping from early November onwards.

Adobe's wide-ranging 2023 shopping forecast report found that early discounts averaged at 12 to 20% off around November 1st, followed by further reductions of up to 30% in the week preceding Black Friday. I wouldn't treat this as the gospel, however, as the early deals tend to get better each year.

Cyber Monday deals 2024: FAQ

Is Cyber Monday better than Black Friday?

In terms of deal quality - not really. Both events feature relative parity when it comes to overall discounts. You may see a few Cyber Monday specials and visa versa, but generally speaking you'll see the same things on sale with similar prices.

We've been covering both events for years here at TechRadar and we can confidently say you'll be able to get great tech deals on both days. It used to be the case that Black Friday was more focused on brick-and-mortar sales while Cyber Monday was more online-based, but that's simply not the case now. Both are primarily online-focused these days - and you don't need to rush out to find a great deal. As previously mentioned, you may even have a few days to consider your purchase and do some research.

In terms of spend, Cyber Monday actually exceeds Black Friday at $12.4B vs $9.98B, which we'd put down to the 'fear of missing out' factor and the fact that consumers are more aware that they have time to research now.

Once discounted on Black Friday, retailers don't tend to further discount items on Cyber Monday, although we do often see 'flash sales' or something similar. These don't tend to be on massively popular tech like iPads or Air Pods, but they do sometimes cover more nice things like vacuums or cameras. Amazon in particular loves a 'lightning deal' so keep an eye out for those throughout the period.
