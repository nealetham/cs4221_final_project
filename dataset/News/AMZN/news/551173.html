What does chicken, casinos & my dog have in common? 10 coronavirus observations

  • Published: Apr. 22, 2020, 3:06 p.m.
By
  • Kevin Minnick | NJ Advance Media for NJ.com

Here we are approaching the end of April, a little more than a month since the coronavirus outbreak has created a much different normal.

In the past, this would have been the time when spring sports really took off at the high school level. I would have been fully engaged in helping prepare for the annual Joe Hartmann Diamond Classic in South Jersey. The annual Woodbury Relays would have been held in track and field, while my son would have been teeing it up for his golf team.

The new normal has forced people to think outside the box. Coaches have shared virtual workouts for their players, programs have done some extra homework and developed All-Decade teams in their respective sports. Teachers through the state have been challenged to deliver online lessons.

Needless to say, it's been ... well, a learning experience for everyone. Change is often tough to digest and this type is like none anyone has every lived through.

While we continue to battle this unknown enemy, there is a light at the end of the tunnel. It may take a while to see it at its brightest, but it is there.

My situation is like so many others, while it surely has its unique features, too. Here are 10 things I've learned an/or observed since the outbreak.

Teachers do the craziest things

With schools closed, students have been on their computers a lot longer each day with online learning. Teachers have gone out of their way to re-invent themselves, keeping their students engaged with a variety of methods. I never knew oysters were farmed or college kids could make serious cash playing video games. To be honest, Iâm learning something new every day.

Full house for dinner

When you're a sports reporter, you often miss sitting at the table with your family at dinner time. We usually don't return from afternoon games until after dark, while we generally leave early for Friday night football or that big basketball showdown. Coaches know what I mean. Now I can't remember the last time I haven't had dinner with the family.

Better appreciation for trash guys

I don't know about you, but I think I've taken out the kitchen trash just about every day in recent weeks. If not, guaranteed at least every other day. There were times in the past when I'd have the recycling can and one trash can on the curb. It feels like it takes an hour to walk from the side of the house to the curb nowadays. Maybe a couple Wawa gift cards are in order.

Rummy can be addictive

I've got admit, there are a few card games I've rarely played. Rummy was one of them before the outbreak. In the last weeks, it's been a daily activity. My strategy has changed and to say it's become competitive is probably an understatement. Maybe I should put in a call to the Guinness Book of World Records for most decks shuffled in a single day.

Let me tell you about chicken

While I never really thought about it too deeply, you've got to be kidding me with the voluminous ways in which one can consume chicken. Chicken strips, chicken sandwiches, chicken cordon bleu, chicken salad, Notre Dame chicken (a recipe from a friend that includes mushrooms, wild rice and a few more items of the vegetable variety). Then there's grilled chicken, baked chicken, chicken parm. Need I go on?

Craps, backjack and roulette

My oldest son recently turned 21 and was looking forward to playing a few table games in Atlantic City. With a handful of casino visits already under his belt (you need only be 18 in England and Australia, 19 in Canada), he was feeling confident. When the casinos closed their doors ... "That's a tough one. Casinos never close their doors," he said. So if he couldn't go to the casino, we found a way to bring the casino to him. Amazon truly has everything. Instead of high stakes poker, we played for jelly beans and Extra Toasty Cheez-Its.

Itâs hard to believe all of these dogs rarely saw anything but their backyards. And as far as the people outside walking, I couldnât tell you who most of them are.

Youâre my neighbor?

In what was a strange twist of fate, I've come to find out that a guy who was my baseball coach when I was maybe 8, 9 years old actually lives on the same street. It all stems from walking Lucky. I saw this guy in front of his house talking with a neighbor and didn't bat an eye in recognizing him. "Hey Fred, I didn't know you lived there." Usually working opposite schedules, we never ran into each other. Now ... he beeped and waved just the other day.

Rising to the occasion

One of the first stories I wrote when shifted to the news department was on Food4Staff. In what started as a simple text, a Washington Township family thought it would be nice to raise a little bit of money and use it to feed some local health care workers. Well, that small gesture has exploded into one of the biggest charitable groups around. Its Facebook page has over 11,600 members and it is providing meals for hundreds each day in New Jersey, Pennsylvania and Delaware.
