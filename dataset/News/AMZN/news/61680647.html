PS5, Baby Yoda, PJ Mask HQ and 10 other Christmas toys you (probably) can’t find

  • Published: Dec. 09, 2020, 3:57 p.m.
By
  • Troy L. Smith, Cleveland.com

CLEVELAND, Ohio – The holiday season can be (and should be) a time of joy and merriment. Unless you’re a parent who can’t find that one soldout toy your child has been asking you for.

Maybe you waited too long or, perhaps, the item was just so popular, it has you thinking back to the Tickle Me Elmo craze that had everyone fending for themselves in Toys R Us.

Some of the most popular items on this year’s toy list are more expensive than ever. But that hasn’t stopped them from selling out. Any parent who has been asked for a gaming console or something involving “Baby Yoda” knows exactly what we’re talking about.

Here are 13 must-have items that your kids (probably) can’t have unless you’re willing to pay a hefty price. We mixed in a few items for the child in all us adults as well.

Star Wars the Child Animatronic Edition

Yes, Baby Yoda actually has a name. But if you haven’t seen the latest episodes of Disney+’s “The Mandalorian,” we won’t spoil it for you. Still, items featuring the cute guy previously known as “The Child” are hot items this Christmas. And atop that list is the animatronic version that makes it feel lifelike. Walmart and Target would be the ideal locations to get him, but most stores are either sold out or in low stock. Walmart has just a few left for in-store purchases. Amazon has it available from secondary retailers.

Xbox Series X/S

So you want an Xbox Series X or Series S? Good luck with that. The new game console from Microsoft has been pretty much sold out since it arrived thanks to people scooping them up and selling them on secondary markets. As of this week, Walmart’s pages for the items were coming up empty with out of stock messages. Both the Xbox Series X and Series S are “unavailable” on Amazon with the wording, “We don’t know when or if this item will be back in stock.” Target and GameStop are also out of stock. The same goes for Best Buy. Both consoles will cost you several hundred dollars on eBay if you’re willing to pay the price.

PS5

Take pretty much everything we wrote for the new Xbox consoles and insert it here for the PlayStation 5. Amazon is sold out and uncertain when it will be back in stock. The same goes for Target, Best Buy and Walmart. If a restock does come for any of those sites, you’ll have to act fast. And in case you’re wondering, the PS5 costs even more on eBay than the Xbox Series X.

Nintendo Switch

The Nintendo Switch maybe three years old, but it’s still popular. And if that’s your gaming console of choice, you’re in luck. The Switch and Switch Lite are in stock at Amazon, GameStop, Target, Best Buy and a few other retailers, but only while supplies last (which won’t be long). The Lite costs around $229.99, while the regular Switch comes in at just under $300.

PJ Masks Transformer 2 in 1 Mobile HQ

Those pesky PJ Masks have become so popular, it’s hard getting your hands on the most coveted toy from the series, which airs on Disney, this year. The Transformer 2 in 1 Mobile HQ is only available from secondary retailers on Amazon. Target has a few in stock for less. Walmart and Kohls are out of stock.

Hess Truck 2020 Ambulance and Rescue

Yes, Hess Trucks are still a thing and they’re still VERY popular. The 2020 Ambulance and Rescue pair of trucks are sold out on the Hess Toy Truck’s website. It’s also unavailable on Amazon. Though, Walmart is offering stock updates.

Vtech KidiZoom Creator Cam

Vtech’s KidiZoom Creator Cam is built for future Tiktok stars. But it’s hard to find. The camera, marketed toward kids ages 5 to 10 is sold out at Target, Walmart and Best Buy. It’s only available from secondary retailers on Amazon.

Squeakee The Balloon Dog

Yes, a balloon dog that you can feed and teach to do tricks (and pop!) is one of the hottest items for this holiday season. You can’t get it directly from Amazon. Though, Target and Walmart do have a limited supply starting at $59.99.

AirPods Max

The Apple Store is already sold out of the new AirPods Max. They’ll be back in stock online on Dec. 15 but won’t ship for another 12 to 14 weeks. Target and Best Buy list the noise-canceling headphones as “Coming Soon.” You might want to go ahead and cross these off your Christmas list.

LEGO Super Mario Adventures with Mario Starter Course

The most popular LEGO item of 2020 may very well be this Mario Starter Course building set. Walmart has just a handful left online.Target says “limited stock.” Amazon offers secondary buying options. Why is it so popular? Well, it pairs with an app that offers virtual coins. But since Mario and Luigi are celebrating 35 years, adults see the item as a collectible.

Healthy Roots Dolls: Zoe

The most popular doll of 2020 is Zoe, which comes from the Black-owned women’s brand Healthy Roots. Kids can use real hair products on the doll to create styles. Zoe is so popular, she’s sold out. Pre-orders won’t begin shipping until after the holidays.

Little Live Pets Gotta Go Flamingo

A bird that poops. And 2-year-olds can’t get enough of it. This pooping flamingo, which also talks and sings, is a hot item. It’s back in stock on Dec. 23 on Amazon but won’t arrive until after Christmas. Walmart is offering in-stock alerts.

Star Wars Mandalorian Darksaber Lightsaber

Last, but not least, “The Mandalorian” strikes again. The show debuted a popular item in the Star Wars Universe. Now, every kid and fanboy wants the Darksaber. But you’ll have to work (or pay up) to find it. Amazon doesn’t have it. Walmart has it for in-store purchases only. Target has very few left.
