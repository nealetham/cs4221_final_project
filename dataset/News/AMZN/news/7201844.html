Where can I watch Premier League matches for free? Every TV match and free-to-air details

Two batches of fixtures up to mid-July have had the details of which broadcaster will be showing them confirmed so far

    The Premier League has returned with all 92 remaining fixtures being shown on TV by various broadcasters.

    And the action got back under way when Sheffield United took on Aston Villa in what turned out to be a goalless draw at Villa Park.

    Manchester City against Arsenal followed that at the Etihad Stadium with a comfortable 3-0 win for the hosts against a 10-man Gunners side after David Luiz’s second half dismissal.

    Both games, like all those due to be played in the rest of the 2019/20 season, are taking place behind closed doors in light of the coronavirus pandemic, with fans watching the action from home.

    All Premier League games remaining this campaign are being shown by broadcasters including Sky Sports, BT Sport, Amazon Prime Video and the BBC.

    In addition to this, 33 of these fixtures will be shown free-to-air with no need for a subscription.

    These include 25 which will be shown on Sky Pick - channel 144 on Freeview and 159 on Sky - as well as the eight games being shown by Amazon Prime and the BBC.

    Matches between now and up to Monday, July 13, have now had their broadcast details confirmed and only the last three rounds of fixtures are still to have these confirmed.

    Which free Premier League games are on BBC Sport?

    The BBC will show live Premier League matches for the first time in its history when the action resumes.

    They will have a total of four fixtures in the remaining weeks of the 2019/20 campaign, all of which will be shown for free.

    Bournemouth's clash with Newcastle on Saturday, June 20 and Norwich's encounter with Everton four days later are the two BBC picks confirmed from the opening three rounds of Premier League fixtures after the restart.

    And Southampton v Manchester City on Sunday, July 5, is the third match to be shown on terrestrial television by the broadcaster.

    Are BT Sport showing any free matches?

    BT Sport will show a total of 20 of the remaining 92 league games.

    However, the channel will not be making any of them available to watch for free.
