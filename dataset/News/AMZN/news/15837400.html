Packaging waste from online retailers to grow by a third

George Morahan
03/12/2018

Packaging waste from online retailers generated by consumers in Ireland will increase by more than third this year, according to a new report from Repak.

The recycling and waste management company says Ireland will generate more than 10,000 tonnes of packaging waste from online shopping, up from 7,500 tonnes last year.

The report shows that Irish shoppers import 27 tonnes of packaging everyday with their purchases from sites such as eBay and AliExpress.

Repak says of the online retail giants only Amazon is contributing towards the annual €560,000 bill for collecting and recycling this packaging, which is being passed onto its members.

Repak chief executive Seamus Clancy praised Amazon for showing ‘leadership with its environmental responsibilities’ but said the situation would continue to get ‘dramatically worse’ without government intervention.

At the current rate, the volume of online packaging sent to Ireland will exceed 100,000 tonnes by 2031. Repak is now calling on the Department of Communications, Climate Action and Environment to set up an online retail forum to address the problem.

‘What is considered a significant problem within the recycling industry and retail industry today, will soon become overwhelming in time,’ Mr Clancy said.

‘Online shopping is outpacing the growth in traditional retail by more than double. The longer we wait to enforce reasonable and sustainable recycling legislation for online retailers the more unfair it becomes for Irish businesses contributing to our recycling effort.’

Irish shoppers will spend €2bn online on some 33m different consumer this year, with clothing and electronics proving to be the most popular products.
