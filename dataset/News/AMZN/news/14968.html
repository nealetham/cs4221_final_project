7 best student credit cards of November 2024

CNBC Select analyzed the top student credit cards to find the best ones for various spending habits.

Updated Fri, Nov 15 2024
Jason Stauffer@/in/jason-stauffer-224b7398/
Alexandria White
Share

A good credit score is essential when making many of the major life decisions you face after graduation, such as renting an apartment or applying for an auto loan. Opening a college student credit card is a smart way to start building credit early while taking advantage of rewards and special financing offers.

Student cards provide useful benefits for different types of students from travelers and foodies to commuters. Some cards are lenient with credit history requirements, meaning you may qualify with no credit history. You do have to be 18 to apply for a credit card and need a steady source of income.

If you're feeling overwhelmed by all the choices, don't stress. CNBC Select, dug into various cards' perks and drawbacks, to find the best student credit cards based on your spending habits. (See ourmethodology for more information on how we choose the best student credit cards.)

    What is a student credit card?

    A student credit card is a card designed for college students because students are more likely to have a limited credit history. This type of credit card typically offers weaker rewards and smaller intro bonuses, if there's any welcome bonus or rewards at all. However, they provide an opportunity for students to build credit while they're still in school.

    How to compare student credit cards

    To compare student credit cards, you'll want to take a look at the fine print to fully understand the benefits, fees and APR. The first fee to consider is the annual fee. If a credit card has an annual fee, you'll pay that every year regardless of whether or not you use the card. The card's APR and other types of fees (late payment fee, return payment fee, etc.) are important to be aware of. However, if you plan on missing payments or accumulating credit card debt and paying interest, then you may be better off skipping credit cards and using a debit card for now.

    One of the best perks of having a student credit card is being able to earn rewards on your spending. Look at how rewarding each card is and factor in your spending habits. Some student credit cards come with an opportunity to earn a healthy intro bonus, which is an important aspect to compare.

    How to choose a student credit card

    To choose the right student credit card for you, first think about what you want from a credit card. Are you trying to earn rewards for a trip home? Then a travel rewards card might be best. Or do you want an interest-free period to finance a new computer purchase? In that case, a card with an introductory 0%-APR offer could be ideal.

    Once you've honed in on what you want in a card, it's much easier to identify the best fit for your needs. It's also important to pay attention to more than just the card's perks. Credit cards have fees and charge interest when you carry a balance. These costs can easily offset the perks of having the card if you're not careful. For example, a no foreign transaction fee card is essential if you plan on studying abroad because otherwise, these fees can quickly add up.

    How to make the best of your student credit card

    To make the most of your student credit card you'll need to understand everything available. If you know what purchases your card earns the most on, you can make sure you're using it on the right transactions. And if your card has a welcome bonus offer, it's vital to know what you need to do to earn that bonus.

    One of the biggest benefits of having a credit card as a college student is the opportunity it provides for you to build your credit. However, to do this effectively you'll need to be sure to pay your credit card bill on time each month and pay off the balance in full. Over time, doing this will slowly improve your credit score and boost your chances of getting a better deal when you need to open a credit card or borrow money later on.

    FAQs

    Should college students have credit cards?

    Having a credit card can help you build your credit history, raise your credit score and teach you how to manage money. When used responsibly, student credit cards can save you money through the rewards you earn on your purchases. Student credit cards also provide more security through their various built-in protections.

    How can a student with no income get a credit card?

    A student with no income can get a credit card by applying with a qualified cosigner or by being added as an authorized user under a parent's card. If they are over 21 years of age, students are also able to apply for credit cards using their household income.

    Which type of credit card is best for students?

    The best type of credit card for students is a card that can earn rewards and help you build credit at the same time. It's even better if you can qualify for a card that earns extra rewards in categories where you spend the most.

    Are student cards good for building credit?

    Student credit cards can be a good way to build credit because they are easier to qualify for than a typical credit card. But for a student credit card to help your credit, you'll need to pay your balance in full and on time every month.

    Are student credit cards hard to get?

    Student credit cards are generally easier to qualify for than many of the best credit cards. However, the application may require proof of college enrollment in addition to the standard information required for a credit card application.

    Bottom line

    Getting a student credit card can help you establish a credit history, build your credit score and earn rewards along the way. And there are cards tailored to the common needs of college students, such as cards that earn bonus rewards for dining or gas.
