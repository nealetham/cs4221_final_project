Amazon begins online pharmacy, drug delivery services in Japan

TOKYO - E-commerce giant Amazon.com Inc. on Tuesday started online prescriptions and medicine delivery services in Japan in cooperation with some 2,500 drug stores across the country, the retailer's Japan unit said.

Through the Amazon Pharmacy services, already available outside Japan, consumers can obtain electronic prescriptions after diagnosis at hospitals and clinics or remotely online.

----------

Tokyo-Osaka bullet train service resumes after partial all-day halt

TOKYO - Bullet trains connecting Tokyo and Osaka resumed full service from the first run on Tuesday, after an accident involving railway maintenance vehicles caused an all-day suspension on part of the Tokaido Shinkansen Line a day earlier.

JR Central completed restoration work late Monday night and increased the number of trains in the morning as part of the response to the disruption.

----------

Kobayashi Pharma chairman, president resign over supplements scandal

TOKYO - Kobayashi Pharmaceutical Co. said Tuesday its chairman and president are stepping down to take responsibility for a health hazard scandal linked to its red yeast rice dietary supplements that are suspected to have caused deaths.

The resignation of Chairman Kazumasa Kobayashi, 84, and President Akihiro Kobayashi, 53, who both hail from the founding family, comes as the company released an investigation report over its handling of the issue, acknowledging delays in alerting consumers over the potential risks and recalling their products.

----------

Japan imposes sanctions on Israeli settlers in West Bank for 1st time

TOKYO - The Japanese government on Tuesday decided to freeze the assets of four Israeli settlers in the West Bank for violence against Palestinians, in the first such sanction of its kind by the country.

The move comes amid a sharp rise in violence in the occupied West Bank since the Palestinian militant group Hamas carried out a surprise attack on Israel on Oct. 7, Japan's top government spokesman Yoshimasa Hayashi said.

----------

Taiwan's Lai inspects 1st annual military drill as island leader

HUALIEN, Taiwan - Taiwanese leader Lai Ching-te inspected an annual military exercise on Tuesday, the first since he assumed the presidency in May, as part of preparations for a possible Chinese invasion.

At an air base in the eastern county of Hualien, Lai observed the military's overall air defense, emergency runway repair capabilities and an area where wounded are treated.

----------

Blinken to make clear U.S. priority on Asia intact in upcoming trip

WASHINGTON - U.S. Secretary of State Antony Blinken will speak "loud and clear" during an upcoming 11-day trip that Washington's commitment to Asia remains unchanged, regardless of President Joe Biden's decision to end his reelection bid, the country's top diplomat for the region said Monday.

"I think the message that the secretary is going to be conveying to the region is that America is all in on the Indo-Pacific," said Daniel Kritenbrink, assistant secretary of state for East Asian and Pacific affairs.

----------

U.S. forces to exchange views with Okinawans after sex crimes

TOKYO - U.S. Forces Japan said Monday it will create a "new forum for cooperation" with Okinawa's government and local residents, amid criticism over recent revelations of cases of alleged sexual assault involving U.S. military members in the southern island prefecture.

The forum, to be launched in coordination with Japan's central government, will "serve as a venue for the constructive exchange of ideas in the pursuit of shared goals," U.S. Forces Japan Commander Lt. Gen. Ricky Rupp said in a press release.

----------

Harris secures enough delegates to replace Biden in presidential race

WASHINGTON - U.S. Vice President Kamala Harris on Monday gained the support of a majority of Democratic delegates to become their party's standard-bearer in the race for the White House, while also setting a record single-day fundraising total.

A day after President Joe Biden abandoned his reelection bid, endorsing Harris to become his successor, she has already secured the backing of far more than the 1,976 delegates to the party's national convention required to win the nomination in the first round of balloting, according to an Associated Press survey.
