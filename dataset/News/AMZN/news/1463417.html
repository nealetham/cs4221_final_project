Sky Sports and BT unveil new ways for fans to watch Premier League return

The broadcasters are launching new innovations to help supporters watch together and bring the matchday experience into viewers' home after first batch of fixtures was confirmed

Sky Sports and BT have unveiled new ways for fans to enjoy the Premier League action when England's top flight resumes later this month.

On Friday the Premier League confirmed the first batch of fixtures to be held from June 17 to July 2, as well as kick-off times and television details.

With matches having to be held behind closed doors due to the coronavirus pandemic, all 92 remaining top-flight games are to be televised.

And main broadcasters Sky Sports and BT Sport have announced a range of innovations aimed at enhancing the experience of watching from home and combating concerns over a lack of atmosphere with no supporters in the stadia.

Perhaps most interesting is that both broadcasters will launch new features whereby fans will be able to see and interact with friends while watching the games - just like they'd be able to if they were at the game or watching in a pub.

Sky Sports Fanzone will let viewers watch select matches with friends in a video room and interact while the action unfolds, giving them the chance to chat about the match and influence the crowd noise they hear on screen.

Users of the app can post predictions, join in-match polls and use the Sky Sports stats to fuel the virtual conversation.

Similarly, BT Sport are launching Watch Together, which will allow fans to see and chat with friends in a split screen while watching the match.

BT Sport viewers will also have the option to watch with or without a pre-recorded dynamic crowd noise feature, using the red button.

Sky Sports will offer a similar service to their customers. Sky Sports Crowds - run in association with EA Sports - will provide bespoke and team-specific crowd noise and chants to replicate the atmosphere of Premier League clashes. Viewers will be able to select the Sky channel to watch with the added sound or without it.

The broadcaster is also launching Sky Sports Recap, which will offer viewers the chance to catch up on key highlights during live matches in a short burst.

Each game will have a live timeline enabling viewers to quickly catch up on the highlights at any point during the match, even if they have not watched it from the beginning.

Sky Sports is set to broadcast 64 matches 39 exclusive to subscribers and an extra 25 available on Skys free-to-air channel Pick.
