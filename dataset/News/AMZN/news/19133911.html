Amazon Prime offers some great horror movie choices to fill a fright flick marathon, and these are the best Amazon Prime horror movies 2022 has to offer. While horror has long been regarded as a lesser genre by those inclined to snobbery, audiences at large seem to be finally waking up to horror's merits — as more and more horror (or horror adjacent) movies and TV shows earn rave reviews and/or huge profits at the box office. Almost every studio and streaming service has gotten into the horror content game, from crossover hits like Stranger Things, to cult items like Into the Dark.

Every streaming service offers its own selection, and Amazon Prime is no different. The service features many terrific terror tales, both old and new. From iconic classics to indie gems, the best horror movies on Amazon Prime match up well with the many competing services out there.

Related: Every Horror Movie Coming In Summer 2022

Presented below is a selection of the best horror movies on Amazon Prime, representing a range of decades and sub-genres. The Amazon Prime horror movies listed below are put in alphabetical order and are not ranked. They're all great films, and it's difficult to go wrong with any of them.

A Quiet Place Part II

✕ Remove Ads

It's not necessary to see A Quiet Placeto enjoy A Quiet Place Part II. The second movie gives enough of a recap to those who aren't familiar with the franchise. The movie follows the same family as they try to navigate a post-apocalyptic world riddled with creatures that kill and hunt by sound. John Krasinski and Emily Blunt return for their roles as Lee and Evelyn Abbott. In addition, the sequel offers more backstory than the first film, showing the very start of the Earth's alien problem and population losses. This horror movie, offered on Amazon Prime, has plenty of jump scares and boasts a thriller-like quality as the stakes are raised in every scene.

Black Swan

✕ Remove Ads

Black Swan is perfect for Amazon Prime subscribers seeking more Natalie Portman movies after her stand-out portrayal of Jane Foster in Thor: Love and Thunder.2010's Black Swanis a psychological thriller by director Darren Aronofsky. Black Swan centers on Portman's character, aspiring ballet dancer Nina Sayers, and the impact the oppressive and toxic world of professional dance has on her psyche. Portman's performance secured her an Oscar for Best Actress. Natalie Portman's performance as Nina and BlackSwan's ending aren't the only selling points though. Aronofsky landed the Oscar for Best Director in 2011 with Black Swan,and the movie also scooped Best Picture, Best Film Editing, and Best Cinematography the same year. Black Swanis one of the most impressive horror movies on Amazon Prime – one to watch even for people that aren't usually fans of psychological horror.

✕ Remove Ads

Carrie

Carrie is a classic fright-fest based on the Stephen King novel of the same name. Both the remake and the original horror movies are available to watch on Amazon Prime, and the Sissy Spacek version is definitely a sight to behold. The actress puts her best foot forward as the painfully awkward high school student Carrie White, who discovers that she has telekinetic powers. Piper Laurie's performance as her overzealously religious and abusive mother is one for the ages, and it's hard not to sympathize with Stephen King's Carrie as a protagonist/villain by the end. Those that haven't seen the iconic film are in for a treat, as the troubled teen eventually takes on her whole school in a fit of vengeance.

Saint Maud

Saint Maud is one of the best Amazon Prime horror movies currently available. From StudioCanalUK, the studio behind breakout hit Midsommar, 2020'sSaint Maud bucks the horror movie curse of low rankings outside of genre fans. Rose Glass's directorial debut is a psychological horror starring Morfydd Clark as the titular Maud. Saint Maudfollows Maud as she joins a palliative care facility on the English coast, and she believes it's her duty to save the souls of the various residents due to her strong Catholic faith. This leads Maud down a dark path, made all the more harrowing by Saint Maud's widely praised score, casting, and directorial approach by Glass. Despite its release being delayed in the U.S., Saint Maud was incredibly well-received. Saint Maud has an 83% score on Metacritic and 93% on Rotten Tomatoes – an Amazon Prime must-watch for both horror fans and discerning cinephiles seeking their annual scare.

Candyman (2021) 

Candyman (2021) continues the Hollywood tradition of horror franchise reboots. The Jordan-Peele-scribed reboot of the 1992 classic Candymanis now on Amazon Prime, and it's one to watch for fans of the original and newcomers to the franchise alike. Candyman (2021)isn't a subversive psychological nailbiter like some other modern horror flicks, such as Get Outor His House,but it was never trying to be. Nia DaCosta's revamp captures the jump-scare fun of the original 1992 Candyman, finely walking the line between legitimate terror and astute sociopolitical commentary. If you're looking to fill 90 minutes with a supernatural menace that harkens back to the golden age of slasher-horror through a timely and urgent lense, Candyman (2021)is one of the best candidates available on Amazon Prime right now.

The Exorcist 3

The Exorcist has received several awful follow-ups, but one that stands out as worthwhile is 1990's The Exorcist 3, which has developed a large cult following over the years and is now possessing Amazon Prime. George C. Scott takes over the role of Lt. Kinderman, who is investing a demonically possessed serial killer called Gemini. Jason Miller, Father Karras in the original, also makes a welcome return. The Exorcist 3is inspired by real events, just likeThe Exorcist. However, the third outing is a supernatural take on The Zodiac Killer, instead of a possession case. Viewers should be warned that The Exorcist 3 contains arguably the most effective jump scare in history.

✕ Remove Ads

Hellraiser

By this point, any dedicated horror fan has surely had their soul torn apart multiple times by Hellraiser's Pinhead (Doug Bradley). Director Clive Barker, adapting his own novella, crafted one of the most enduring tales of terror to come out of the 1980s. The movie spawned one of the longest-running horror franchises in existence. Hellraiser is a certified classic of the genre, one of the best horror movies on Amazon Prime, and a necessary watch for anyone who enjoys a good fright flick. It's the perfect time to be introduced to the franchise too since the upcoming Hellraiser reboot is one of the most anticipated horror movies in 2022.

The Poughkeepsie Tapes (2007)

The Poughkeepsie Tapesis one of the most notorious found-footage horror movies ever made. Despite debuting on the film festival circuit in 2007 to wide acclaim, The Poughkeepsie Tapesseemingly vanished for several years before reemerging on streaming services like Amazon Prime. It's a harrowing movie that pushes viewers to their limits and is definitely one to watch for fans of extreme horror, adding some true-crime elements for extremely disturbing verisimilitude. The Poughkeepsie Tapeswere purportedly left by a serial killer, and the movie of the same name is shot in an immersive pseudo-documentary style that makes for either incredibly uncomfortable or incredibly enthralling viewing. Horror movie fans that like to test their resilience need look no further than finding out firsthand why The Poughkeepsie Tapes experienced a 10-year delay for disturbing content.

✕ Remove Ads

The Sixth Sense

The Sixth Sense is the film that put M. Night Shyamalan and his infamous twist endings on the map. Bruce Willis plays child psychologist Malcolm Crowe as he attempts to understand why his patient, Cole Sear (Haley Joel Osment), claims that he can see dead people. While the trope of clunky and convoluted M. Night Shyamalan plot twists has become a bit of a running joke these days, his first was genuinely a masterstroke in subversive storytelling.  The ending is far too good to give away for those who haven't seen The Sixth Sense. And while the process might seem tedious at times, people are only let in on just enough information to keep the storyline moving. The payoff alone makes The Sixth Sense one of the best horror movies on Amazon Prime right now.

✕ Remove Ads

Train to Busan

Zombies have invaded rural neighborhoods, cities, underground facilities, and apocalyptic landscapes, but in Train to Busan, the setting is far more contained. When Seok-Woo (Gong Yoo) travels with his estranged daughter to Busan, they discover that they are in the middle of a zombie outbreak. The movie approaches survival horror with a creative spin, in which the passengers on the train desperately attempt to find their way to safety without bringing the zombie horde along with them. US and Korean horror are different, and Train to Busanis a fantastic starting point for people curious about why Korean horror is so widely loved by Western audiences. With a 96% rating on Rotten Tomatoes, Train to Busan has been heralded by many as one of the best zombie movies ever.
