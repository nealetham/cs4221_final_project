Best Christmas board games for hours of family fun

Classic and kooky options that won’t (necessarily) start any arguments

If we were to conjure up our dream wintery staycation, we’d picture a cosy single-story cottage somewhere in Cumbria.

A log fire is burning away, the kettle is perpetually boiled and there's an endless supply of sweet snacks. With our favourite set of matching plaid pyjamas on, the activities would consist simply of Christmas movies and festive board games. Lakeside walks can wait for weekends in the summer.

In the ultimate fantasy scenario, perhaps we’d have Harry Potter and the Philosopher’s Stone playing while we engage in our own, significantly less life-sized and high-stakes, tandem chess match.

Chess, checkers and backgammon may have ruled for many years but, over time, world-building board games have become more favourable than traditional tabletop ones (except for during that limited three-month period following the release of The Queen’s Gambit in 2020).

Rather, as the seminal, spooky TV show Stranger Things demonstrates in season five, Dungeons & Dragons had a vice-like grip on edgy teens throughout the Eighties. In the decades since it’s become clear that the world of fantasy games with a distinct narrative theme is no longer limited to the realm of stereotypically geeky American teenagers.

Wooden game pieces have been cast aside in favour of complex, multi-card extended boards, and families have found new games to play when the entire clan is home for the festive period.

From building ancient mythical settlements, cities and roads in the beloved Catan, to creating the perfect train route across both fictional and non-fictional lands with Ticket to Ride – the more complex, it seems, the better.

While strategy remains a key element of tabletop games, it must be shrouded in a pseudo-literary shell. We’re simply no longer settling for tabletop games that don’t transport us into some kind of fantasy realm.

If not fantasy, then a comically fast-paced game appeals more to our modern sensibilities. Even our mobile games have developed to reflect this. Solitaire has been replaced by the sweet, fairytale land of Candy Crush.

We’ve rounded up a selection of the best board games to play this Christmas with the entire family, whether you’re a traditionalist, fantasy-lover or a fan of fast-paced gameplay. Keep scrolling for our top picks.

Christmas Monopoly

Monopoly

Players:2-6

The all-time favourite game of Monopoly has had a festive glow-up in this Christmas edition – hop around the board with the choice of six special tokens including Rudolph and Santa.

Instead of London landmarks like Bond Street and Mayfair, there are reindeer, festive treats and Christmas shopping, while hotels and houses have been swapped for grottos and workshops. But be careful – jail, taxes and bankruptcy still very much exist in this competitive trading game.

Buy now£27.98,Amazon

Concept

Repos Production Store

Players: 4-12

A fantastic Christmas game mainly because it can accommodate up to 12 players, Concept is essentially a word association game. You and your opponents must use the icons on the board to convey one of 110 different concept cards. It’s all about communicating without words, which could be a welcome relief after a noisy Christmas dinner.

Buy now£25.90,Amazon

The Shakespeare Game

The Shakespeare Game

Players: 2-5

New for 2022, be transported back to Elizabethan England and travel from Shakespeare’s birthplace of Stratford to London to perform his plays at different theatres.

Race against others before they steal your ideas, burn down your theatre or even spread the plague. You will rise to victory when you’ve made the most money at the box office. An immersive game for the whole family to get stuck into.

Buy now£18.02,Amazon

Scrabble

Scrabble

Players: 2-4

Wordsmiths can get very competitive in a game of Scrabble, challenging themselves and other players to spell long unusual words to earn the highest score.

Start with seven tiles each, place them across and down the board to create your words and play until they’re all gone. A great game to interact as a family unit, while encouraging unique vocabulary and spelling.

Buy now£15.99,Amazon

Articulate Christmas

Articulate

Players: 2-8

The classic fast-talking game has had a Christmas makeover. Describe as many festive words as you can in 30 seconds – from seasonal films and songs to foods and traditions – and work your way around the board as you advance.

For every correct word guessed, add a piece to the additional Christmas jigsaw to win. A fun family-friendly game for hours of giggles and merriment.

Buy now£13.97,Amazon

Cluedo

Cluedo

Players:2-6

Where was the murder, which weapon was used and who was the suspect? In this nail-biting board game of whodunnit, use your cards, clues and a process of elimination to solve the mystery of the murder in the mansion. Think you’ve guessed it? Reveal the cards at the end to crack the suspense.

Buy now£24.99,Amazon

Ticket to Ride Nordic Countries

Asmodee

Players: 2-3

A fast-paced strategy game designed for smaller groups, Ticket to Ride takes you on a Nordic adventure through Denmark, Finland, Norway and Sweden. Compete to score points by figuring out the best route through the wondrous Christmassy landscapes.

Buy now£41.50,Amazon

The Best of Christmas Family Board Game

The Best of Christmas Family Board Game

Players: 2+

Think you’re a Christmas expert? Put yourself and the whole family to the test with pictured, themed and pot-luck cards to quiz your festive knowledge and climb your way up the Christmas tree. Be the first team to add the twinkling star to the top to win.

Buy now£13.99,Amazon

Herd Mentality Board Game

Herd

Players: 4-20

Follow the herd in this crowd-pleasing board game. When fired questions, the aim is to think of the response that everyone else will guess. If your answer is the majority, you win cows.

If you’re the odd one out, you’ll land the pink cow until someone else thinks outside the box. Easy to pick up and can take up a whole afternoon post-turkey and all the trimmings.

Buy now£17.98,Amazon

Harry Potter Hogwarts Wizardly Quest Board Game

Cartamundi

Players: 2-4

Harry Potter fans can show off their wizardry knowledge in this Hogwarts adventure. Be a student in class and learn magical skills. You need to collect five tokens from different subjects to pass your exams and win the game. Just remember it’s leviosa not leviosar!

Buy now£9.48,Amazon

Trivial Pursuit

Trivial Pursuit

Players: 2-6

It’s been a stalwart on the board game scene since the 80s for a reason. Step away from the Christmas pudding and gather your players to go head-to-head with trivial knowledge as you move around the retro-style board.

There are 400 cards with more than 2,000 trivia questions, covering topics on entertainment, history, geography, art and literature, science and nature, and sports and leisure. Let the competition begin.

Buy now£18.99,Amazon

Horrible Histories Board Game

Horrible Histories

Players: 2-4

Journey through different eras – from the Saxons and Middle Ages to the Tudors and Georgians – as you travel across the different sections on the board to reach the present day. Answer multiple choice questions, act out charades and steer clear of Rattus and tricky chance cards in this educational game. A welcome break from screens.

Buy now£17,Amazon

Asmodee Dobble

Amazon

Age range:6+

A twist on the classic game of Snap, Dobble features an array of spherical cards which display a range of different symbols. Every card is different, though each will have one matching symbol with any other card. The first to spot the matching symbol wins the set. Action-packed and designed to be a race, you’ll be playing Dobble for hours on end.

Buy now£9,Amazon

Catan

Catan

Players: 5-6 (+ expansion packs)

The infamous world-building game based on the mythical island of Catan, this fantastical strategy game can be learned in under 15 minutes and played in 60. The perfect combination between a fast-paced board game and one that encompasses a fictional narrative, players will build settlements, infrastructure and learn to trade to see whose village comes out on top.
