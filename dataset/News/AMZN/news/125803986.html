What You Missed On Wall Street This Morning
Mid/Close Wrap
Sam Martinelli - email
Five top five lists with the news and calls moving stocks, powered by The Fly.

Get caught up quickly on the top news and calls moving stocks with these five Top Five lists.

1. STOCK NEWS:

  • Micron (MU) reported better-than-expected Q1 results and provided upbeat Q2 guidance [read more]
  • Warner Bros. Discovery (WBD) CEO David Zaslav reportedly met with Paramount (PARA) CEO Bob Bakish on Tuesday in New York City to discuss a possible merger[read more]
  • Carnival (CCL) reported upbeat Q4 earnings and revenue and provided its outlook for Q1 and FY24[read more]
  • Coinbase (COIN) has received a crypto license in France, according to CNBC[read more]
  • MillerKnoll (MLKN) provided a "beat and raise" report for Q2 [read more]

2. WALL STREET CALLS:

  • Morgan Stanley upgraded Salesforce (CRM) to Overweight a day after Wells Fargo downgraded the shares [read more]
  • Wedbush downgraded Chipotle (CMG) and Wendy's (WEN) to Neutral ratings[read more]
  • Macquarie initiated coverage of Genius Sports (GENI) with an Outperform rating [read more]
  • Pivotal Research upgraded Spotify (SPOT) to Buy[read more]
  • Morgan Stanley downgraded Simon Property (SPG) to Equal Weight [read more]

3. AROUND THE WEB:

  • Tesla (TSLA) is facing an inquiry by the Norwegian Public Roads Administration, Reuters reports [read more]
  • Samsung (SSNLF) will invest $280M over five years in a facility it will set up in Japan, Reuters says [read more]
  • Cytokinetics (CYTK) is seeing "strong interest" from Novartis (NVS) and AstraZeneca (AZN) as the company weighs its options after receiving takeover interest, Dealreporter reports [read more]
  • Boeing (BA) plans to resume deliveries of its 787 Dreamliner to China, Reuters says [read more]
  • General Motors (GM) has bought out roughly 50% of its 2,000 Buick dealers across the nation, WSJ reports [read more]

4. MOVERS:

  • Triumph Group (TGI) increases after announcing it will sell its product support business to AAR Corp. (AIR) [read more]
  • Energy Fuels (UUUU) gains after announcing it has commenced uranium production at three of its permitted and developed uranium mines in Arizona and Utah [read more]
  • Joby Aviation (JOBY) rises after it successfully completed a series of air traffic simulations with NASA's Ames Research Center [read more]
  • Caterpillar (CAT) falls after Bernstein put the stock on "negative watch," believing 2024 street estimates are 10% too high [read more]
  • Polished (POL) drops after announcing it was not aware of any material changes in the business to account for the 618% spike in the stock's price in the last regular session [read more]

5. EARNINGS/GUIDANCE:

  • BlackBerry (BB) reported Q3 EPS and revenue that beat consensus, with CEO John Giamatteo commenting "This was a good quarter for BlackBerry" [read more]
  • CarMax (KMX) announced its Q3 results, with EPS beating consensus, but revenue falling short [read more]
  • Apogee Enterprises (APOG) reported Q3 results and raised its FY24 guidance [read more]
  • Paychex (PAYX) released its Q2 results and provided guidance for Q3 and FY24, with CEO John Gibson commenting "We are pleased with our results" [read more]
  • Nike (NKE) will report its Q2 results later today [read more]

INDEXES:

Near midday, the Dow was up 196.56, or 0.53%, to 37,278.56, the Nasdaq was up 129.14, or 0.87%, to 14,907.09, and the S&P 500 was up 32.00, or 0.68%, to 4,730.35.
